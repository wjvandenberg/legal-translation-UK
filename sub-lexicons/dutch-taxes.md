# Dutch Tax Law — Sub-Lexicon

Use alongside `references/taxes.md` (main tax lexicon) and `sub-lexicons/dutch-corporate-ma-jv.md` for the corporate/M&A interface.

This sub-lexicon covers the Netherlands (European kingdom). Caribbean Netherlands (Bonaire, Sint Eustatius, Saba) has a separate BES tax regime not covered here.

Translator's note: translate **Vpb** as "CIT" or "corporate income tax"; do not render as "corporation tax" because the Dutch system differs structurally from UK corporation tax. **Btw** (belasting over de toegevoegde waarde) and **omzetbelasting** mean the same thing — translate as "VAT"; "btw" is the colloquial/invoice term, "omzetbelasting" the statutory term. **Bronbelasting** (since 2021) is distinct from ordinary dividend WHT — translate as "conditional withholding tax" to preserve its anti-abuse character.

Translator's note: **goed koopmansgebruik** is the Dutch equivalent of conservative accrual accounting for tax purposes — translate as "sound business practice" (the term is quasi-technical and appears in case law; never translate literally as "good merchant custom"). **Deelnemingsvrijstelling** is the crown jewel of Dutch holding taxation — translate as "participation exemption"; the Dutch version is broader than many others (no minimum holding period), but requires the participation to pass at least one of the subject-to-tax, asset, or activity tests when applied to investment participations. The **Box 3** regime has been in constitutional turmoil since the Supreme Court's *Kerst-arrest* of 24 December 2021 (HR 24 December 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1963); a transitional act (*Wet rechtsherstel box 3*) applies for years 2017–2022, and a *Wet overbruggingswet box 3* applies from 2023 pending structural reform.

Translator's note: ordinary **dividendbelasting** remains at 15% and is creditable against Vpb or (for non-residents) eligible for treaty relief. Translate the two distinctly — **dividend withholding tax** vs **conditional withholding tax**. Note that the Netherlands abolished the proposed full repeal of dividend WHT in 2018 after political backlash.

## 15. Notes and Cautions

- **Citation style.** Dutch tax statutes are cited as *Art. X Wet Vpb / Wet IB / Wet OB* (or by colloquial abbreviation). In English translation, render as "Art. X of the [full English name] Act" on first mention and then as "Art. X CIT Act" / "Art. X PIT Act" / "Art. X VAT Act" thereafter.
- **Hoge Raad cases.** Cited as *HR [date], ECLI:NL:HR:[year]:[number], BNB [year]/[page]*. Preserve the ECLI identifier (it is the official EU-wide court-decision identifier); BNB is a specialist reporter.
- **Box 3 in flux.** Since the Supreme Court's **HR 24 December 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1963** (the "Kerst-arrest"), the statutory notional-yield regime has been held to violate Art. 1 First Protocol ECHR in certain cases. The **Wet rechtsherstel box 3** (for 2017–2022) and **Wet overbruggingswet box 3** (from 2023) apply transitionally; a structural reform based on actual yield (Wet werkelijk rendement) is under consultation. In translation, flag the applicable year and statute.
- **Fiscale eenheid reform.** The post-**X BV and X NV** (CJEU C-398/16 and C-399/16) landscape means the **Wet spoedreparatie fiscale eenheid** (2019) applies retroactively from 1 January 2018. A replacement regime (the "group regime") has been in consultation but not yet enacted.
- **Pillar Two.** The **Wet minimumbelasting 2024** implements the EU Pillar Two Directive. IIR and QDMTT apply for financial years starting on or after 31 December 2023; UTPR applies for financial years starting on or after 31 December 2024. Use "minimum top-up tax" and, where precision matters, distinguish QDMTT (Dutch domestic top-up), IIR (income inclusion rule) and UTPR (undertaxed profits rule).
- **30%-regeling changes.** The 30%/20%/10% tapered version applies from 1 January 2024 (5-year periods at declining percentages, capped at WNT norm). The **partiële buitenlandse belastingplicht** (partial non-resident status for 30%-ruling holders, allowing Box 2/3 as non-resident) is abolished from 1 January 2025 (transitional for existing rulings). Always flag the year.
- **Bedrijfsopvolgingsregeling (BOR) curtailment.** Reforms effective 1 January 2025 (and further tightening in 2026) narrow the scope of the business succession relief in inheritance/gift tax — reduced from 83% above €1.2m/83% to 70% and a capped structure; check effective date precisely.
- **Overdrachtsbelasting rates.** Standard is 10.4% since 1 January 2023 (was 8% before) for non-owner-occupied and commercial; 2% for owner-occupier; 0% starter exemption up to a price cap; 8% for second homes or buy-to-let. Flag the year and status.
- **Fraus legis vs GAAR.** Unlike in France/Italy, the Dutch GAAR lives in case law (fraus legis) for most direct-tax purposes; ATAD GAAR (Art. 6) is implemented via Art. 10a-type specific rules and Art. 17 Wet Vpb, but fraus legis continues to apply alongside. In translation, preserve "fraus legis" as a term of art if the legal source itself uses it.
- **Verrekenprijsdecreet (TP Decree).** Updated in 2022. References to older decrees (e.g., 2018 version) matter for historic years.
- **BES Islands.** If the document covers the BES Islands (Bonaire, Sint Eustatius, Saba), those have a separate tax system (vastgoedbelasting, opbrengstbelasting, ABB instead of btw) — clarify the territorial scope.
- **False friends.**
  - *Accijns* → excise duty (not "accession").
  - *Aanslag* → assessment (not "attack").
  - *Afdracht* → remittance of withheld tax (not "payment" generically; **afdragen** is to pay *over* to the authority).
  - *Belastingplichtige* → taxable person / taxpayer (not "tax-plighted").
  - *Heffing* → levy (general term); **heffen** = to levy/impose.
  - *Verlegging* → reverse charge (VAT); not "relocation".
  - *Vaststelling* → determination/assessment (context-dependent).
  - *Nageheven belasting* → recovered tax (distinct from *nagevorderde belasting*, additionally-assessed tax).
  - *Belastbare grondslag* → taxable base.
  - *Fiscaal jaar / boekjaar* → tax year / financial year (usually coincide for Dutch CIT, but can differ — translate precisely).
