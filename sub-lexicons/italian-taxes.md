# Italian — Taxes Sub-Lexicon

Italian tax-law terminology mapped to the neutral English equivalents in
`references/taxes.md`. Italian tax law is governed primarily by the TUIR
(Testo Unico delle Imposte sui Redditi, D.P.R. 917/1986), the IVA decree
(D.P.R. 633/1972), the Statuto del Contribuente (L. 212/2000), and the
procedural code on tax assessment (D.P.R. 600/1973). For corporate / M&A
context use alongside `italian-corporate-ma-jv.md`.

---

## 15. Notes — Translator Guidance

- **IRES vs "corporate tax"**. IRES (currently 24%) is Italy's corporate income tax.
  Translate as "corporate income tax (IRES)" on first mention. Don't translate as
  "corporation tax" (UK-only) or "company tax" (informal).
- **IRAP — no English equivalent.** IRAP is a regional tax on net production value
  (broadly value added: wages + interest + profit), not a profit tax. There is no
  direct foreign analogue. Always translate as "regional production tax (IRAP)" with
  a translator's note on first substantive use; never as "regional income tax".
- **IVA = VAT**. Italian IVA is the EU VAT regime. Always translate as "VAT" — not
  "GST" or "sales tax". Italian VAT rates: standard 22%, reduced 10% and 5%, super-
  reduced 4%, plus 0% (zero rate) for exports and intra-Community supplies.
- **Imposta di registro**. The Italian registration tax operates on transactional
  documents (deeds, contracts, court orders) at fixed or proportional rates. It is
  conceptually similar to UK stamp duty but operates on the registration of the
  document, not its execution. Translate as "registration tax" or "registration
  duty"; flag where a UK reader might assume "stamp duty".
- **Ritenuta — never "retention tax"**. *Ritenuta alla fonte* is "withholding tax".
  *Sostituto d'imposta* is "withholding agent". *Ritenuta a titolo d'acconto* =
  withholding on account; *ritenuta a titolo d'imposta* = final / definitive
  withholding. The distinction matters because the latter discharges the recipient's
  liability while the former is creditable against final tax.
- **Esterovestizione**. Anti-abuse doctrine treating a foreign-incorporated entity
  with effective Italian residence as Italian-resident for tax purposes (Art. 73(5-bis)
  TUIR — rebuttable presumption where the foreign entity is controlled by Italian
  residents and holds majority Italian-resident directors). Translate as "foreign
  incorporation with effective Italian residence" or "anti-foreign-incorporation
  rule" and explain — there is no clean English single-word equivalent.
- **Stabile organizzazione**. The Italian rendering of "permanent establishment"
  (PE). The OECD PE concept applies, with Italian-specific case-law on hidden /
  undisclosed PEs (*stabile organizzazione occulta*). Translate as "permanent
  establishment" and use "hidden PE" or "undisclosed PE" for the *occulta* variant.
- **Prescrizione vs decadenza**. In Italian tax procedure, *decadenza* is the
  forfeiture period within which the tax authority must act (typically by 31
  December of the 5th year after the year of filing, or 7th if no return was filed
  — Art. 43 D.P.R. 600/1973). *Prescrizione* is the longer-tail substantive
  limitation on the right to collect. Translate *decadenza* as "forfeiture period"
  and *prescrizione* as "prescription period" or "statute of limitations" — and add
  a translator's note distinguishing the two.
- **Accertamento con adesione, reclamo-mediazione, conciliazione**. These are
  three distinct administrative/early-resolution procedures under Italian tax law:
  *accertamento con adesione* (D.Lgs. 218/1997) is post-PVC settlement; *reclamo-
  mediazione* is the pre-litigation step for disputes up to €50,000; *conciliazione
  giudiziale* is in-court settlement. Translate functionally and explain.
- **Modello 770**. The annual return filed by withholding agents reporting amounts
  withheld. Translate as "Form 770 (annual withholding tax return)" and keep the
  Italian form name on first mention.
- **Cessione d'azienda / di ramo d'azienda**. The transfer of a going concern
  (TOGC) under Italian law is not a VAT-relevant supply (Art. 2(3)(b) D.P.R.
  633/1972), but is subject to registration tax on the underlying asset values.
  This is the inverse of a share deal (no VAT, no registration on the share
  consideration; only fixed registration tax). Always identify which deal structure
  the source contemplates.
- **Tax warranties + tax indemnity / tax covenant.** Italian SPAs typically include
  both: *dichiarazioni e garanzie fiscali* (tax warranties — backwards-looking
  representations on filings, payments, returns) and *indennizzo fiscale / tax
  covenant* (pound-for-pound indemnity for pre-closing tax). Translate keeping the
  English drafting convention; the Italian *manleva fiscale* is best rendered as
  "tax indemnity".
- **Affrancamento**. A substitute-tax election allowing the buyer (or in
  reorganisations, the surviving entity) to step up the tax basis of acquired
  assets to fair value, paying a substitute tax (typically 12-16% depending on the
  asset and regime). Translate as "step-up of tax basis (by substitute-tax
  election)" or "tax re-alignment". This is a recurring planning concept in Italian
  M&A.
- **Bonus edilizi / superbonus / cessione del credito**. The Italian building-
  bonus mechanisms (110% superbonus, façade bonus, ecobonus, sismabonus) are
  delivered as tax credits that can be assigned (cessione del credito) or applied as
  invoice discounts (sconto in fattura). The 2023–2024 reforms tightened the
  assignment regime sharply. Always render as "tax credit" and "assignment of tax
  credit" — and flag the dated regulatory landscape.
- **Pillar Two / global minimum tax**. Italy implemented Pillar Two via D.Lgs.
  209/2023 effective 1 January 2024. Use the OECD English terminology (IIR, UTPR,
  QDMTT, GloBE) without translation; *imposta minima nazionale* = QDMTT.
- **Fatturazione elettronica e Sistema di Interscambio (SdI)**. Italy was the EU
  pioneer of mandatory B2B/B2C electronic invoicing through the Sistema di
  Interscambio (SdI), the central exchange platform run by Agenzia delle Entrate.
  Translate as "electronic invoicing through the Italian e-invoicing exchange
  system (SdI)" — keeping the Italian acronym on first mention.
- **Currency / number formats**. Italian uses comma as decimal separator and dot as
  thousands separator (1.234,56 €). Convert to UK English style (£1,234.56 or
  €1,234.56) unless the user specifies otherwise. Always preserve the currency
  symbol and the exact figure.
