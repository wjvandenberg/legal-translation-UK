## Notes for Translators

- **Aanbestedingswet 2012**: Dutch implementation of EU procurement directives. Major amendments in 2016 (2014 directives) and 2022. Translate as "Public Procurement Act 2012" or "Aw 2012" (explain on first use).
- **Gids Proportionaliteit**: Unique Dutch instrument. Not statute but quasi-binding — contracting authorities must apply or explain deviations. Deals with clustering, financial requirements, reference requirements, award criteria. Flag where tender documents invoke Gids.
- **Clusterverbod (Art. 1.5 Aw 2012)**: Prohibition on disproportionate clustering of contracts that would unduly disadvantage SMEs. Dutch-specific elaboration of SME-access principle. Key issue in Dutch tender challenges. Translate as "prohibition on clustering" with gloss.
- **Nota van inlichtingen**: Distinctive Dutch feature — formal Q&A document issued by contracting authority during tender period, aggregating and answering tenderer questions. Binding part of tender documents. Translate as "clarification note" or "Q&A document" — preserve the formal status.
- **Kort geding / voorzieningenrechter**: Primary procurement remedy route in the Netherlands. Tenderers challenge award decisions via kort geding before the voorzieningenrechter of a district court, typically within the standstill period. Translate as "preliminary relief proceedings" / "judge in preliminary relief".
- **Grossmann-verweer / rechtsverwerking**: Named after ECJ Grossmann case — defence that tenderer forfeited rights to challenge by failing to object promptly during the tender. Dutch courts apply rigorously. Translate as "Grossmann defence" or "rights forfeiture" with gloss.
- **Commissie van Aanbestedingsexperts (CvAE)**: Non-binding advisory body on procurement complaints. Free of charge. Used extensively. Translate as "Committee of Procurement Experts (CvAE)".
- **TenderNed / PIANOo**: TenderNed is the Dutch official e-procurement platform. PIANOo is the government expertise centre (similar to UK CCS). Preserve names.
- **Bibob-toets**: Integrity test under the Wet Bibob — allows contracting authorities to assess tenderers' backgrounds for criminal/integrity risks. Unique Dutch instrument. Translate as "Bibob test" with gloss on first use.
- **EMVI vs BPKV**: EMVI = economisch meest voordelige inschrijving = most economically advantageous tender (MEAT). BPKV = beste prijs-kwaliteitverhouding = best price-quality ratio. Under Aw 2012, BPKV is the default sub-category of EMVI; lowest price requires justification. Translate BPKV as "best price-quality ratio" — distinct from "best value".
- **Standstilltermijn**: 20-day mandatory pause between award decision and contract signing during which tenderers can challenge. Dutch contracting authorities must observe strictly — breach leads to possible contract annulment. Translate as "standstill period"; sometimes "Alcatel period" after ECJ Alcatel case.
- **Aanbestedende dienst vs speciaal-sectorbedrijf**: Distinction between classical-sector contracting authority (government, agency) and utilities-sector contracting entity. Different directives and different thresholds. Translate consistently as "contracting authority" (classical) or "contracting entity" (utilities).
- **Aanbestedingsreglement Werken (ARW 2016)**: Dutch-specific procurement regulation for below-threshold works contracts. Voluntarily applied; not EU-required. Translate as "Public Works Procurement Regulation 2016".
- **Percelen / lots**: Under Aw 2012, contracting authorities must either split contracts into lots or justify non-splitting. Relates to clusterverbod. Translate as "lots" and "split into lots".
- **Social return (SROI)**: Dutch-standard requirement for 2–5% of contract value to fund employment of disadvantaged groups. Translate as "social return" or SROI (retain English acronym). Common in large government contracts.
- **Duidelijk grensoverschrijdend belang**: Even below-threshold contracts trigger EU Treaty principles (non-discrimination, transparency) if there is "clear cross-border interest". Dutch courts apply this standard. Translate as "clear cross-border interest".
- **Foreign Subsidies Regulation (FSR)**: EU 2022/2560 — in force July 2023. Affects procurement above EUR 250M (bids from non-EU subsidised undertakings require notification). Dutch procurement officers increasingly mandate FSR compliance statements. Retain acronym.
- **IPI / Wet internationale aanbestedingsinstrument**: EU Regulation 2022/1031 — allows EU to exclude/restrict non-EU economic operators from third countries without reciprocal access. Being applied. Translate as "International Procurement Instrument".
- **Vifo / FDI screening**: Wet veiligheidstoets investeringen, fusies en overnames — Dutch FDI screening regime since June 2023. Intersects with procurement where tenderers are non-EU. Flag for security-sensitive contracts.
- **Belgian procurement**: Belgian Public Procurement Act 2016 (Wet overheidsopdrachten) transposes same EU directives, but Belgian-specific: different thresholds, Conseil d'État as review body (not kort geding), different clustering rules. Always differentiate Belgian documents.
- **ESPD / UEA**: European Single Procurement Document — self-declaration used across EU procurements. Dutch "Uniform Europees Aanbestedingsdocument" = ESPD.
