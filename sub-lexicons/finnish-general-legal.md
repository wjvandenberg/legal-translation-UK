## Notes

- **Finnish contract law**: Finland does not have a comprehensive civil code. Contract law
  is based on the Contracts Act (oikeustoimilaki, 1929), the Sale of Goods Act (kauppalaki,
  1987), and extensive case law. The Nordic legal tradition applies — more flexible than
  civil law systems but with some codification. Flag this hybrid nature.
- **"Sopimus" vs "sopimus"**: Finnish legal texts often use "Sopimus" (capitalised) to
  refer to the specific agreement being executed. Translate as "the Agreement" when
  capitalised.
- **Nordic cooperation**: Finnish contract law shares significant features with other
  Nordic jurisdictions (Sweden, Denmark, Norway). The Sale of Goods Act is based on the
  Nordic Uniform Sales Act. Terms may align with Swedish equivalents.
- **EU member state**: Finland is an EU member. EU regulations (GDPR, etc.) apply directly.
  EU directives are implemented through national legislation. Always check for EU law
  dimensions in Finnish legal documents.
- **"Oikeustoimilaki" (Contracts Act)**: The foundational contract law statute. Key
  provisions include rules on formation (offer and acceptance), invalidity (sections 28-36
  on unfair terms), and representation. The "reasonableness rule" (kohtuullistaminen,
  section 36) allows courts to adjust unfair contract terms. Flag this broad adjustment
  power.
- **Bracketed placeholder dates (`[1.2020]`, `[15.1.2021]`)**: Finnish legal drafts
  commonly use square brackets around a date (or date-range) to signal "to be confirmed"
  — typically when the commencement of an obligation depends on an event whose date is
  not yet fixed (start of construction, long-stop date, signature date of a related
  deed). The brackets carry real drafting meaning and MUST be preserved in the English
  translation. Render as `[1 May 2020]` (UK) / `[May 1, 2020]` (US) — keep the brackets
  around the date, do not silently resolve them. When the bracketed date is itself the
  subject of a tracked-change edit (bracket ins/del around a date ins/del), keep both
  the bracket wrappers AND the date edit so that a reviewer using Accept/Reject can see
  the placeholder-resolution gesture. The skill's `strip_noop_tracked_changes.py` is
  bracket-aware and will not collapse these brackets on its own,
  but the translator must still render the brackets in the `en` and `en_segments`
  fields.
