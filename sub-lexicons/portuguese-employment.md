## Notes

- **Código do Trabalho (CT)**: Portugal's Labour Code (Lei 7/2009, as amended) — the
  principal employment legislation. Reference as "Labour Code (CT)" on first mention.
- **"Justa causa"**: Dismissal for just cause (Art. 351 CT) does not require notice and
  involves a mandatory disciplinary procedure (nota de culpa + hearing + decision). The
  burden of proof is on the employer. Translate as "just cause" or "dismissal for cause".
- **"Período experimental"**: The trial period under Portuguese law varies by contract type
  and employee seniority: 90 days for most employees, 180 days for complex technical roles,
  240 days for senior management (Art. 112 CT). Either party can terminate without cause
  during this period.
- **"Compensação por cessação"**: Portuguese law provides for statutory severance in certain
  termination scenarios. The formula has changed multiple times — currently calculated based
  on a daily rate per year of service (Art. 366 CT). Flag the applicable formula.
- **"Pacto de não concorrência" (Art. 136 CT)**: Post-contractual non-competes require
  fair compensation and are limited to a maximum of 2 years (3 years for employees with
  special access to information). Without compensation, the clause is void.
- **"Comissão de trabalhadores"**: The workers' committee — an employee representation
  body with constitutional status (Art. 54 of the Portuguese Constitution). Distinct from
  trade union representation. Must be consulted before collective redundancies.
- **ACT vs CCT vs AE**: Portuguese collective agreements come in three forms — CCT (between
  unions and employer associations), ACT (between unions and multiple employers), and AE
  (between unions and a single employer). The scope of application differs significantly.
