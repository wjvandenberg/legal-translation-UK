## Design Stage Terminology (CRITICAL)

| Italian | English | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| progetto preliminare | preliminary design | "preliminary project" |
| progetto definitivo | detailed design | "definitive project" (calque), "preliminary design" (WRONG STAGE) |
| progetto esecutivo | construction design | "executive project" (calque), "detailed design" (reserve for definitivo) |
| disegni esecutivi | construction design / working drawings | "as-built drawings" (WRONG — as-builts are post-construction) |

**WARNING**: Confusing "progetto definitivo" (detailed design) with "preliminary design"
is a material translation error. The three stages form a hierarchy:
Preliminary Design → Detailed Design → Construction Design. Getting these wrong changes the contractual meaning.

**WARNING**: "Disegni esecutivi" (construction design/working drawings) and "disegni as-built"
(as-built drawings) are completely different things. Construction design documents are produced
BEFORE construction; as-built drawings are produced AFTER construction showing what was
actually built.

## Notes

- **FIDIC alignment**: Use FIDIC terminology where the source document follows that structure:
  "Engineer" (not "Director of Works"), "Taking-Over Certificate" (not "Provisional Acceptance
  Certificate"), "Defects Notification Period" (FIDIC 2017).
- **"Employer" vs "Owner"**: FIDIC uses "Employer"; many US contracts use "Owner". Default to
  "Employer" for international/European contracts.
- **"Penale"**: Always translate as "liquidated damages" (not "penalties") to ensure
  enforceability under English law.
- **"Direttore dei Lavori"**: In FIDIC context → "Engineer". In non-FIDIC Italian domestic
  context → "works supervisor". Never use "works director" (literal calque).

## Notes on Italian Regulatory Context

- **GSE vs. GME**: GSE manages renewable support (FIT, net metering, green certificates). GME operates wholesale markets. Distinct entities; do not confuse.

- **ARERA Delibere**: Italian regulatory decisions ("delibere") are numbered chronologically. Example: "ARERA delibera 300/2017/R/eel" = Resolution 300 issued in 2017 on electricity. Translate as "ARERA Resolution" or "ARERA Decision" when citing.

- **"Conto Energia"**: Italy's primary FIT scheme, operational 2005–2013 with legacy incentives continuing. Explain as: "Italy's 'conto energia' feed-in tariff scheme". Do not translate the Italian term; it is a proper name.

- **Capacity vs. Energy**: Italian contracts often confuse "potenza" (MW, capacity) with "energia" (MWh, energy). In translation, clarify the unit and concept to prevent errors.

- **"Ritiro Dedicato"**: Italian utility-purchase mechanism at regulated prices; distinct from PPA. Translate as "simplified sale" or "dedicated offtake" in energy context.

- **Energy Units**: Italian convention uses "MW" for power, "MWh" for energy. UK/US follow same convention. Always specify units clearly in translated English.
