acquisitions, joint ventures, shareholders' agreements and related collateral, and powers
of attorney / proxies. Use alongside `references/corporate-ma-jv.md`. For tax-related
terminology (tax indemnities, tax warranties, tax authorities), see `portuguese-taxes.md`
and `references/taxes.md`.

## 18. Notes

- **"Participações sociais"**: Generic Portuguese term covering both "ações" (shares in
  an SA) and "quotas" (quotas in an Lda). Translate as "equity interests" or "shares"
  depending on the company type.
- **"Sociedade Anónima (SA)" vs "Sociedade por Quotas (Lda)"**: SA is similar to a
  public company; Lda is similar to a private limited company. Preserve the designation
  in parentheses on first mention.
- **Código das Sociedades Comerciais (CSC)**: Portugal's Companies Code — the principal
  corporate-law statute governing mergers, demergers, transformations and corporate
  governance. Reference as "Companies Code (CSC)" on first mention.
- **CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários)**: The Portuguese securities
  market commission — relevant for public-company M&A and takeover bids.
- **"Acordo parassocial"**: Shareholders' agreement governed by Arts. 17–19 CSC. Binds
  only the signatories and cannot be invoked against the company or third parties.
- **"Quotas" vs "ações"**: In an Lda, equity interests are quotas; in an SA they are
  ações. Translate as "quotas" and "shares" respectively, preserving the distinction.
- **"Prestações suplementares"**: Additional capital contributions that may be required
  from quotaholders of an Lda (Arts. 210–213 CSC). Not loan capital — they form part of
  the company's own funds. Translate as "supplementary contributions".
- **"Suprimentos"**: Shareholder loans to the company (Arts. 243–245 CSC). Subject to
  special rules including subordination in insolvency. Translate as "shareholder loans".
- **"Consórcio"**: Under Portuguese law a consortium does not create a separate legal
  entity (Art. 2 DL 231/81). Each participant retains its own legal personality. Flag
  the non-entity status on first mention.
- **"Assembleia universal"**: Specifically Portuguese concept (Art. 54 CSC) — a meeting
  is valid without prior notice if all shareholders are present and none objects.
  Translate as "universal meeting" with brief explanation.
- **Three governance models for SAs**: Portuguese SAs may choose under the CSC between
  (1) the Latin model (board of directors + fiscal council), (2) the Anglo-Saxon model
  (board + audit committee + statutory auditor), and (3) the Germanic model (board +
  supervisory board + statutory auditor). Identify the model before translating
  governance bodies.
- **"Conselho fiscal" vs "fiscal único"**: Larger companies must have a conselho fiscal
  (collective body); smaller companies may appoint a fiscal único (single auditor).
  Translate as "supervisory board" / "fiscal council" and "sole statutory auditor"
  respectively.
- **"Gerente" of an Lda**: Managing director of a Sociedade por Quotas. Has unlimited
  civil liability for certain tax and social-security debts of the company — flag this
  distinction when relevant.
- **"Procuração"**: Generic term for power of attorney. Portuguese law (Arts. 262 ff.
  Civil Code) distinguishes the underlying mandato (mandate) from the procuração itself
  (the document conferring power). Both terms may need translation depending on context.
- **"Substabelecimento"**: Sub-delegation of powers to a substitute attorney-in-fact.
  Distinguish "com reserva" (substitutor retains powers in parallel) from "sem reserva"
  (powers fully transferred) — this affects whether both can act.
- **Form requirements for POAs**: Portuguese law requires the POA to follow the same
  form as the act it authorises (Art. 262(2) Civil Code). A POA to sell real estate
  must be by public deed or authenticated private document. Flag form defects.
- **Apostille vs consular legalisation**: Portuguese POAs to be used abroad require
  Hague Apostille if the destination country is a signatory; otherwise consular
  legalisation. The competent apostille authority is the Procuradoria-Geral da República.
- **"Registo comercial"**: The Portuguese commercial register. Corporate resolutions
  that must be registered include changes to articles, appointments/removals of
  directors and capital increases. Translate as "commercial register".
