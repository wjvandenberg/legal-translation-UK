# Finnish — NDAs and Service Agreements Sub-Lexicon

Maps Finnish confidentiality and services vocabulary to correct English equivalents.
Covers salassapitosopimus / luottamuksellisuussopimus, and palvelusopimus,
toimeksiantosopimus, puitesopimus, alihankintasopimus and konsulttisopimus.

See also `finnish-corporate-ma-jv.md` and `finnish-ip-it-technology.md`.

## Notes

- **Trade Secrets Act (liikesalaisuuslaki, 595/2018)**: Finland's trade secret
  legislation, implementing the EU Trade Secrets Directive (2016/943). Provides civil
  remedies for unlawful acquisition, use, and disclosure. Reference as "Trade Secrets
  Act" on first mention.
- **"Sopimussakko" (contractual penalty)**: Finnish NDAs commonly include contractual
  penalties for breach. Generally enforceable, but courts may reduce excessive
  penalties under the Contracts Act section 36 (kohtuullistaminen). In English
  translation render as "liquidated damages" in commercial context (avoid "penalty"
  as unenforceable under English law); use "service credits" in SLAs.
- **No statutory NDA form or general service agreement statute**: Finland has no
  prescribed NDA format and no comprehensive statute governing service agreements.
  The Sale of Goods Act (kauppalaki) applies to goods; services are governed by the
  Contracts Act, general principles of obligations, and case law. Consumer services
  are subject to specific consumer-protection provisions.
- **"Huolellisuusvelvollisuus" (duty of care)**: Standard of care in Finnish contract
  law — service provider must act with the care of a diligent professional. Similar
  to the Nordic standard of professional diligence.
- **Copyright in deliverables**: Under the Finnish Copyright Act
  (tekijänoikeuslaki), copyright vests in the creator (natural person), not the
  commissioning party. Assignment must be explicitly agreed in the contract. Moral
  rights are non-transferable under Finnish law. Flag when translating IP provisions.
- **VAT**: Finland's standard VAT rate is 25.5% (increased from 24% in September
  2024). Reverse charge mechanism applies for certain cross-border services.
- **"Alihankinta"** (subcontracting): The Act on the Contractor's Obligations and
  Liability When Work is Contracted Out (tilaajavastuulaki) imposes due-diligence
  obligations on the commissioning party regarding the subcontractor's tax and
  social-security compliance. Flag when translating.
