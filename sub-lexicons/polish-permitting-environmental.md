# Polish — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps Polish environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers environmental impact assessment, integrated permits, water,
waste, nature protection, chemicals, contaminated land, climate, and
construction/zoning permits.

## Notes

- **Decyzja o środowiskowych uwarunkowaniach (DŚU)**: Issued by the wójt /
  burmistrz / prezydent miasta, RDOŚ, starost or voivode depending on project
  type. Prerequisite for building permit, water-law permit, or other development
  consent for Annex I/II projects. Appeal lies to SKO, then to WSA/NSA. Very
  common subject of translation — render as "environmental decision" or, in
  parallel with an EU-law discussion, as "development-consent decision for EIA
  purposes".
- **Grupa I vs Grupa II**: Group I ("zawsze znacząco") — projects from the
  Annex I list with mandatory EIA. Group II ("potencjalnie znacząco") — Annex II
  projects subject to screening. Screening regulation (Rozporządzenie Rady
  Ministrów in sprawie przedsięwzięć mogących znacząco oddziaływać na środowisko,
  as amended) lists thresholds and categories. Frequently amended — verify
  current version at translation date.
- **GDOŚ / RDOŚ**: GDOŚ is the central nature-protection and EIA authority;
  RDOŚs (16 regional) handle project-level decisions and Natura 2000
  authorisations. Appeals from RDOŚ decisions go to GDOŚ. Render as "General /
  Regional Directorate for Environmental Protection".
- **Prawo wodne (2017)**: Complete recast in 2017, effective 1 January 2018.
  Created Wody Polskie (Państwowe Gospodarstwo Wodne), consolidating water
  management. RZGW (regional water-management boards), zarządy zlewni (catchment
  boards) and nadzory wodne form the three-tier structure. Water-law permit
  ("pozwolenie wodnoprawne") replaced by broader "zgoda wodnoprawna"
  (water-law consent) with subcategories: permit, assessment, notification,
  licence.
- **Pozwolenie zintegrowane (IED)**: Required for Annex I activities under the
  Industrial Emissions Directive 2010/75/EU. Revised in 2024 by Directive
  2024/1785 (extended to livestock farms, batteries, etc.) — Polish
  transposition pending. Installations must comply with BAT conclusions; derogations
  from BAT-AELs require written reasons and cost-benefit justification.
- **Ustawa o zapobieganiu szkodom w środowisku**: Implements ELD Directive
  2004/35/EC. Strict liability for listed activities (Annex III) and fault-based
  liability for other activities causing damage to protected species/habitats.
  Operator must notify RDOŚ of imminent threat; RDOŚ may issue a remediation
  order. Register of environmental damage maintained by GDOŚ.
- **Natura 2000 and appropriate assessment**: Any project likely to have
  significant effect on a Natura 2000 site must undergo habitat assessment.
  Adverse-effect finding blocks consent unless no alternatives + IROPI +
  compensatory measures. In Polish practice this is integrated with DŚU
  proceedings and handled by RDOŚ. Heavily litigated — see CJEU Białowieża
  jurisprudence (C-441/17).
- **BDO (Baza danych o odpadach)**: Nationwide electronic waste registration
  and tracking system. Mandatory registration for waste generators, collectors,
  transporters and treatment operators. Electronic KPO / KEO since 2020. Major
  compliance burden — flag registration numbers and KPO codes in contracts.
- **ROP (rozszerzona odpowiedzialność producenta)**: Evolving. Polish EPR
  regime for packaging (implementing Packaging & Packaging Waste Directive and
  moving toward 2022/1616 Regulation), WEEE (ZSEiE Act), batteries (Battery
  Regulation 2023/1542). Single-use plastics directive transposition
  (Dyrektywa SUP) implemented in 2023 via amendments to multiple acts.
- **Specustawy (special acts)**: Separate lex specialis regimes for linear
  projects of public importance — road (ustawa o szczególnych zasadach
  przygotowania i realizacji inwestycji w zakresie dróg publicznych),
  transmission (ustawa przesyłowa), railway, nuclear, CPK airport. All
  consolidate environmental, planning and expropriation into a single permit
  (ZRID for roads, decyzja lokalizacyjna for transmission). Common in
  infrastructure translations.
- **Uchwały antysmogowe**: Voivodeship-assembly resolutions under Art. 96
  P.o.ś. restricting solid-fuel heating. Małopolska was first (2017); most
  voivodeships now have one. Transitional phase-outs for older boilers.
  Important in local real-estate diligence.
- **KOBiZE and EU ETS**: KOBiZE operates the national emissions register and
  acts as national administrator for EU ETS allowances (EUA accounts in Union
  Registry). Annual surrender deadline: 30 September (from 2024 onward; was
  30 April until Phase IV revision). CBAM Polish implementation under
  rozporządzenie 2023/956 — reporting phase 2023–2025, charges from 2026.
- **Aarhus Convention**: Poland ratified in 2001. Implemented mainly through
  the EIA Act (information and participation) and the Act on Environmental
  NGOs' standing. Environmental NGOs may participate in EIA proceedings as
  "podmiot na prawach strony" and appeal adverse decisions — a common
  procedural issue in infrastructure litigation.
- **Kodeks karny — przestępstwa przeciwko środowisku**: Articles 181–188 of
  the Criminal Code criminalise serious environmental offences (habitat
  destruction, unlawful waste management, damage to water/air). Reinforced
  by 2022 amendments raising penalties. EU Environmental Crime Directive
  2024/1203 requires further expansion by 2026.
