# Japanese — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps Japanese environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers EIA, air/water/soil pollution control, waste, nature,
chemicals, contaminated land, climate, and construction/city planning.

## Notes

- **Basic Environment Act (環境基本法)**: Framework statute (1993).
  Establishes environmental-protection principles, roles of national and
  local government, and environmental quality standards (環境基準) for air,
  water, noise, soil and dioxins. Under-updated but remains foundational.
- **Environmental Impact Assessment Act (1997, as amended 2011)**: Four-stage
  procedure — 配慮書 → 方法書 → 準備書 → 評価書 (+ 報告書 follow-up). Scale-
  based Class 1 (mandatory) and Class 2 (screening) projects. Covers major
  infrastructure (roads, dams, power plants, urban development, land
  reclamation). Not universally applicable to all projects — some sectors
  have separate regimes.
- **Prefectural permitting competence**: Most permitting (air, water, waste,
  soil) is delegated to prefectural governors or mayors of designated cities.
  Upward-stringency (上乗せ) and broader-scope (横出し) regulation allowed at
  prefectural level. Always identify competent authority — may vary by facility
  scale even within the same city.
- **Pollution Control Manager (公害防止管理者)**: Designated facilities must
  appoint a qualified pollution-control manager under the Act on the
  Improvement of Pollution Prevention Systems in Specified Factories.
  Categorised by pollutant type and scale. Central concept in Japanese
  industrial compliance translations.
- **Waste Management Act and producer responsibility**: Japan's producer-
  responsibility regime is splintered across multiple recycling laws —
  containers and packaging, home appliances, construction materials, food,
  automobiles, small electrical appliances. Each imposes separate duties on
  producers. Industrial waste = producer responsibility (排出事業者責任);
  municipalities handle general waste. Manifest system (JWNET) electronic.
- **Soil Contamination Countermeasures Act (2003, major 2010 amendment)**:
  Triggers — (i) cessation of use of hazardous-substance-using specified
  facility, (ii) alteration of land character on sites ≥3,000 m² where
  contamination is suspected, (iii) ordering by prefectural governor. Two
  area designations — 要措置区域 (action required) and 形質変更時要届出区域
  (notification on alteration). Most Japanese soil contamination is managed
  outside the formal regime via voluntary investigation. Central in M&A
  environmental due diligence.
- **2050 Carbon Neutrality Declaration**: Declared by PM Suga in October
  2020. Legislated via 2021 amendment to Global Warming Countermeasures Act.
  Associated with 2030 target of 46% reduction from 2013 levels.
- **GX (Green Transformation) and GX-ETS**: "Growth-Oriented Carbon Pricing"
  policy announced 2023. GX-ETS launched April 2023 as voluntary emissions
  trading among 750+ participating companies. Mandatory phase begins FY2026;
  auctioning for power sector from FY2033. Carbon surcharge (炭素賦課金) on
  fossil-fuel importers from FY2028. Flag as evolving regime.
- **J-Credit Scheme**: National voluntary offset credit scheme certifying
  emissions reductions and removals. Administered by METI / Ministry of
  Environment / Ministry of Agriculture. Covers energy efficiency,
  renewable energy, forest management. Credits tradable and usable for
  reporting under various schemes.
- **Tokyo Cap-and-Trade**: World's first subnational cap-and-trade scheme
  launched April 2010 by Tokyo Metropolitan Government. Covers large office
  buildings and factories. Saitama Prefecture operates linked programme.
  Independent from national GX-ETS — flag in subnational permitting
  translations.
- **Pollution disputes — 公害紛争処理**: Quasi-judicial dispute-resolution
  system under Pollution-Related Dispute Resolution Act. Environmental
  Disputes Coordination Commission (公調委) handles major matters;
  prefectural Pollution Review Boards handle local matters. Alternative to
  civil litigation for pollution-damage claims. Distinctive Japanese
  feature — no direct English analogue.
- **Building Standards Act / City Planning Act**: 用途地域 (use zones)
  recently increased to 13 categories. Building confirmation (建築確認) is
  pre-construction regulatory check; separate "kaihatsu kyoka" (development
  permit) for land development. 建ぺい率 / 容積率 zoning parameters central
  in real-estate translations.
- **Japan and Aarhus Convention**: Japan has not ratified Aarhus. Public-
  participation and information-access rights derive from domestic statutes
  — Public Notice/Inspection procedures in EIA Act, Administrative Procedure
  Act, Information Disclosure Act. Narrower standing rules for
  environmental litigation than in Aarhus signatory states.
