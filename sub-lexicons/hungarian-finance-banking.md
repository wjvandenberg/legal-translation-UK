## Notes

- **Hungarian Civil Code (Ptk.) — Security interests**: Book 5 of the Ptk. (Property Law)
  governs security interests including zálogjog (pledge), jelzálogjog (mortgage), and
  óvadék (financial collateral). The registration system is maintained by the Hungarian
  Notarial Chamber's online register (hitelbiztosítéki nyilvántartás). Reference the Ptk.
- **"Készfizető kezesség" vs "egyszerű kezesség"**: Hungarian law distinguishes between
  a guarantee as primary obligation (készfizető kezesség — no benefit of excussion) and
  a subsidiary guarantee (egyszerű kezesség — creditor must first pursue the debtor).
  The default is egyszerű kezesség. Flag in translation.
- **BUBOR**: The Budapest Interbank Offered Rate — Hungary's primary benchmark for HUF
  loans. Published by the MNB (Magyar Nemzeti Bank). Flag the applicable benchmark.
- **MNB (Magyar Nemzeti Bank)**: The central bank and prudential supervisor. Reference as
  "Magyar Nemzeti Bank (MNB)" or "National Bank of Hungary (MNB)" on first mention.
- **"Vagyont terhelő zálogjog"**: An enterprise-wide floating charge under Hungarian law.
  Covers all assets of the debtor (present and future). Registered in the online pledge
  register. Translate as "floating charge" or "enterprise-wide lien" with note.
- **Cross-border lending**: Hungary's foreign exchange regulations were largely liberalised
  but specific reporting obligations remain for cross-border transactions. Flag when
  translating cross-border loan documentation.

- **Credit Institutions Act (2013. évi CCXXXVII. törvény)**: Hungary's primary banking
  legislation, implementing CRD IV/CRR. Covers licensing, prudential requirements, and
  governance of credit institutions. Reference as "Credit Institutions Act" on first
  mention.
- **MNB as banking supervisor**: The Magyar Nemzeti Bank (MNB) is the integrated financial
  supervisory authority. For significant credit institutions, the ECB conducts direct
  supervision under the SSM (Single Supervisory Mechanism). Hungary is not in the Banking
  Union but cooperates through close cooperation agreements. Flag the ECB/MNB supervisory
  split.
- **"Készfizető kezesség" vs "egyszerű kezesség"**: The Ptk. distinguishes between készfizető
  kezesség (primary guarantee — creditor can claim directly from guarantor) and egyszerű
  kezesség (secondary guarantee — guarantor can invoke benefit of excussion). Translate as
  "primary guarantee" and "secondary guarantee" respectively, and flag the distinction.
- **"Vagyont terhelő zálogjog" (enterprise charge / floating charge)**: Under the Ptk.,
  this charge covers the debtor's entire movable and intangible assets as a fluctuating
  pool. Registered in the hitelbiztosítéki nyilvántartás (collateral register). Translate
  as "floating charge" for UK-oriented texts and "enterprise charge" more generally.
- **BUBOR**: The Budapest Interbank Offered Rate, the benchmark interest rate for
  Hungarian forint (HUF) lending. Published daily by the MNB for maturities from overnight
  to 12 months. Flag as the local benchmark rate.
- **Deposit insurance**: The Országos Betétbiztosítási Alap (OBA / National Deposit
  Insurance Fund) provides deposit insurance up to EUR 100,000 per depositor per
  institution, in line with the EU Deposit Guarantee Scheme Directive. Flag deposit
  insurance provisions.

## Notes

- **Capital Markets Act (2001. évi CXX. törvény)**: The primary legislation governing
  Hungary's capital markets. Covers public offering, prospectus requirements, market
  abuse, and mandatory bids. Reference as "Capital Markets Act" on first mention.
- **MNB as financial supervisor**: The Magyar Nemzeti Bank (MNB) is the single financial
  supervisory authority, covering banking, insurance, capital markets, and funds. Reference
  as "MNB" and note the integrated supervisory model.
- **EU capital markets regulation**: Hungary implements the EU MAR (Market Abuse Regulation),
  MiFID II, Prospectus Regulation, EMIR, and other EU capital markets legislation directly
  or through national implementing measures. Flag EU regulatory references.
- **Budapest Stock Exchange (BÉT)**: The sole regulated market in Hungary. Operates Prime
  Market, Standard, and Xtend segments. Mandatory bid threshold is 33% of voting rights.
  Flag when translating listing or takeover documents.
- **"Kiszorítás" (squeeze-out)**: Under the Capital Markets Act, a shareholder holding
  90% or more of voting rights can squeeze out minority shareholders. The price must be
  fair. Flag squeeze-out provisions.
- **Investment Fund Act (2014. évi XVI. törvény)**: Governs collective investment schemes,
  implementing the UCITS Directive and AIFMD. Fund managers must be licensed by the MNB.
  Reference as "Investment Fund Act."
