# Spanish — Transport and Insurance Sub-Lexicon

Combines Spanish-language transport and insurance vocabulary. Part 1 covers maritime, road, rail, air and multimodal transport: carriage contracts, bills of lading, charterparties, freight forwarding, carrier liability. Part 2 covers insurance contracts, life and non-life lines, reinsurance, brokers, claims handling and regulatory framework. Marine/transport insurance (cargo, hull, P&I, carrier liability) sits at the intersection.

## Notes

- **"Sobreestadías" (demurrage)**: Compensation payable by the charterer for exceeding
  laytime. Always "demurrage" in English, never "demurrage penalties".
- **"Avería gruesa" (general average)**: The York-Antwerp Rules apply. Translate as
  "general average".
- **"Conocimiento de embarque"**: Always "bill of lading". A "carta de porte marítimo"
  (sea waybill) is NOT a bill of lading — it is non-negotiable.
- **Spanish maritime law**: Governed by the Ley de Navegación Marítima (Ley 14/2014).
  Reference as "Spanish Maritime Navigation Act" on first mention.

## Notes

- **"Franquicia"**: In most insurance contexts, translate as "deductible" or "excess"
  (UK). Context-dependent like French and Italian.
- **"Seguro de caución"**: A surety bond issued by an insurance company (common in Spain,
  less common in Anglo-Saxon markets). Translate as "surety bond insurance".
- **"Tomador del seguro"**: The policyholder / person who takes out the policy. May differ
  from the "asegurado" (insured person) and "beneficiario" (beneficiary). Translate as
  "policyholder".
- **Ley de Contrato de Seguro (LCS)**: The Spanish Insurance Contract Act (Ley 50/1980).
  Reference as "Spanish Insurance Contract Act" on first mention.
- **Lloyd's market terminology**: When the document references the London market, use
  Lloyd's-specific terms.
