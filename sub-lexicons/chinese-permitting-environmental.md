# Chinese — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps Chinese (PRC) environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers EIA, pollutant-discharge permits, water, waste, nature,
chemicals, contaminated land, climate, and construction/planning. Most
references are Simplified Chinese (mainland PRC); Traditional-character
variants noted where relevant.

## Notes

- **Environmental Protection Law (2015 revision)**: The 2015 revision is
  known as China's "most stringent environmental law" — introduced daily
  continuous penalties (按日连续处罚), public-interest litigation standing,
  and broader enforcement tools. Remains the central framework statute.
  Further amendments under discussion (2024–2025) but not yet enacted.
- **Pollutant Discharge Permit (排污许可)**: Since 2020, all pollutant-
  discharging units must obtain a pollutant-discharge permit or complete
  registration. Three categories — key permit (重点管理), simplified permit
  (简化管理), registration (登记管理) — by industry and scale. Pollutant
  Discharge Permit Regulation (State Council 2021) is the key implementing
  instrument. Central concept in current Chinese environmental compliance
  translations.
- **"Three Simultaneities" (三同时)**: Longstanding requirement that
  pollution-prevention facilities be designed, constructed and operated
  simultaneously with the main project. Verified at 竣工环境保护验收
  (environmental-protection acceptance upon completion). Failure to comply
  blocks project operation and triggers administrative penalties.
- **Soil Pollution Prevention and Control Law (2019)**: First dedicated soil
  statute. Distinguishes agricultural land and construction-use land regimes.
  Requires soil surveys before land transfer; polluter-pays with fallback
  landowner liability; establishes remediation obligations and soil-fund.
  Critical for M&A environmental due diligence in China.
- **Carbon Peaking and Carbon Neutrality ("双碳")**: President Xi announced
  in 2020 — peak emissions before 2030 and carbon neutrality by 2060.
  "1+N" policy framework — "1" is the 2021 Opinion on Fully and Accurately
  Implementing the New Development Philosophy and the 2021 Action Plan for
  Peaking Carbon Emissions before 2030; "N" refers to sectoral and regional
  implementation plans. National Carbon Market launched July 2021 — power
  sector only initially, expanding to aluminium, cement, steel from 2024–2025.
- **CCER relaunch (2024)**: Chinese Certified Emission Reductions (CCER)
  voluntary scheme relaunched January 2024 after 6-year pause. Four approved
  methodology areas — afforestation, grid-connected solar, offshore wind,
  mangrove-carbon. New methodologies under development. Used for offsetting
  up to 5% of CEA obligations.
- **Central Environmental Inspection (中央生态环境保护督察)**: Centralised
  top-down inspection regime (launched 2015, institutionalised 2019).
  Inspection teams dispatched by Central Committee to provinces, ministries
  and state-owned enterprises. Results in binding rectification requirements
  and personal accountability. Major enforcement mechanism beyond ordinary
  administrative inspection.
- **Environmental public-interest litigation**: Two tracks — civil
  public-interest (standing: qualified environmental NGOs) and procuratorial
  public-interest (standing: procuratorates). Has produced significant body
  of case law since 2015. Damages may include ecological restoration costs
  and punitive damages under Civil Code art. 1232. Important in environmental
  litigation translations.
- **Ecological conservation red line (生态红线)**: Fundamental zoning
  concept — areas of critical ecological function subject to strict
  protection. Finalised nationwide in 2022. Prohibits most development.
  Critical in site-selection translations.
- **Environmental impact assessment categories**: Three tiers based on
  environmental impact — 报告书 (full report for high-impact projects) >
  报告表 (form for medium) > 登记表 (registration for low). Specified in
  the EIA Catalogue (建设项目环境影响评价分类管理名录, most recently 2021,
  amended 2023). Verify current version.
- **Environmental Protection Tax**: Since 2018, replaces the former pollutant
  discharge fee. Administered by tax authorities with MEE support. Four
  taxable categories — air, water, solid waste, noise. Verification through
  continuous monitoring systems or emission factors.
- **Provincial variation**: Chinese environmental law is largely nationally
  uniform, but enforcement and complementary provincial regulations vary
  significantly. Beijing, Shanghai, Guangdong and Jiangsu typically lead on
  stringency. Pilot carbon markets in Shenzhen, Shanghai, Beijing, Guangdong,
  Tianjin, Chongqing, Hubei continue alongside national market.
