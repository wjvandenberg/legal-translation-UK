# Spanish — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps Spanish environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers EIA, integrated permits, water, waste, nature, chemicals,
contaminated land, climate, and construction/urbanism.

## Notes

- **Ley 21/2013 (EIA framework)**: Unified state-level EIA statute transposing
  Directive 2011/92/EU as amended by 2014/52/EU. Applies to projects subject to
  state authorisation; autonomous communities have parallel regimes for
  regional projects. Verify whether a given project is sujeto a ordinary or
  simplified procedure — tight deadlines and significant translation
  consequences.
- **Ley 7/2022 (Waste Act)**: Replaces Ley 22/2011. Fully implements Single-Use
  Plastics Directive, establishes new EPR schemes (textiles, furniture,
  cigarette butts, tyres, agricultural plastics, fishing gear) phased in
  2024–2025. Introduces plastic-packaging tax. Critical for retail, FMCG and
  waste-management translations.
- **Ley 7/2021 (Climate Change Act)**: Sets Spain's climate-neutrality
  trajectory — 23% emissions reduction by 2030 vs 1990, climate-neutral by
  2050, 42% renewables in final energy consumption by 2030. Creates duty on
  large companies to publish decarbonisation plans. Mandates carbon footprint
  and reduction plans for certain enterprises.
- **CCAA competence**: Environmental competence is heavily devolved. Each of
  17 autonomous communities has its own environmental authority, legislation
  (on top of state framework laws), and integrated-permit regime. Translations
  must identify applicable CCAA regime. Key CCAA statutes include Catalonia's
  "Ley de prevención y control ambiental", Andalusia's "Ley GICA",
  Madrid's "Ley de evaluación ambiental".
- **AAI — IPPC competence**: AAI is issued by the competent CCAA authority,
  not the State. Covers Annex I activities under Ley 16/2002 (transposing IED
  2010/75/EU). Renewal every 4–8 years. Must align with BAT conclusions.
  Practice point: AAI modifications distinguish "sustancial" (requiring new
  EIA) from "no sustancial".
- **Ley 26/2007 (Environmental Liability)**: Implements ELD Directive
  2004/35/EC. Strict liability for Annex III activities with mandatory
  financial security (since 2022, phased implementation). "Garantía
  financiera obligatoria" (mandatory financial security) thresholds specified
  in Order ARM/1783/2011 (Orden de umbrales MORA). Common in M&A warranty
  discussions.
- **Confederaciones Hidrográficas**: Nine inter-autonomic river basin
  authorities (Miño-Sil, Cantábrico, Duero, Tajo, Guadiana, Guadalquivir,
  Segura, Júcar, Ebro) plus intra-autonomic basins managed by CCAA. Competent
  for water concessions, discharge authorisations, and river basin plans.
- **Red Natura 2000 in Spain**: Spain has the largest Natura 2000 network in
  the EU (~27% of territory). Competent management is devolved to CCAA.
  Spanish case law (TS / TJUE) on appropriate assessment is abundant,
  especially around wind farms and coastal developments.
- **Delitos ambientales (Criminal Code)**: Articles 325–331 Penal Code
  criminalise serious environmental offences. Article 328 specifically covers
  illegal waste management. Legal-persons criminal liability applies (art. 31
  bis). EU Environmental Crime Directive 2024/1203 requires expansion by 2026.
- **Ley 27/2006 (Aarhus implementation)**: Implements Aarhus through (i)
  access to environmental information, (ii) public participation, (iii) access
  to justice. Recognised environmental NGOs (registered under art. 23) have
  standing in environmental judicial review and may benefit from free legal
  aid. Heavily relied on by NGOs before contentious-administrative courts.

  urbanism; `spanish-energy-infrastructure.md` for renewables permitting and
  EU ETS; `spanish-corporate-ma-jv.md` for environmental reps and warranties;
  `spanish-litigation-settlement.md` for administrative-court procedure.
