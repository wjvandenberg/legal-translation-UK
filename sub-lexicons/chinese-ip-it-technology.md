## Notes

- **PRC Patent Law (专利法)**: Revised in 2020 (4th amendment, effective June 2021).
  Key changes include patent term extensions for pharmaceutical patents, introduction of
  punitive damages, and open licensing. Reference as "Patent Law" on first mention.
- **PRC Trademark Law (商标法)**: Revised in 2019. Added provisions against bad-faith
  filings and increased damages. Reference as "Trademark Law" on first mention.
- **PRC Copyright Law (著作权法)**: Revised in 2020 (effective June 2021). Added
  protections for audiovisual works and increased statutory damages. Reference as
  "Copyright Law" on first mention.
- **"实用新型" (utility model)**: A PRC-specific IP right — a "petty patent" for less
  inventive technical solutions. Examination is less rigorous than for invention patents.
  No direct equivalent in US/UK law. Translate as "utility model" or "utility model patent".
- **CNIPA (国家知识产权局)**: The China National Intellectual Property Administration —
  handles patent and trade mark examination and registration. Reference as "China National
  Intellectual Property Administration (CNIPA)" on first mention.
- **"惩罚性赔偿"**: The 2020 Patent Law and 2019 Trademark Law introduced punitive damages
  (up to 5x actual damages) for intentional IP infringement. This is a significant
  enforcement development. Flag when translating IP enforcement provisions.
- **Technology export controls**: Technology transfer by PRC entities may be subject to
  export control under the PRC Export Control Law (2020) and the Catalogue of Technologies
  Prohibited or Restricted from Export. Always flag cross-border technology licensing.

## Notes

- **PIPL (个人信息保护法)**: China's Personal Information Protection Law — the PRC's
  principal data protection statute, effective November 1, 2021. Functionally similar to
  the GDPR but with significant differences (e.g., government access exemptions, data
  localisation requirements). Reference as "Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)"
  on first mention.
- **DSL (数据安全法)**: The Data Security Law — governs data classification, "important
  data" handling, and data export security reviews. Reference as "Data Security Law (DSL)"
  on first mention.
- **CSL (网络安全法)**: The Cybersecurity Law — imposes the multi-level protection scheme
  (等保 / MLPS) and network security obligations. Reference as "Cybersecurity Law (CSL)"
  on first mention.
- **"等保" (Multi-Level Protection Scheme)**: Mandatory cybersecurity classification
  system for information systems in China. Systems are classified into 5 levels; Level 3+
  requires formal assessment. Flag when translating IT/cloud agreements with PRC parties.
- **Data localisation**: Certain categories of data (e.g., critical information
  infrastructure operators' personal information and "important data") must be stored
  within China. Cross-border transfer requires security assessment, standard contracts,
  or certification. Flag in all cloud/SaaS agreements.
- **CAC (国家互联网信息办公室 / 网信办)**: The Cyberspace Administration of China — the
  primary data protection and internet content regulator. Reference as "Cyberspace
  Administration of China (CAC)" on first mention.
