## Notes

- **"Completion" vs "Closing"**: UK practice uses "completion"; US practice uses "closing".
  Default to UK unless the document context or governing law indicates US style.

- **"Arras confirmatorias" vs "arras penitenciales"** (Art. 1454 CC): Confirmatory arras
  confirm the contract and serve as pre-agreed damages on breach. Penal/penitential arras
  allow withdrawal from the contract for the price of forfeiting the deposit (or returning
  double, if it is the seller withdrawing). Translate using English explanatory wording
  (e.g., "confirmatory deposit", "withdrawal deposit") with a translator's note. Do NOT
  default to "earnest money" (US-only).

- **"Préstamo participativo"**: A Spanish subordinated loan with interest linked to
  company performance, treated as quasi-equity under Spanish insolvency law. Translate
  as "participating loan" or "subordinated loan" with a context note.

- **"Participaciones sociales" vs "acciones"**: "Participaciones" are ownership interests
  in an SL (limited liability company); "acciones" are shares in an SA (public limited
  company). Both translate as "shares" in English, but preserve the distinction if
  relevant.

- **"Empresa conjunta"**: The standard Spanish term for joint venture. Also commonly
  referred to as "joint venture" (borrowed English) in Spanish legal practice.

- **"Aportación de industria"**: A contribution of services or know-how (permitted in some
  company forms under Spanish law). Translate as "contribution of services" or "sweat
  equity" depending on context.

- **"Materias reservadas"**: Decisions requiring unanimity or special majority. Translate
  as "reserved matters" (UK standard).

- **"Junta general universal"** (Art. 178 LSC): A specifically Spanish concept where a
  general meeting is valid without prior notice if all shareholders are present and
  unanimously agree to hold the meeting. No English equivalent — translate as "universal
  meeting" with a context note.

- **"Administradores mancomunados" vs "administradores solidarios"**: Mancomunados must
  act jointly (typically two required to bind the company). Solidarios can each act
  individually. Translate as "joint directors" and "several directors" respectively.

- **"Administrador único"**: A sole director who exercises all management powers.
  Translate as "sole director".

- **"Consejero delegado"**: The managing director / CEO of an SA. Translate as "chief
  executive officer" or "managing director" depending on context.

- **SA vs SL**: An SA has a board of directors or an administrador único. An SL can have
  any of: sole director, joint directors, several directors, or a board. Preserve the
  company form in translation.

- **"Poder" — POA vs proxy**: The same Spanish word covers both meanings. Use "power of
  attorney" for general representation (signing, transactions, litigation) and "proxy"
  where the operative scope is attendance and voting at a meeting.

- **"Escritura pública" vs "póliza intervenida"**: Spanish notarial practice distinguishes
  between a notarial deed (escritura pública) and a notary-intervened policy (típicamente
  bancario). Both are notarised, but the form differs. Translate "póliza intervenida" as
  "notary-intervened policy" or "notary-witnessed policy".

- **"Apoderado"**: Translate as "attorney-in-fact" or "authorised representative". Do not
  translate as "attorney" alone (which in English suggests a lawyer).

- **"Apostilla" vs "legalización"**: Use "apostille" only for Hague Convention countries;
  use "consular legalisation" for non-Hague countries.
