## Notes for Translators

- **Consument — BW 7:5 definition**: Natural person acting for purposes outside trade/profession. Excludes legal persons and mixed-purpose transactions. Unique BW definition must be applied literally — never translate "consument" as "client" or "customer". Always "consumer".
- **Herroepingsrecht (14-day cooling-off)**: EU-harmonised consumer right to withdraw from distance/off-premises contracts within 14 days. Dutch implementation BW 6:230o-v. If trader fails to inform of right, extended to 12 months + 14 days. Translate as "right of withdrawal" and preserve 14-day reference.
- **Bedenktijd**: Broader term for "reflection period". Specific instances: 14-day herroepingsrecht, 3-day residential property (BW 7:2), timeshare, package travel. Translate as "reflection period" or "cooling-off period" depending on context.
- **Conformiteit (BW 7:17)**: Statutory requirement that goods sold match expectations. Durability is part of conformity — no fixed period but presumption of conformity violated if defect arises within 1 year (consumer sales post-2022). Translate as "conformity" — flag 1-year presumption if relevant.
- **Klachttermijn (BW 7:23)**: Consumer must notify non-conformity "within a reasonable time" — 2 months from discovery is safe. Shorter periods ineffective against consumers. Professional parties have stricter requirements. Translate as "complaint period" and note special rules.
- **Hierarchy of remedies (BW 7:21-22)**: Consumer first entitled to repair or replacement (seller's choice); if impossible/disproportionate/not done promptly, price reduction or rescission. Rescission unavailable for minor non-conformity. Translate carefully; hierarchy is mandatory.
- **Wettelijke garantie vs commerciële garantie**: Crucial distinction. Wettelijke = statutory conformity obligation (BW 7:17), no fixed term but durability-based. Commerciële = voluntary additional trader guarantee. Traders often confuse consumers; always clarify in translation.
- **Zwarte lijst / grijze lijst (BW 6:236/6:237)**: Dutch unique pre-emptive lists of unreasonable standard terms in consumer contracts. Zwarte (black) list terms are always unreasonable; grijze (grey) list terms presumed unreasonable unless trader proves reasonableness. Translate as "black list" / "grey list" with gloss.
- **Reflexwerking**: Extension of consumer protection (particularly algemene voorwaarden rules) to SMEs and smaller professional parties in comparable position to consumers. Judicial doctrine. Translate as "reflex effect" / "reflex application" with gloss.
- **Kernbedingen**: Core terms of a contract (price, main subject matter) — not subject to general unfairness review under BW 6:231, but subject to transparency requirement. Analogous to EU UCTD Art. 4(2). Translate as "core terms".
- **Wet van Dam (2010/2011)**: Restricts tacit renewal of consumer contracts. After initial term (max 1 year with valid tacit renewal), consumer can cancel monthly. Specific consumer/retail applications: magazines, gym memberships, phone contracts. Translate as "Van Dam Act" with gloss.
- **"Bestellen met betalingsverplichting"**: Mandatory wording on order button for consumer online purchases (BW 6:230v(3)). No alternative language acceptable. If missing, consumer not bound. Translate as "order with obligation to pay" — preserve specificity.
- **Omnibus-richtlijn / prijsreductie**: EU Directive 2019/2161 — since 2022, advertised price reductions must reference the lowest price in the 30 days preceding. Anti-fake-sale rule. Dutch implementation in BW 6:193g. Key for retail/e-commerce translation.
- **ACM (Autoriteit Consument en Markt)**: Combined Dutch competition and consumer protection authority. Primary enforcer of consumer rules, unfair terms, unfair practices. Translate as "Authority for Consumers and Markets"; preserve ACM acronym.
- **Reclame Code / RCC**: Dutch self-regulatory advertising framework. Rulings not binding in strict legal sense but widely followed and affect brand. Translate as "Dutch Advertising Code" / "Advertising Code Committee".
- **Consumentenbond**: Influential Dutch consumer NGO. Publishes comparative tests, lobbies, supports individual complaints. Translate as "Consumers' Association" or "Consumentenbond" with gloss; well-known brand.
- **Geschillencommissie**: Sectoral consumer ADR covering 50+ sectors (travel, telecom, energy, electronics, etc.). Decisions are binding via bindend advies (BW 7:900+). Low cost, relatively fast. Flag where contract references specific Geschillencommissie.
- **Consumentenkrediet**: Covered by BW 7:57+ (Wet consumentenkrediet repealed 2006 and integrated into BW). Maximum cost cap of 14% APR for most consumer credit (2024 level). BKR registration required. Flag for credit product translations.
- **Product liability (BW 6:185+)**: Dutch implementation of EU Product Liability Directive 85/374/EEC. Strict producer liability for defective products. New PLD (EU 2024/2853) applying from December 2026 — expanded scope to software, AI systems, etc. Flag for 2024+ product claims.
- **GPSR (General Product Safety Regulation)**: EU 2023/988 applicable since 13 December 2024 — replaces GPSD. Extended scope, online marketplace obligations, recall rules, new mandatory information. Critical for e-commerce. Translate as "General Product Safety Regulation".
- **NVWA**: Dutch Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority — enforces product safety, food labelling, animal welfare. Translate as "Dutch Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority" or retain acronym.
- **Franchise law (BW Book 7 Title 16)**: Wet franchise since 2021 — codified franchise-specific duties. Pre-contractual information duty (standstill 4 weeks), consent rights on changes, mandatory good-faith provisions. Translate as "Franchise Act"; flag 2021 effective date.
- **Digital content (BW 7:50aa+)**: Implements Digital Content Directive (EU) 2019/770. Conformity, updates, remedies for digital content/services. Applies even if paid only with personal data. Translate as "digital content" / "digital service"; distinguish from conventional goods.
- **Greenwashing / green claims**: High-enforcement area. ACM issued Leidraad Duurzaamheidsclaims (2021, updated). Empowering Consumers Directive transposition deadline 2026. Translate claims carefully; preserve qualifiers ("CO2-neutraal door compensatie" ≠ "CO2-neutraal" unqualified).
- **Belgian consumer law**: Belgian Code of Economic Law Book VI — similar EU-directive implementation but different numbering. Belgian consumer authorities are FPS Economy and Economic Inspectorate. Flag Belgian documents.
- **DSA / DMA**: EU Digital Services Act and Digital Markets Act directly applicable. Affects online platforms' consumer-facing operations. Translate as "DSA" / "DMA" — preserve abbreviations.
- **Dutch information duties**: Very extensive under BW 6:230m for distance and off-premises contracts. Checklist includes identity, contact, price, payment, delivery, conformity, complaint handling, right of withdrawal, etc. Translate fully in regulatory/compliance translations; flag omissions.
