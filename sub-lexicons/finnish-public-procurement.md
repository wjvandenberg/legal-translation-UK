## Notes

- **Act on Public Procurement and Concession Contracts (hankintalaki, 1397/2016)**: Finland's
  primary procurement legislation, implementing the EU Public Procurement Directives
  (2014/24/EU and 2014/23/EU). Reference as "Public Procurement Act" on first mention.
- **HILMA**: The Finnish electronic procurement portal (hankintailmoitukset.fi). All
  above-threshold procurements must be published on HILMA and TED (EU portal). Below-
  threshold procurements are published on HILMA only. Reference as "HILMA" on first mention.
- **Market Court (markkinaoikeus)**: The specialised court for procurement appeals. The
  appeal must be filed within 14 days of the award decision. The Market Court can annul
  the decision, impose compensation, or order a penalty. Flag appeal deadlines.
- **"Hankintaoikaisu" (self-correction)**: The contracting authority can correct its own
  procurement decision within 90 days. This is a non-judicial remedy that does not affect
  the right to appeal to the Market Court. Translate as "self-correction" and note.
- **EU procurement thresholds**: Finland follows EU procurement thresholds. Above-threshold
  procurements must follow EU procedures. Below-threshold procurements follow simplified
  national procedures. Flag the applicable threshold level.
- **"Puitejärjestely" (framework agreement)**: Widely used in Finnish public procurement.
  Can be concluded with one or several suppliers. Mini-competitions within framework
  agreements follow specific rules. Translate as "framework agreement".
