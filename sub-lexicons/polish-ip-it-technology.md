# Polish — IP, Technology and IT Sub-Lexicon

Combines Polish-language IP/technology and IT/SaaS vocabulary. Part 1 covers copyright, industrial property (patents, designs, trade marks), trade secrets and technology transfer. Part 2 covers software licensing, SaaS/cloud services, implementation projects, SLAs, data processing and cybersecurity.

## Notes

- **Polish Copyright Act (ustawa o prawie autorskim i prawach pokrewnych)**: Act of 4
  February 1994. Key provisions: arts. 1–4 (protected works), arts. 8–15 (authorship and
  joint works), arts. 12, 74 (employee works and software), arts. 16, 78 (moral rights),
  arts. 41–68 (transfers and licences), arts. 74–77⁵ (computer programs). Moral rights
  are non-transferable — the standard drafting solution is an undertaking not to exercise
  them (zobowiązanie do niewykonywania autorskich praw osobistych).
- **Pola eksploatacji — art. 41 § 2 and art. 50**: A transfer or licence of economic
  rights must expressly identify the fields of exploitation covered. Fields not expressly
  listed are excluded. Art. 50 lists illustrative examples (reproduction, distribution,
  public display, rental, lending, broadcasting, making available online, etc.). This is
  a mandatory drafting requirement — omissions render the transfer/licence ineffective
  for the omitted fields. Highlight consistently.
- **"Utwór pracowniczy" — art. 12**: Employer acquires economic rights to works created
  by employees within their employment duties, upon acceptance of the work and within the
  purpose of the employment contract. Special rule for computer programs (art. 74 § 3) —
  economic rights vest in the employer by operation of law.
- **Prawo własności przemysłowej (p.w.p.)**: Act of 30 June 2000. Governs patents, utility
  models, industrial designs, trade marks, geographical indications, and topographies of
  integrated circuits. The Polish Patent Office (Urząd Patentowy RP) administers national
  industrial property rights and handles oppositions and invalidations.
- **EU patent regime — UP/UPC**: The Unitary Patent and Unified Patent Court system
  entered into force on 1 June 2023. Poland has not yet ratified — so Polish unitary-patent
  effects do not extend to Poland. Translate references to JPE/UPC with a note on the
  Polish position.
- **EUIPO trade marks and Community designs**: Polish practice uses both national
  (UPRP-registered) and EU-wide (EUIPO-registered) trade marks and Community designs.
  Translate acronyms: EUTM (EU Trade Mark), RCD (Registered Community Design).
- **U.z.n.k. (Act on Combating Unfair Competition of 16 April 1993)**: Governs trade
  secret protection (art. 11), misleading advertising (art. 16), passing off (art. 5–7),
  and other unfair-competition acts. Important remedies include cessation, removal of
  effects, restitution of unjustly obtained benefits (art. 18) — beyond damages.
- **Trade secret definition**: Per art. 11 ust. 2 u.z.n.k. (as amended in 2018 to
  implement EU Directive 2016/943) — (i) technical, technological, organisational or
  other information of economic value, (ii) not generally known or readily accessible,
  (iii) subject to reasonable confidentiality measures by the holder.
- **Collective management (OZZ)**: Under the Act on Collective Management of Copyright
  and Related Rights of 15 June 2018 (implementing Directive 2014/26/EU). Major CMOs
  include ZAiKS (literary and musical works), STOART, SAWP, ZPAV, Stowarzyszenie
  Filmowców Polskich. Mandatory collective management applies in certain areas (e.g.,
  cable retransmission).
- **IP Box (tax)**: 5% preferential CIT/PIT rate on qualifying IP income, subject to
  nexus approach. Requires extensive documentation of qualifying R&D activities. See
  taxes sub-lexicon.
- **NIS2 / KSC**: NIS2 Directive (2022/2555) must be implemented into Polish law via
  amendments to the Act on the National Cybersecurity System. Watch for evolving
  implementation status.
- **AI Act**: EU Regulation 2024/1689 applies directly across the EU including Poland.
  Phased entry into force; Polish supervisory authority structure is being established.
  Translate "akt w sprawie AI" consistently as "AI Act" (official name: Regulation laying
  down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence).

## Notes

- **Software under Polish Copyright Act — arts. 74–77⁵**: Programs are protected as
  literary works. Art. 74 § 3: economic rights to programs created within employment
  duties vest in the employer automatically (lex specialis overriding the "upon acceptance"
  rule for other works). Art. 75 allows limited decompilation for interoperability.
  Reverse engineering outside art. 75 is unlawful.
- **"Pola eksploatacji" in software licensing**: Polish Copyright Act requires precise
  identification of fields of exploitation in licences too. For SaaS and cloud services,
  licensing clauses should enumerate: reproduction, adaptation, distribution, public
  communication, making available online (the last is key for SaaS).
- **RODO (GDPR)**: Article 28 GDPR governs processor-controller DPAs. Polish UODO (Act of
  10 May 2018) supplements. Must be concluded in written (including electronic) form. The
  PUODO (President of the Personal Data Protection Office) is the supervisory authority.
  Polish DPIA methodology follows the EDPB guidelines.
- **Cloud adoption by regulated sectors**: Banks, insurers and investment firms using
  cloud services must comply with KNF's Cloud Communication (Komunikat dot. przetwarzania
  przez podmioty nadzorowane informacji w chmurze obliczeniowej, 2020). Outsourcing
  categories (standard vs. estoppel special) determine notification requirements.
- **DORA Regulation**: EU Regulation 2022/2554 on digital operational resilience for the
  financial sector applies from 17 January 2025 across all EU member states including
  Poland. Imposes ICT risk management, third-party provider oversight, incident
  reporting, and resilience testing obligations on financial entities and their critical
  ICT providers.
- **NIS2 implementation**: Directive 2022/2555 is being implemented via an amended Act
  on the National Cybersecurity System. Expanded scope covers more sectors (including
  medium-sized entities in critical sectors), stricter incident reporting, and
  supply-chain security obligations. Verify Polish implementation status.
- **Source code escrow**: Polish practice follows standard patterns (tripartite agreement
  licensor-licensee-depositary, trigger conditions tied to supplier's insolvency or
  service failure). Polish notaries often serve as escrow agents; specialised providers
  (e.g., Iron Mountain) also operate.
- **Kara umowna vs service credits**: Both are available under Polish law. Kara umowna
  (contractual penalty — k.c. art. 483) is payable on breach without proof of damage;
  service credits are typically structured as price reductions for SLA non-compliance.
  Use "service credits" for SLA discounting; "liquidated damages" for penalty clauses.
  See `polish-ndas-service-agreements.md`.
- **"Umowa wdrożeniowa" structure**: Polish implementation agreements usually combine
  elements of umowa o dzieło (for deliverables) and umowa zlecenia (for ongoing services).
  Mixed contracts trigger both regimes for their respective elements — affects acceptance,
  warranty and termination. Flag in complex multi-phase deals.
- **Procurement of IT in the public sector**: Governed by Prawo zamówień publicznych
  (Public Procurement Law). Specific provisions on IT contracts — particularly dealing
  with IP transfer, source code escrow, and post-contract exit — see
  `polish-public-procurement.md`.
- **Open source compliance**: Polish courts respect copyleft obligations. Commercial use
  of GPL-licensed components without compliance creates copyright infringement exposure
  under the Copyright Act. Flag in M&A technical due diligence.
