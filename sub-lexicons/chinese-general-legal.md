## Notes

- **Measure words and legal numbering**: Chinese legal texts use specific numbering
  conventions: 条 (article), 款 (paragraph), 项 (item), 目 (sub-item). Preserve the
  hierarchical structure in translation.
- **"合同" vs "协议"**: Both mean "agreement" but 合同 is more formal (typically used
  for binding contracts), while 协议 is broader and can include non-binding arrangements.
  Default to "contract" for 合同 and "agreement" for 协议 unless context dictates otherwise.
- **Simplified vs Traditional Chinese**: This lexicon uses Simplified Chinese (PRC standard).
  Hong Kong and Taiwan use Traditional Chinese with some different legal terminology. Flag
  when the source may be from a Traditional Chinese jurisdiction.
- **PRC Civil Code (2021)**: The PRC Civil Code, effective January 1, 2021, consolidated
  and replaced several individual statutes including the Contract Law, Property Law, and
  Tort Liability Law. All references should cite the Civil Code rather than the superseded
  individual statutes.
- **"法人"**: In PRC law, a 法人 (legal person) is a broader concept than in common law.
  It includes companies, government organs, public institutions, and social organisations.
  Translate as "legal person" or "legal entity" depending on context.
