terms to their correct English equivalents. Dutch employment law is governed by
BW Book 7 Title 10 (Arbeidsovereenkomst), the Wet arbeidsmarkt in balans (WAB 2020),
Wet werk en zekerheid (WWZ 2015), the Work and Security Act jurisprudence,
collective-labour-agreements regime (Wet op de CAO), and EU directives.

## Notes for Translators

- **BW 7:610 test**: Dutch employment is defined by (1) work (arbeid), (2) wages (loon), (3) authority (gezag). All three must be present. Gezagsverhouding (authority relationship) is the key differentiator from self-employment. Dutch courts (Deliveroo, X/Y) increasingly look at economic substance, not contract labels. Flag characterisation issues.
- **Ketenregeling (BW 7:668a)**: Three-contract / three-year rule. After 3 successive fixed-term contracts OR 3 years total (with breaks of less than 6 months counted), next contract converts to indefinite. Exceptions in some CLAs (e.g., temporary agency CLAs). Translate as "chain of contracts rule" with gloss.
- **WAB 2020 (Wet arbeidsmarkt in balans)**: Major reform — introduced cumulation ground (i-grond), increased transition payment for dismissed employees, differentiated unemployment contributions, restricted payroll-arrangement advantages. Key turning point in Dutch employment law. Flag pre/post-2020 differences.
- **Transitievergoeding (BW 7:673)**: Statutory severance due on employer-initiated termination. Formula: 1/3 monthly salary per year served (pro rata). Due from day 1 of employment. Capped at EUR 98,000 (2025) OR one year's salary if higher. Translate as "transition payment".
- **Billijke vergoeding**: Additional judicial compensation for serious breach by employer (especially in wrongful dismissal, constructive dismissal). No statutory cap. Different from transitievergoeding. Translate as "fair compensation"; flag distinction.
- **H-grond vs i-grond**: BW 7:669 lists exhaustive dismissal grounds a-i. H-grond = "other circumstances" catchall (narrow scope per case law). I-grond (since WAB 2020) = cumulation of insufficient grounds. If i-grond used, court may award up to 50% additional transitievergoeding. Translate with letter references preserved.
- **Opzegverbod**: Strong prohibitions on termination during sickness (up to 104 weeks), pregnancy/maternity, works council activity, military service, etc. Termination in violation is invalid/annullable. Always flag. Translate as "prohibition on termination".
- **Ontslag op staande voet**: Summary dismissal for urgent cause — must be communicated immediately, for urgent cause, with prompt consequences. Strict case law. Often litigated. Translate as "summary dismissal" with gloss.
- **UWV vs kantonrechter routes**: Post-WWZ (2015), dual system: UWV handles economic/long-term-sickness dismissals (a-grond, b-grond); kantonrechter handles personal-grounds (c-i). Translate "UWV" as "Employee Insurance Agency" or retain acronym with gloss.
- **Vaststellingsovereenkomst**: Negotiated mutual termination agreement — most Dutch terminations are resolved this way to preserve WW (unemployment benefit) entitlement. Employee has 14-day reflection period (BW 7:670b) — if not stated in agreement, extends to 3 weeks. Translate as "termination agreement" or "settlement agreement"; flag reflection period.
- **Concurrentiebeding (BW 7:653)**: Strictly regulated non-compete. Only valid in indefinite contracts; in fixed-term only with "dwingend bedrijfsbelang" justification in writing. New 2026 legislation expected to further restrict. Translate as "non-compete clause" and flag tightening regime.
- **Studiekostenbeding**: EU Directive on Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions (2022) prohibits charging for mandatory training. Dutch implementation: employer cannot recover cost of mandatory training. Translate as "study costs clawback" and flag post-2022 restriction.
- **Overgang van onderneming (BW 7:662+)**: Dutch TUPE / Acquired Rights Directive implementation. Only applies to asset deals / outsourcing — not share deals. Employees transfer automatically with terms intact. Strong protection. Translate as "transfer of undertaking" with TUPE reference for UK audience.
- **Wet op de ondernemingsraden (WOR)**: 50+ employee threshold. Works council has consent rights (Art. 27 — HR matters) and advisory rights (Art. 25 — strategic). Breach can invalidate decisions. Translate as "Works Councils Act"; flag WOR thresholds.
- **Wet Poortwachter**: Complex sickness/reintegration regime — 2 years of continued pay, with mandatory re-integration steps (first/second track), periodic case reviews, plans of approach, end-review. Non-compliance triggers UWV loonsancties (wage sanctions). Translate as "Gatekeeper Act" with gloss.
- **Wet toekomst pensioenen (Wtp, 2023)**: Major pension reform — migrating collective defined benefit to individual defined contribution. Transition deadline 2028. Complex consent/conversion processes. Flag for pension-affecting translations. Translate as "Future Pensions Act".
- **Wet DBA / Wet VBAR / Deliveroo case law**: Dutch enforcement of self-employment vs employment boundary is in flux. Hoge Raad Deliveroo (March 2023) held platform riders are employees. Uber Amsterdam Court of Appeal (Sept 2021) same conclusion. New law (Wet VBAR) in preparation; Wet DBA enforcement resuming 2025. Flag self-employment arrangements especially with individuals. Translate "schijnzelfstandigheid" as "sham self-employment".
- **Wet bescherming klokkenluiders**: Dutch whistleblower protection — implementing EU Directive. Mandatory internal reporting channels for 50+ employee employers. Translate as "Whistleblowers Protection Act".
- **College voor de Rechten van de Mens**: Replaced former Commissie Gelijke Behandeling in 2012. Non-binding opinions on discrimination cases, but persuasive. Translate as "Netherlands Institute for Human Rights"; flag quasi-judicial nature.
- **Gender diversity in boards (2022)**: 1/3 target for supervisory boards of large companies; NV listed companies subject to binding 1/3 rule for new appointments. Translate accurately; flag as Dutch-specific regime.
- **Belgian employment law**: Very different from Dutch. Belgian law uses Wet betreffende de arbeidsovereenkomsten 1978, different termination regime (very generous statutory notice/severance), different CLA system, Belgian works councils vs Dutch OR. Flag Belgian documents for cross-check.
- **English terms retained in Dutch practice**: bonus, stock option, outplacement, expat, employee benefits. Preserve in translation.
- **CAO / CLA**: Dutch collective-labour-agreement system very strong — many sectors have AVV'ed CLAs binding all employers. Flag which CLA applies in every employment translation; it often determines minimum terms.
- **Minimum wage**: Statutory (Wet minimumloon) updated twice-yearly (January and July). Since 2024, hourly rather than monthly basis. Flag current rate for contemporary documents.
