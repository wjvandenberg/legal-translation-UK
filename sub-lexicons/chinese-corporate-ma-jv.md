# Chinese (Mandarin) — Corporate, M&A, JV and Powers of Attorney Sub-Lexicon

Unified Chinese reference covering corporate resolutions and governance under the PRC
Company Law (2023 revision, effective 1 July 2024), mergers and acquisitions, joint
ventures, shareholders' agreements and related collateral, and powers of attorney /
proxies. Use alongside `references/corporate-ma-jv.md`. For tax-related terminology,
see `chinese-taxes.md` and `references/taxes.md`.

## 19. Notes

- **PRC Company Law (2023 revision)**: The Company Law was substantially revised in
  2023 (effective 1 July 2024). Key changes include the 5-year cap on the capital
  contribution period, simplified deregistration, enhanced director/shareholder
  liability for unpaid contributions, the option to replace the supervisory board with
  an audit committee for LLCs, and an expanded role for the legal representative. Cite
  the 2023 revision on first mention.
- **法定代表人 (legal representative)**: Unique to PRC corporate law. A mandatory role —
  typically the chairman of the board, the executive director, or the general manager
  (per the 2023 revision the company has more flexibility). The legal representative
  binds the company and bears personal liability for certain regulatory matters. No
  exact common-law equivalent — translate as "legal representative" and explain.
- **Company chop / 公章**: In PRC practice the company chop has legal effect equivalent
  to a signature. Several specialised chops exist (法定代表人章, 财务专用章, 合同专用章).
  Resolutions and POAs should always address custody and use of the chop.
- **LLC vs JSC governance**: An LLC (有限公司) uses a 股东会 (shareholders' meeting) and
  may have a single 执行董事 (executive director) instead of a full board. A JSC
  (股份公司) must have a 股东大会, a 董事会 and (subject to the 2023 revision flexibility)
  a 监事会 or audit committee. Keep the distinction.
- **特别决议 (special resolution / two-thirds majority)**: Under the PRC Company Law,
  certain matters (capital increase or reduction, merger, demerger, dissolution,
  amendment of articles) require approval by two-thirds of voting rights present. Flag
  thresholds.
- **Foreign Investment Law (2020) / Negative List**: The 2020 FIL abolished the old
  Sino-foreign JV law framework. All foreign-invested enterprises must now comply with
  the Company Law. The five-year transitional period ended 31 December 2024 — legacy
  EJV/CJV structures should be flagged. The Negative List for Foreign Investment Access
  restricts or prohibits foreign investment in listed sectors.
- **Foreign investment security review**: Acquisitions in sensitive sectors (defence,
  energy, agriculture, infrastructure, IT, finance, etc.) by foreign investors are
  subject to security review under the 2020 Measures jointly administered by NDRC and
  MOFCOM.
- **SAMR concentration filing (经营者集中申报)**: Transactions meeting the turnover
  thresholds (worldwide RMB 12bn AND PRC turnover of RMB 800m for at least two parties
  with at least RMB 400m each, per 2024 thresholds) require notification. Flag.
- **VIE structures**: Variable Interest Entity structures are widely used by PRC
  companies for overseas listing and to circumvent foreign-investment restrictions.
  These involve contractual control rather than equity ownership. Flag VIE-related
  provisions and note the regulatory uncertainty.
- **对赌条款 (bet-on / earn-out)**: A common feature of PRC M&A — performance-based
  price adjustments or compensation. PRC courts have developed specific rules on
  enforceability (notably the 2019 Jiangsu High Court guidelines and subsequent SPC
  guidance). Translate as "earn-out" or "performance-based adjustment" depending on
  structure, and flag enforceability nuances.
- **7号公告 (Public Notice No. 7)**: STA Public Notice [2015] No. 7 on indirect transfers
  of PRC taxable assets by non-residents. A reasonable-business-purpose test applies; if
  the offshore transfer is a tax-avoidance device, the PRC tax authority can
  recharacterise the transfer. Flag for cross-border deals.
- **注册资本 vs 实缴资本**: Under the 2023 Company Law revision, subscribed registered
  capital must be fully paid within 5 years of incorporation. This changed the previous
  regime of largely flexible contribution schedules. Flag capital-contribution
  provisions.
- **经营范围 (business scope)**: Defines the permitted activities of the company and is
  registered with SAMR. Activities outside the registered scope may be challenged
  (though the SPC has limited the consequences in many cases). Translate as "business
  scope" and flag restrictions.
- **公证 + 海牙认证 / 领事认证 for foreign POAs**: A POA executed outside Mainland China
  for use in Mainland China generally requires (a) notarisation in the country of
  origin and (b) either Hague apostille (after China's accession on 7 November 2023) or
  consular authentication by the Chinese embassy/consulate. Flag this two-step
  requirement.
- **民法典代理 (Civil Code agency, Arts. 161-175)**: The basic civil-law agency rules
  apply to POAs. The Civil Code distinguishes 委托代理 (entrusted agency, by POA) from
  法定代理 (statutory agency) and 指定代理 (court-designated agency). Concepts of
  apparent agency (表见代理) and unauthorised agency (无权代理) are statutory.
- **AIC / SAMR registration**: Corporate resolutions affecting registered items
  (directors, legal representative, registered capital, business scope) must be filed
  with the local branch of SAMR (formerly AIC). Updates appear on the 国家企业信用信息
  公示系统 — a useful diligence resource.
