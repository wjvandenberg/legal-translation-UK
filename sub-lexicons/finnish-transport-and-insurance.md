# Finnish — Transport and Insurance Sub-Lexicon

Combines Finnish-language transport and insurance vocabulary. Part 1 covers maritime, road, rail, air and multimodal transport: carriage contracts, bills of lading, charterparties, freight forwarding, carrier liability. Part 2 covers insurance contracts, life and non-life lines, reinsurance, brokers, claims handling and regulatory framework. Marine/transport insurance (cargo, hull, P&I, carrier liability) sits at the intersection.

## Notes

- **Maritime Code (merilaki, 674/1994)**: Finland's principal maritime legislation, aligned
  with the other Nordic maritime codes (Sweden, Denmark, Norway). Implements the Hague-Visby
  Rules for cargo carriage. Reference as "Maritime Code" on first mention.
- **Nordic maritime tradition**: Finland shares a common maritime legal framework with the
  other Nordic countries. The Nordic maritime codes are substantially harmonised. Terminology
  should be consistent with the Nordic tradition.
- **"Yhteinen haveri" (general average)**: Follows the York-Antwerp Rules in Finnish
  maritime practice. The Maritime Code provisions mirror the Rules.
- **CMR Convention**: Finland is a party to the CMR Convention on International Carriage
  of Goods by Road. CMR applies to international road transport. Flag when translating
  road transport agreements with a cross-border element.
- **"Huolitsija" (freight forwarder)**: The Finnish Freight Forwarders Association publishes
  the Nordic General Conditions for Freight Forwarding (Pohjoismaisten Speditööriliiton
  yleiset ehdot / NSAB). NSAB terms are widely used. Follow NSAB terminology.
- **Winter navigation**: Finland's Baltic ports face ice conditions. Ice class requirements
  and icebreaker assistance are regulated by the Finnish Transport and Communications
  Agency (Traficom). Flag winter navigation clauses in maritime contracts.

## Notes

- **Insurance Contracts Act (vakuutussopimuslaki, 543/1994)**: Finland's principal insurance
  contract legislation. Relatively consumer-protective. Reference as "Insurance Contracts
  Act" on first mention.
- **Insurance Companies Act (vakuutusyhtiölaki)**: The regulatory statute governing
  insurance companies. Solvency II (EU) applies to Finnish insurers. Administered by the
  Financial Supervisory Authority (Finanssivalvonta/FIN-FSA).
- **"Tiedonantovelvollisuus" (duty of disclosure)**: Under the Insurance Contracts Act,
  the policyholder must provide correct and complete information at the time of application.
  Breach can lead to reduced compensation or policy cancellation. The insurer must also
  disclose policy terms. Translate as "duty of disclosure".
- **"Omavastuu"**: The deductible / excess. Finnish insurance commonly uses both fixed-
  amount and percentage-based deductibles. Translate as "deductible" or "excess (UK)".
- **"Takautumisoikeus" (subrogation)**: Under the Insurance Contracts Act, the insurer
  is subrogated to the insured's rights against third parties to the extent of payment.
  Translate as "subrogation" or "right of recourse".
- **"Liikennevakuutus" (motor third-party liability)**: Mandatory motor insurance under
  the Motor Liability Insurance Act (liikennevakuutuslaki). Covers personal injury and
  property damage caused by the use of a motor vehicle. No-fault system for personal
  injury. Flag the no-fault element.
