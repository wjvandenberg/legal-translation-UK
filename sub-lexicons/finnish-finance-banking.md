## Notes

- **Finnish secured transactions**: Finland uses a system of enterprise mortgage
  (yrityskiinnitys) — a floating charge over the movable assets of a business, registered
  with the Patent and Registration Office (PRH). This is the primary non-possessory
  security over movables. Translate as "enterprise mortgage" or "floating charge" and note
  the registration requirement.
- **"Omavelkainen takaus"**: The standard Finnish guarantee — the guarantor is primarily
  liable alongside the debtor (not entitled to benefit of excussion). This is the default
  form. Translate as "guarantee (as a primary obligation)" and note the primary liability.
- **Nordic LMA**: Finnish syndicated loans typically follow LMA (Loan Market Association)
  documentation adapted for Finnish law. The terminology should align with LMA conventions
  with Finnish law modifications.
- **Euribor**: The standard reference rate for EUR-denominated Finnish loans. EONIA has
  been replaced by €STR. Flag the applicable benchmark.
- **"Yrityskiinnitys"**: Registered at PRH (Patent and Registration Office). Covers all
  movable property of the enterprise — inventory, equipment, receivables. Does not cover
  real property or shares. Priority determined by registration order. Flag the scope.

- **Credit Institutions Act (laki luottolaitostoiminnasta, 610/2014)**: Finland's principal
  banking legislation. Implements the CRD IV/V package. Reference as "Credit Institutions
  Act" on first mention.
- **ECB/SSM supervision**: Finnish significant banks are directly supervised by the ECB
  under the Single Supervisory Mechanism. The FIN-FSA supervises less significant
  institutions. Flag the applicable supervisor.
- **"Yrityskiinnitys" (enterprise mortgage / floating charge)**: A registered security
  interest over all movable assets of an enterprise. Registered with the PRH. Priority
  determined by registration order. Does not cover real property, shares, or specific
  pledged assets. Flag the scope limitations.
- **"Omavelkainen takaus"**: The standard Finnish guarantee — the guarantor is primarily
  liable (no benefit of excussion). Under the Guarantees and Third-Party Pledges Act
  (laki takauksesta ja vierasvelkapanttauksesta), private individuals acting as guarantors
  have specific consumer protections. Flag when a natural person guarantor is involved.
- **Nordea / OP / Danske**: The Finnish banking market is dominated by Nordea, OP Financial
  Group, and Danske Bank. Finnish banking documentation often follows LMA standards adapted
  for Finnish law and Nordic market practice.
- **AML (rahanpesulaki)**: Finland's Anti-Money Laundering Act implements the EU AML
  directives. Supervised by FIN-FSA for financial institutions. Finland also participates
  in the EU AML Authority framework. Flag AML obligations.

## Notes

- **Securities Markets Act (arvopaperimarkkinalaki, 746/2012)**: Finland's principal
  securities legislation, implementing MiFID II and other EU securities directives.
  Reference as "Securities Markets Act" on first mention.
- **FIN-FSA (Finanssivalvonta)**: The Financial Supervisory Authority — Finland's
  integrated financial regulator. Supervises banks, insurers, and securities markets.
  Reference as "Financial Supervisory Authority (FIN-FSA)" on first mention.
- **EU securities regulation**: As an EU member, Finland applies EU regulations directly
  (MAR, Prospectus Regulation, EMIR, SFDR, etc.). Finnish securities law must be read
  together with EU regulations. Flag the applicable EU regulation.
- **Nasdaq Helsinki**: Finland's stock exchange, operated by Nasdaq Nordic. Part of the
  Nasdaq Nordic market structure (Helsinki, Stockholm, Copenhagen, Iceland). Trading rules
  follow the Nasdaq Nordic Member Rules.
- **"Pakollinen ostotarjous" (mandatory bid)**: Under the Securities Markets Act, a
  shareholder acquiring more than 30% (or 50%) of voting rights in a listed company must
  make a mandatory bid for all remaining shares. Flag the thresholds.
- **Nordic securities tradition**: Finnish securities law shares common features with other
  Nordic jurisdictions. Nominee registration and the CSD system (Euroclear Finland) are
  important features. Flag when translating shareholding structures.
