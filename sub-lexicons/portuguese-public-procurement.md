## Notes

- **CCP (Código dos Contratos Públicos)**: Portugal's Public Contracts Code (DL 18/2008,
  as amended), transposing the EU procurement directives. Reference as "Public Contracts
  Code (CCP)" on first mention.
- **"Ajuste direto"**: A Portuguese procurement procedure allowing direct award without
  competition, subject to strict value thresholds and conditions (Arts. 112-129 CCP).
  Translate as "direct award" — broader than the EU "negotiated procedure without
  publication".
- **"Audiência prévia"**: A mandatory prior hearing phase in Portuguese procurement where
  tenderers can comment on the preliminary evaluation report before the final decision.
  A specifically Portuguese procedural requirement with no direct common law equivalent.
- **"Caução"**: Performance security required under Portuguese public procurement law.
  Typically 5% of the contract price. Translate as "performance bond" or "performance
  guarantee".
- **"Resolução sancionatória"**: The contracting authority's right to terminate for the
  contractor's default — a unilateral administrative act, not requiring court approval.
  Translate as "termination for default" with note on the unilateral nature.
- **"Resgate"**: A power unique to concession contracts whereby the contracting authority
  can unilaterally take over the concession before its term, subject to compensation.
  Translate as "unilateral takeover" or "compulsory acquisition of the concession".
