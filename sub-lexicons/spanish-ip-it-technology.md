## Notes

- **"Marca" spelling**: Use "trade mark" (two words, UK) or "trademark" (one word, US)
  depending on target audience. Default to UK (two words).
- **"Propiedad intelectual" vs "propiedad industrial"**: In Spain, "propiedad intelectual"
  technically covers copyright and related rights, while "propiedad industrial" covers
  patents, trade marks, designs, etc. This differs from the common law use of "intellectual
  property" as an umbrella term. Translate both as "intellectual property" unless the
  distinction is legally relevant.
- **"Diligencias de comprobación de hechos"**: Spanish evidence preservation measures
  similar to French saisie-contrefaçon. Translate as "evidence preservation measures".
- **OEPM**: Oficina Española de Patentes y Marcas — the Spanish patent and trade mark
  office. Keep the abbreviation "OEPM" and translate on first mention.

## Notes

- **"Licencia"**: Spanish uses "licencia" for both noun and verb. UK English uses
  "licence" (noun) / "license" (verb). US English uses "license" for both. Default
  to UK noun form.
- **"Encargado del tratamiento"**: The Spanish LOPDGDD term for data processor.
  Under EU GDPR, this is a "processor" (Art. 4(8)).
- **AEPD**: Agencia Española de Protección de Datos — the Spanish data protection
  authority. Keep abbreviation and translate on first mention.
