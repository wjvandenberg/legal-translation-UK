## Notes

- **Construction regulation**: Hungarian construction is regulated by the Building Act
  (1997. évi LXXVIII. törvény) and implementing government decrees. Building permits are
  issued by the local building authority (építésügyi hatóság). Reference as "Building Act"
  on first mention.
- **"Felelős műszaki vezető"**: A statutory role under Hungarian construction law — the
  person technically responsible for construction. Must hold specific professional
  qualifications and registration with the Hungarian Chamber of Engineers. Translate as
  "site manager" or "responsible technical manager" and note the regulatory requirement.
- **FIDIC in Hungary**: FIDIC conditions are used for EU-funded and international projects.
  Domestic projects typically use conditions based on the Ptk. vállalkozási szerződés
  provisions or industry standard terms. Identify the applicable form.
- **"Kötbér" (contractual penalty)**: Widely used in Hungarian construction contracts.
  Under the Ptk., the kötbér is payable without proof of damage but is capped at 50%
  of the relevant obligation's value (for delay: the contract price). Courts may reduce
  excessive penalties. Flag the statutory cap.
- **EU-funded projects**: Many Hungarian infrastructure projects are EU-funded and subject
  to EU procurement rules. FIDIC conditions with specific EU-required modifications are
  commonly used. Flag when translating EU-funded project documentation.

- **Electricity Act (2007. évi LXXXVI. törvény)**: The primary legislation governing
  Hungary's electricity sector. Covers generation, transmission, distribution, and trading.
  Reference as "Electricity Act" on first mention.
- **MEKH**: The Hungarian Energy and Public Utility Regulatory Authority is the independent
  energy regulator. Issues licences, sets regulated tariffs, and oversees the electricity
  and gas markets. Flag MEKH involvement.
- **METÁR (Megújuló Támogatási Rendszer)**: Hungary's renewable energy support scheme,
  replacing the earlier KÁT (kötelező átvételi rendszer) mandatory offtake system. METÁR
  provides feed-in tariffs for small installations and a premium scheme for larger ones.
  Translate as "premium support scheme" and explain the dual structure.
- **MAVIR**: The transmission system operator, responsible for system balancing and grid
  security. Separate from generators and distributors. Keep the Hungarian acronym.
- **Paks II nuclear project**: Hungary's major energy infrastructure project (EU-significant).
  Special legislation governs the project. Flag when translating Paks-related documents.
- **EU energy regulation**: Hungary is subject to the EU Clean Energy Package, including
  the Electricity Regulation and Electricity Directive. Hungarian energy law implements
  these EU requirements. Flag EU-driven provisions.
