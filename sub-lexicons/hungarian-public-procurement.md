## Notes

- **Public Procurement Act (2015. évi CXLIII. törvény)**: The primary legislation governing
  public procurement in Hungary, implementing the EU Public Procurement Directives
  (2014/24/EU, 2014/25/EU, 2014/23/EU). Reference as "Public Procurement Act" on first
  mention.
- **Közbeszerzési Hatóság (Public Procurement Authority)**: The supervisory body overseeing
  Hungary's procurement system. Publishes guidance, maintains the procurement bulletin
  (Közbeszerzési Értesítő), and monitors compliance. Reference as "Public Procurement
  Authority."
- **Közbeszerzési Döntőbizottság (Public Procurement Arbitration Board)**: The first-instance
  review body for procurement disputes. Decisions are appealable to the Budapest-Capital
  Regional Court (Fővárosi Törvényszék). Applications must be filed within specific
  deadlines. Flag review deadlines.
- **"Hiánypótlás" (request for supplementary information)**: A distinctive feature of
  Hungarian procurement — the contracting authority must request missing documents or
  clarifications before rejecting a tender. Mandatory under the Public Procurement Act.
  No exact common law equivalent. Translate as "request for supplementary information."
- **EU-funded procurement**: A significant portion of Hungarian public procurement is
  EU-funded. Additional rules apply regarding eligibility, audit, and reporting. Flag
  EU-funding requirements.
- **Electronic procurement (EKR)**: The Elektronikus Közbeszerzési Rendszer (EKR) is
  Hungary's mandatory electronic procurement platform. All procurement procedures must be
  conducted electronically through the EKR. Flag when translating.
