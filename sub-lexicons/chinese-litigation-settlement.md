## Notes

- **PRC Arbitration Law (仲裁法)**: Enacted 1994 — governs domestic and foreign-related
  arbitration. A comprehensive revision has been in progress since 2021. Reference as
  "Arbitration Law" on first mention.
- **"法院调解" (judicial mediation)**: PRC courts actively encourage mediation during
  litigation. A court-issued mediation statement (调解书) has the same enforceability
  as a judgment. This is a distinctive feature of PRC civil procedure.
- **CIETAC**: The oldest and most prominent PRC arbitration institution. Handles both
  domestic and international disputes. Reference as "China International Economic and
  Trade Arbitration Commission (CIETAC)" on first mention.
- **Two-instance system**: PRC litigation follows a two-instance final system (两审终审制).
  After the second-instance judgment, the case is final. Retrial (再审) is exceptional.
  Flag when translating appeal provisions.
- **"诉讼时效"**: The general limitation period under PRC law is 3 years (Art. 188 Civil
  Code). It runs from when the right-holder knew or should have known of the infringement.
  The maximum period from the date of infringement is 20 years. Flag when translating
  limitation provisions.
- **New York Convention**: China acceded to the New York Convention in 1987 with a
  reciprocity reservation and a commercial reservation. Foreign arbitral awards are
  enforceable in PRC courts subject to these reservations.

## Notes for Translators — Litigation

- **判决 vs 裁定 vs 决定**: All three are court outputs but differ: 判决 = judgment (merits), 裁定 = ruling (procedural/some substantive), 决定 = decision (narrow procedural, e.g., recusal). Do not translate all three as "judgment" or "decision" interchangeably.
- **被告 vs 被告人**: 被告 is civil defendant; 被告人 is criminal defendant (post-indictment); 犯罪嫌疑人 is criminal suspect (pre-indictment). English "defendant" covers both but Chinese legal writing distinguishes — preserve when tracking procedural stage.
- **人民检察院 / 检察官**: "People's Procuratorate" and "procurator" are the conventional translations. Do not use "prosecutor's office" for 检察院 in formal legal translation, though "prosecutor" for 检察官 is acceptable in less formal contexts.
- **上诉 vs 再审 vs 申诉**: 上诉 = ordinary appeal (within statutory window); 再审 = retrial (extraordinary post-finality remedy via trial supervision); 申诉 = petition (informal post-final request). Distinct procedural tracks — do not conflate as "appeal".
- **抗诉**: Procuratorate's protest in trial supervision or appeal — translate as "procuratorial protest" or "protest", not "appeal" (appeal is a party's remedy; protest is a state-organ remedy).
- **调解书**: Enforceable in the same way as a judgment once served. Translate as "mediated settlement" or "mediation document"; do not water down to "memorandum of settlement".
- **执行 / 被执行人**: Prefer "enforcement" and "person subject to enforcement" or "enforcement debtor". Avoid "execution" in civil contexts (collides with "execution" of instruments and capital punishment).
- **失信被执行人**: Often translated as "person subject to enforcement for dishonesty" or "judgment debtor on the social-credit blacklist". No concise English equivalent — gloss may be needed.
- **行政行为**: Prefer "administrative act" (continental tradition) over "administrative action" where ambiguous. "Administrative action" in US law suggests litigation; "administrative act" is clearer for PRC context.
- **行政复议 vs 行政诉讼**: 行政复议 = administrative reconsideration (internal executive review); 行政诉讼 = administrative litigation (judicial review). Distinct stages — do not merge.
- **认罪认罚**: The 2018 institutionalised plea-and-sentence-acceptance framework; translate as "plea of guilt and acceptance of punishment" with explanatory note on first use. Do not translate as "guilty plea" alone — the 认罚 (acceptance of punishment) component is material.
- **取保候审**: Translate as "release on bail pending trial" or "取保候审 (bail pending trial)"; not "release on guaranty".
- **疑罪从无**: Latin maxim equivalent "in dubio pro reo" or plain "acquittal in case of doubt"; a recent (post-1996) reform principle in PRC criminal procedure.
- **检察院 hierarchy**: 最高人民检察院 (SPP), 省级人民检察院, 市级人民检察院, 基层人民检察院 — translate with full name on first mention; SPP is recognised acronym.
- **死刑复核**: Death penalty review by the SPC is a mandatory post-second-instance procedure; translate in full on first mention.
