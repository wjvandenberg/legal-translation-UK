## Notes

- **Patent Act (特許法)**: Japan's principal patent statute. Administered by the Japan
  Patent Office (JPO/特許庁). Reference as "Patent Act" on first mention.
- **"著作者人格権" (moral rights)**: Under Japanese Copyright Act, moral rights are
  non-transferable and cannot be waived. This is stricter than many jurisdictions. Standard
  practice is a "non-assertion" (不行使) clause rather than a waiver. Flag in translation.
- **APPI (個人情報保護法)**: Japan's data protection law, substantially revised in 2020
  (effective April 2022). Key features include cross-border transfer rules, individual
  rights, and breach notification requirements. Japan has EU adequacy recognition.
  Reference as "APPI" on first mention.
- **"専用実施権" vs "通常実施権"**: The exclusive licence (専用実施権) must be registered
  with the JPO to have effect against third parties. The non-exclusive licence (通常実施権)
  is effective without registration since the 2011 Patent Act amendment. Flag the
  registration requirement.
- **Antimonopoly Act and IP**: The Japan Fair Trade Commission (JFTC) publishes Guidelines
  on the Use of Intellectual Property under the Antimonopoly Act. Restrictive licensing
  practices (e.g., grant-back, non-compete, tying) may be challenged. Flag potential issues.

## Notes

- **"業務委託" in IT context**: Japanese IT agreements are frequently titled "業務委託契約"
  (outsourcing agreement). As with service agreements generally, the legal classification
  as 請負 (contract for work) or 準委任 (quasi-mandate) affects the warranty and liability
  regime. Systems development is typically classified as 請負 for the development phase
  and 準委任 for requirements definition and testing phases. Flag the classification.
- **METI model contracts**: The Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI/経済産業省)
  publishes model contracts for systems development and data utilisation. These are widely
  referenced in the Japanese IT industry. Follow METI terminology where applicable.
- **"ソースコードエスクロー"**: Source code escrow is less common in Japan than in the US/UK
  but is increasingly used in critical enterprise software deals. No specific statutory
  framework. Translate as "source code escrow" and note.
- **APPI and cloud services**: When personal information is stored on cloud infrastructure
  (especially overseas servers), APPI cross-border transfer rules apply. The 2022 amendments
  require detailed information about the foreign country's data protection regime. Flag
  when translating cloud service agreements.
- **"請負" classification for development**: If the IT agreement is classified as 請負,
  the vendor bears non-conformity liability (契約不適合責任) under the 2020 Civil Code
  reform. The limitation period is one year from discovery. Flag in translation.
