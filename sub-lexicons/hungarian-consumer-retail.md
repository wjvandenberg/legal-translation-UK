## Notes

- **Consumer Protection Act (1997. évi CLV. törvény)**: Hungary's primary consumer
  protection legislation. Covers information obligations, unfair commercial practices,
  and enforcement. Reference as "Consumer Protection Act" on first mention.
- **Distance Selling Decree (45/2014. Korm. rendelet)**: Implements the EU Consumer
  Rights Directive. Establishes the 14-day right of withdrawal for distance and
  off-premises contracts. Detailed information requirements for online sales. Reference
  as "Distance Selling Decree."
- **"Kötelező jótállás" (mandatory guarantee)**: Hungary has a system of mandatory
  commercial guarantees for certain consumer goods, governed by Government Decree
  (151/2003. Korm. rendelet). The guarantee period is 1-3 years depending on the product's
  value. This goes beyond the EU minimum. Translate as "mandatory guarantee" and flag the
  extended protection.
- **"Kellékszavatosság" (warranty for conformity)**: Under the Ptk. (6:159-6:167),
  consumers can claim repair, replacement, price reduction, or rescission for non-conforming
  goods. The limitation period is 2 years from delivery. The EU Sales of Goods Directive
  extends the reversal of burden of proof to 1 year. Flag warranty periods.
- **"Békéltető testület" (consumer arbitration board)**: Alternative dispute resolution
  bodies attached to the county chambers of commerce. Binding for the trader only if the
  trader has submitted to the board's jurisdiction. Translate as "consumer arbitration
  board" or "consumer mediation board."
- **Unfair Commercial Practices Directive**: Hungary implements the EU UCPD through the
  Competition Act (1996. évi LVII. törvény) and the Consumer Protection Act. The GVH
  enforces against businesses and the consumer protection authority against other traders.
  Flag the dual enforcement model.
