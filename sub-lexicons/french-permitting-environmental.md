equivalents. Covers EIA, ICPE regime, water, waste, nature, chemicals,
contaminated sites, climate, and urbanism.

## Notes

- **Code de l'environnement**: Central codification (2000). Livre I:
  Common provisions, information, participation, damage. Livre II: Water and
  air. Livre III: Natural areas. Livre IV: Fauna and flora. Livre V: ICPE and
  waste. Livre VI: Mining and quarrying. Livre VII: Overseas. Regularly
  amended — verify consolidated version.
- **Autorisation environnementale unique (2017)**: Consolidates ICPE
  authorisation + water-law authorisation + forest clearance + protected
  species derogation + energy authorisation + others into a single procedure
  before the prefect. Pre-2017 procedures were separate. Common translation
  pitfall — do not confuse with "autorisation environnementale" in the narrow
  pre-2017 sense.
- **ICPE nomenclature and regimes**: Three regimes by increasing risk —
  Declaration (D), Registration (E), Authorisation (A). Each nomenclature
  rubric specifies the regime. Declaration = simple filing; Registration =
  standardised prescriptions; Authorisation = full EIA and public inquiry.
  Quasi-permanent translation distinction.
- **IED / MTD conclusions**: French ICPE-A installations falling within IED
  Annex I must comply with MTD conclusions (BAT conclusions). Conformity check
  within 4 years of publication. Substantial modifications require new
  authorisation review.
- **Loi AGEC (2020)**: Anti-Waste for a Circular Economy Act — significant
  EPR expansion (construction products, toys, textiles, tobacco butts,
  sanitary textiles, etc.), bans on single-use plastics, reuse obligations.
  Ongoing implementation decrees through 2028. Critical for FMCG and retail
  translations.
- **ERC (Éviter, Réduire, Compenser)**: Mandatory mitigation hierarchy
  across EIA, water-law authorisation and species derogations. "Absence de
  perte nette de biodiversité" doctrine. Compensation measures must be
  additional, permanent, equivalent and controllable. Common source of
  litigation.
- **Loi Climat et Résilience (2021)**: Transposes Convention Citoyenne pour
  le Climat recommendations. Key measures: objective of zero net
  artificialisation by 2050 (ZAN), low-emission mobility zones (ZFE) for
  cities > 150k, ban on short-haul flights where rail alternative < 2.5 hours,
  climate-related CSR reporting.
- **Loi APER (2023)**: Accelerates renewables permitting — "zones
  d'accélération" designated by municipalities, reduced consultation periods,
  overriding public-interest presumption for major projects (article 19).
  Paralleled by the Nuclear Acceleration Act for new reactor permitting.
  Central in energy-project translations.
- **Cessation d'activité ICPE**: Obligation to rehabilitate the site to a
  state compatible with future use. Three-step procedure (notification, memoir
  on rehabilitation, confirmation). Article L. 556-1 introduces certified
  technical office ("bureau d'études certifié") requirement since 2023.
  Critical in M&A environmental due diligence.
- **Préjudice écologique (2016)**: Civil-law ecological harm introduced in
  Civil Code articles 1246-1252 following the Erika case. Strict liability;
  restoration in kind prioritised. Expansive standing including environmental
  associations. Growing body of case law.
- **Convention d'Aarhus — French implementation**: Charter of the Environment
  (2005) is constitutional — article 7 guarantees participation; Code de
  l'environnement articles L. 120-1 et seq. operationalise. Commission
  nationale du débat public (CNDP) organises major-project debates.

  `french-corporate-ma-jv.md` for environmental reps and warranties;
  `french-litigation-settlement.md` for administrative-court procedure.
