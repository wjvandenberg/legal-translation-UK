## Notes

- **Employment Contracts Act (työsopimuslaki, 55/2001)**: Finland's principal employment
  legislation. Governs the formation, terms, and termination of employment contracts.
  Reference as "Employment Contracts Act" on first mention.
- **"Yleissitovuus" (universal applicability)**: Finnish collective agreements can be declared
  universally applicable, meaning they bind all employers in the sector — including non-
  organised employers. This is a distinctive feature of the Finnish labour market. Flag
  the applicable collective agreement.
- **"Yhteistoimintaneuvottelut" (co-determination negotiations)**: Under the Co-determination
  Act (yhteistoimintalaki, 1333/2021), employers with 20+ employees must conduct negotiations
  before redundancies, reorganisations, or other significant changes. Minimum negotiation
  periods apply (14 days or 6 weeks). Flag the obligation.
- **"Kilpailukieltosopimus" (non-compete agreement)**: Post-employment non-competes are
  regulated by the Employment Contracts Act. Maximum duration: 1 year (6 months if no
  compensation). Since 2022, employers must pay compensation for all non-competes exceeding
  6 months. Flag the compensation requirement.
- **"Koeaika" (probationary period)**: Maximum 6 months. During probation, either party
  may terminate with immediate effect (no notice period required), but not on discriminatory
  or otherwise improper grounds. Flag the limitations.
- **Collective agreement system**: Finnish labour law is heavily shaped by collective
  agreements. Approximately 90% of employees are covered. The collective agreement
  determines many employment terms including pay scales, working time, and overtime
  compensation. Always identify the applicable collective agreement.
