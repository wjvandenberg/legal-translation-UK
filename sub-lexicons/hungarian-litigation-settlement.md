## Notes for Translators

- **New Pp. (2016. évi CXXX. tv.)**: Hungary's new Civil Procedure Code, in force from 1 January 2018, introduced a radically two-phase trial structure (osztott perszerkezet): a preparatory phase (perfelvételi szak) where the scope of dispute is fixed, followed by a merits phase (érdemi tárgyalási szak). Parties' pleadings are sharply restricted after the preparatory phase closes. The code also strengthened judicial case management (anyagi pervezetés). Flag this bifurcation when translating — it has no direct English equivalent.
- **Court structure**: Four-tier — district courts (járásbíróság), regional courts (törvényszék), regional courts of appeal (ítélőtábla, five of them), and the Kúria as apex. The regional court is first-instance court for high-value claims, labour disputes, and IP matters. Translate "Kúria" as "Kúria" with "(Supreme Court)" gloss on first use to preserve the proper name.
- **Felülvizsgálat**: The Kúria's extraordinary review power on points of law. Strictly distinguish from "fellebbezés" (ordinary appeal). Translate as "review" or "review on points of law".
- **Jogegységi határozat**: Binding uniformity decisions issued by the Kúria to resolve divergent case law. Precedent-like but technically abstract. Translate as "uniformity decision" and flag for significance.
- **Ákr. (2016. évi CL. tv.) vs earlier Ket.**: The general administrative procedure statute was replaced in 2018. Older documents will refer to "Ket." (Közigazgatási hatósági eljárás és szolgáltatás általános szabályairól szóló tv.) — preserve but flag as superseded.
- **Kp. (2017. évi I. tv.)**: The Code of Administrative Litigation — separate from the Civil Procedure Code since 2018. Admin cases proceed by distinct action types (megtámadási / marasztalási / megállapítási / mulasztási / normakontroll per). Translate action types consistently and explain.
- **Abolished administrative court reform**: The 2018 reform to create a separate administrative court system was suspended in 2019; administrative cases are now heard by special chambers within ordinary courts (notably the Fővárosi Törvényszék). Flag when pre-2020 documents refer to the now-cancelled Public Administration High Court (Közigazgatási Felsőbíróság).
- **Be. (2017. évi XC. tv.)**: Modernised Criminal Procedure Code in force from 1 July 2018. Introduced plea agreement (egyezség a vádról és a büntetés kiszabásáról), modernised investigation structure. Translate "terhelt" as "accused" (generic), "gyanúsított" as "suspect" (pre-indictment), "vádlott" as "defendant" (post-indictment).
- **Btk. (2012. évi C. tv.)**: Criminal Code — substantive criminal law. Distinguishes bűntett (felony, prison > 2 years) from vétség (misdemeanour); szabálysértés (petty offence) is governed separately and is administrative-criminal in nature.
- **Pótmagánvádló**: Substitute private prosecutor — unique feature allowing the injured party to continue prosecution after the prosecutor has declined. No direct common-law equivalent; translate and explain.
- **Sérelemdíj**: Post-2014 Ptk. innovation (§ 2:52) — compensation payable for violation of personality rights without proof of specific pecuniary damage. Replaced the pre-2014 "nem vagyoni kártérítés" regime. Translate as "injury fee" or "compensation for infringement of personality rights" — explain the no-damage-proof feature.
- **Egyezség (Ptk. 6:27)**: Contractual settlement requiring mutual concessions. Can be out-of-court or judicially approved. Judicial settlement (bírói egyezség) has force of final judgment and is directly enforceable. Distinguish in translation.
- **Fizetési meghagyás**: Order-for-payment procedure administered by notaries (MOKK — Magyar Országos Közjegyzői Kamara), not courts. Streamlined collection procedure. If debtor contests, case becomes contentious at court. Preserve "payment order" terminology; flag the notary administration.
- **Arbitration Act (2017. évi LX. tv.)**: In force from 1 January 2018, UNCITRAL Model Law-based with 2006 amendments. Replaced the 1994 arbitration statute. Covers both domestic and international arbitration. The "Budapest Arbitration Court" (Állandó Választottbíróság at MKIK) is the leading institution. Translate statutory references as "Arbitration Act" with full Hungarian citation in parentheses on first use.
- **Arbitrability**: Under Art. 1 of the Arbitration Act, arbitrability extends to commercial disputes where parties may freely dispose; consumer and employment arbitration restricted. Flag where relevant.
- **PBT (Pénzügyi Békéltető Testület)**: Financial Arbitration Board operated by the National Bank of Hungary (MNB) for consumer disputes with financial service providers. Not a court, but decisions can be binding. Translate as "Financial Arbitration Board" (note: not strictly arbitration in the UNCITRAL sense).
- **Alkotmányjogi panasz**: Constitutional complaint — several forms under Act CLI of 2011 on the Constitutional Court: (i) against legislation applied in individual case (real complaint, Art. 26(1)), (ii) direct complaint without intervening application (Art. 26(2)), (iii) against court decisions (Art. 27). Distinguish carefully.
- **Mediation Act (2002. évi LV. tv.)**: Governs professional mediators; mediation agreement does not automatically have force of judgment unless incorporated into court-approved settlement. Court-annexed mediation is available and may be ordered.
- **Jogerő / anyagi jogerő / alaki jogerő**: Distinguish formal res judicata (no more ordinary appeals available) from substantive res judicata (claim barred). Both translated as "res judicata" in English but the Hungarian distinction may matter — flag when clear.
- **Végrehajtás**: Enforcement handled by independent court bailiffs (önálló bírósági végrehajtó) chartered through the Hungarian Chamber of Court Bailiffs (MBVK). Distinguish from self-help enforcement. Preserve "court bailiff" terminology.
- **Public documents (közokirat) vs private documents (magánokirat)**: Hungarian law places strong evidentiary weight on "közokirat" (authentic instrument prepared by notary/court/authority) and "teljes bizonyító erejű magánokirat" (private document with full evidentiary value — signed with two witnesses or by lawyer-countersigned). These distinctions matter for enforceability. Translate carefully.
- **Ideiglenes intézkedés vs biztosítási intézkedés**: Interim injunctions (Pp. 103+) secure the legal position during litigation; protective measures (biztosítási intézkedés) secure future enforcement. Distinguish.
- **Labour courts**: Labour cases formerly heard by dedicated "munkaügyi bíróság" (labour courts); since 2013 reform, handled by labour chambers within regional courts. Continue to translate as "labour court" for readability.
- **Kúria's guiding functions**: The Kúria issues uniformity decisions (jogegységi határozat), guiding court decisions (elvi bírósági határozat — EBH), and chamber opinions (kollégiumi vélemény). Together they function as Hungary's precedent-like system even though Hungary is formally civil-law. Flag.
- **Insolvency**: Separate regime (1991. évi XLIX. tv. on bankruptcy and liquidation — Csődtörvény). Csőd = reorganisation / bankruptcy with debtor protection; felszámolás = liquidation. Do not conflate. Translate "csőd" as "bankruptcy" or "reorganisation" depending on context; flag.
- **Szabálysértés**: Petty / administrative infractions under the separate Act II of 2012. Administrative-criminal hybrid. Translate as "petty offence" or "infraction"; distinguish from bűntett / vétség under the Criminal Code.
