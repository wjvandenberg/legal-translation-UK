## Notes for Translators

- **Aanneming van werk (BW 7:750)**: The Dutch-law name for a construction/works contract. Translate as "contract for works" or in EPC context "EPC contract". Distinguish from "opdracht" (services, BW 7:400).
- **Oplevering**: Critical concept. Under BW 7:758, oplevering marks (a) transfer of risk, (b) start of defects liability period, (c) payment of final instalment/release of retention. Two-stage oplevering (voorlopig + definitief) is market standard for EPC. Translate as "delivery" or "taking-over" depending on audience (construction specialists prefer "taking-over"; general commercial "delivery").
- **Waarschuwingsplicht**: Contractor's duty to warn under BW 7:754 — contractor must warn the employer of errors in specifications or designs provided by the employer. Common source of disputes. Translate as "duty to warn" and flag where disclaimed.
- **UAV-GC 2005 / UAV 2012**: Dutch standard general conditions. UAV 2012 = traditional (bouwteam/design-bid-build); UAV-GC 2005 = integrated contracts (design-build, EPC). These are incorporated by reference in most Dutch construction agreements. Translate names literally ("Uniform Administrative Conditions"); preserve abbreviation.
- **Omgevingswet**: The new consolidated Dutch environmental and planning law in force from 1 January 2024. It replaces 26 separate laws including the Wabo, Crisis- en herstelwet, Wet ruimtelijke ordening, etc. Translate as "Environment and Planning Act" or keep "Omgevingswet" with gloss. Post-2024 permits go through the omgevingsvergunning.
- **Stikstof / stikstofcrisis**: After the 2019 Raad van State ruling striking down the PAS, nitrogen deposition has become a major permitting bottleneck. AERIUS calculations and additional mitigation are typical for infrastructure permits. Flag in translations of permit strategies or project timelines.
- **SDE++**: Dutch renewable and carbon reduction subsidy regime. Main support mechanism for renewable power, heat, CCS, hydrogen. Translate as "SDE++" (Stimulering Duurzame Energieproductie en Klimaattransitie) or "renewable energy production subsidy". Administered by RVO.
- **Energiewet (2025)**: New consolidated Dutch Energy Act, merging Electricity Act 1998 and Gas Act. In force 2025. Flag date of translation — Elektriciteitswet 1998 references in older documents should generally be preserved (not replaced with Energiewet).
- **Wcw / Warmtewet**: Heating network regulation. Wet collectieve warmtevoorziening (Wcw) is the proposed successor to the 2014 Warmtewet with broader scope including municipal designation of heat supply areas. Still in legislative process — check in force status at time of translation.
- **Wkb — Wet kwaliteitsborging voor het bouwen**: Came into force 1 January 2024 alongside Omgevingswet. Private quality inspectors now approve building-quality assurance; local authority role reduced. New contractor liability for defects (BW 7:758 amended). Relevant for Wkb-category buildings (currently Gevolgklasse 1).
- **Net op zee**: Offshore grid — TenneT builds and operates offshore transmission for wind parks. Wind park connects at offshore substation; transmission risk allocation is a specific concern in offshore wind PPA and connection contracts.
- **Congestie / congestion**: Dutch grid is severely congested in many regions (particularly Gelderland, Noord-Brabant, Limburg). Large industrial connections often subject to congestion management, time-of-use restrictions, or deferred connections. Flag in translations — timing assumptions may be unreliable.
- **Erfpacht vs opstalrecht**: Two distinct Dutch property rights. Erfpacht (long lease / ground lease, BW 5:85) = right to use and exploit another's land, usually for annual canon. Opstalrecht (right of superficies, BW 5:101) = right to own buildings/works on/under another's land (severs the principle of "vertical accession"). Critical for solar parks, wind farms, cable routes, heating networks. Translate separately.
- **Rijksinpassingsplan (RIP)**: Central-government equivalent of an omgevingsplan — used for national infrastructure (e.g., offshore wind zones, major power lines). Translate as "national coordination plan" or "central government planning decision".
- **Kavelbesluit**: Plot decision for offshore wind — a decision specifying which plot may be developed, with what capacity, and under what conditions. Analogous to a site permit.
- **BRP / programmaverantwoordelijkheid**: BRP (balancing responsible party) carries programme responsibility under Dutch electricity market rules — must submit schedules to TenneT and bears imbalance costs. Preserve BRP acronym; translate programmaverantwoordelijkheid as "programme responsibility".
- **Belgian energy documents**: Belgium's regulatory framework differs — Elia (transmission), federal + regional regulators (CREG federal, VREG Flanders, CWaPE Wallonia, Brugel Brussels). Different support regimes (green certificate systems in Flanders/Wallonia). Flag Belgian context.
