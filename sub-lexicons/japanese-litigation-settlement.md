# Japanese — Litigation, Administrative, Criminal and Settlement Sub-Lexicon

Auto-generated from translation experience. Maps Japanese civil, administrative,
criminal procedural, settlement, and arbitration terms to their correct English
equivalents. Covers the Japanese court hierarchy, the Code of Civil Procedure
(民事訴訟法), the Administrative Case Litigation Act (行政事件訴訟法) and
Administrative Procedure Act (行政手続法), the Code of Criminal Procedure
(刑事訴訟法), the Criminal Code (刑法), the Arbitration Act (仲裁法, UNCITRAL
Model Law-based, 2023 amendments) and the Civil Code (民法) settlement regime.

## Notes for Translators

- **Court hierarchy**: Japan has a unified court system — Summary Courts, District Courts, Family Courts, High Courts, Supreme Court. There is no separate constitutional court — the Supreme Court exercises incidental constitutional review. The IP High Court is a specialised branch of the Tokyo High Court. Translate "最高裁判所" consistently as "Supreme Court", "最高裁" in shortened form as "Supreme Court" (don't invent alternatives).
- **民事訴訟法 (Code of Civil Procedure, 1996)**: Replaced the 1890 statute. Introduced preparatory proceedings, document-production orders, and modern case management. The 2022 amendment introduced remote hearings and IT-ification; further digitisation reforms ongoing through 2025-2026. Flag currency of translation.
- **裁判上の和解 (Judicial Settlement)**: Concluded before a court during pending litigation under CCP Art. 89. Recorded in a settlement record (和解調書), which has the same effect as a final judgment (同一の効力) and is directly enforceable as an executory title. Crucial to preserve this special status in translation.
- **示談 (Jidan)**: Informal out-of-court settlement, culturally very important in Japan, especially in personal injury, traffic accidents, and minor criminal cases (where jidan with the victim may lead to suspension of prosecution). Not a court settlement — not directly enforceable unless made into notarial deed. Translate as "out-of-court settlement" or preserve "jidan" with gloss.
- **清算条項 (Full-and-final settlement clause)**: Critical Japanese settlement drafting convention — parties expressly confirm that apart from obligations recorded in the settlement, they have no mutual claims. Functions as a comprehensive release. Always translate as "full-and-final settlement clause" or "settlement quittance clause"; flag.
- **即決和解 / 起訴前和解 (Pre-filing settlement)**: Summary court procedure under CCP Art. 275 to convert an agreement into an enforceable executory title without filing a full lawsuit. Useful for securing enforceable collection. Translate as "pre-filing settlement" or "sokketsu-wakai" with gloss.
- **控訴 (kōso) vs 上告 (jōkoku)**: Two distinct types of appeal. Kōso (to High Court) reviews facts and law. Jōkoku (to Supreme Court) is limited to constitutional violations and specific legal errors; discretionary acceptance via 上告受理申立て. Translate as "kōso appeal" / "jōkoku appeal" or "appeal to the High Court" / "appeal to the Supreme Court" — never use one English term for both.
- **裁判員制度 (Lay judge system)**: Introduced 2009. Six lay judges + three professional judges hear serious criminal cases (死刑・無期 punishable offences and certain others). Lay judges decide facts AND sentence together with professional judges (unlike US juries). Translate "裁判員" as "lay judge" or "saiban'in"; do NOT use "juror".
- **起訴便宜主義 (Principle of discretionary prosecution)**: Prosecutors may decline to indict even with sufficient evidence, based on offender's age, circumstances, and remedial measures. Combined with high acceptance of 示談, creates high non-prosecution rate. Translate and flag as distinctive.
- **起訴猶予 (Suspension of prosecution)**: Most important prosecutorial diversion tool. Technically case closed; no trial. Different from 執行猶予 (suspended execution of sentence — post-conviction). Flag the distinction.
- **検察審査会 (Committee for the Inquest of Prosecution)**: 11-member citizen panel reviewing non-prosecution decisions. If twice decides by 8+ votes that prosecution should occur (起訴議決), mandatory prosecution through court-appointed prosecutor. Unique mechanism. Translate as "Committee for the Inquest of Prosecution" (officially).
- **合意制度 / 日本型司法取引 (Japanese-style plea bargain, 2018)**: Very limited — only cooperation-based agreements for financial and organised crime; no US-style charge bargaining. Preserve "plea bargaining" label but flag the restrictive scope.
- **行政指導 (Administrative guidance)**: Non-binding but ubiquitous Japanese regulatory tool — authorities "suggest" action outside formal orders. Administrative Procedure Act Chapter IV regulates it. Translate as "administrative guidance"; flag the non-binding (but practically powerful) character.
- **行政処分 vs 行政指導**: Only 行政処分 (administrative dispositions) are judicially reviewable via 取消訴訟. This makes the "処分性" (qualification as disposition) threshold a major litigation battleground. Flag.
- **ADR法 (2004)**: Act on Promotion of Use of Alternative Dispute Resolution, created certification regime for private ADR providers. Certified ADR enjoys limitation-tolling effect. Translate as "ADR Promotion Act" or "Act on Promotion of Use of ADR".
- **労働審判 (Labour Tribunal)**: Post-2006 hybrid procedure for individual labour disputes. One professional judge + two expert members (employer/employee side) in up to three hearings. If parties reject tribunal's decision, case automatically becomes civil litigation. Distinctive feature: labour tribunal decisions gain judgment-equivalent force only if not challenged within 2 weeks. Translate "労働審判" as "labour tribunal procedure"; never "labour arbitration" — it is not arbitration.
- **民事調停 (Civil Conciliation)**: Court-annexed mediation by a committee of one judge and two lay conciliators (調停委員). If successful, 調停調書 (conciliation record) has effect of final judgment. Translate as "civil conciliation"; flag binding effect of resulting record.
- **家事調停 (Family Conciliation)**: Mandatory pre-litigation step for most family disputes under Act on Domestic Relations Case Procedure. Translate as "family conciliation" and flag the调停前置 (conciliation-first) principle.
- **Arbitration Act 2003 / 2023 amendment**: Based on UNCITRAL Model Law (1985). 2023 amendment introduced court-enforceability of tribunal-ordered interim measures (aligning with 2006 Model Law), expanded language flexibility, and Supreme Court/Tokyo & Osaka District Courts centralised jurisdiction. Flag post-2023 changes when translating recent documents.
- **JCAA rules (2021)**: Commercial Arbitration Rules, Interactive Arbitration Rules (with tribunal intervention to narrow issues), Administrative Rules for UNCITRAL Arbitration. Distinguish.
- **JIDRC (Japan International Dispute Resolution Center)**: State-of-the-art hearing facility (Tokyo and Osaka) — not an institution. Proceedings administered by JCAA or other institutions can use JIDRC. Translate as "Japan International Dispute Resolution Center".
- **2020 Civil Code reform (消滅時効)**: Replaced complex period-specific regime with unified subjective-5/objective-10 year framework for contract claims (Art. 166). Older terminology "時効の中断" replaced with "時効の更新". Translate according to applicable Civil Code version; flag the 2020 reform date for documents straddling it.
- **既判力 (Res judicata)**: Civil Code / CCP concept — final judgment, judicial settlement, conciliation record all have 既判力. Translate consistently as "res judicata" and note the same-effect provision for settlements/conciliations.
- **Japanese-English bilingual boilerplate**: Many Japanese settlements are drafted bilingually. Japanese version often controls (日本語正本 / 英語参考訳). Flag which version controls — important legal consequence.
- **Notarial deed (公正証書)**: Not strictly a court settlement but a notarial instrument that can be executory. Often used for structured payments. If agreement includes 強制執行認諾文言 (acceptance of compulsory execution clause), it is directly enforceable for money claims. Translate "公正証書" as "notarial deed" and flag executory status.
- **国家賠償法 (State Redress Act)**: Separate statute for tortious liability of State/public bodies. Translate as "State Redress Act" (not "State Compensation Act"; the official rendering is "State Redress").
- **Typographic conventions**: Japanese legal documents use 甲 (kō) / 乙 (otsu) / 丙 (hei) for Party A / Party B / Party C. Translate as "Party A / B / C" in English or preserve with gloss on first use.
- **Supreme Court case citation**: Use format 最判 [date] 民集 [vol.-no.-page] for Supreme Court civil judgment; 最判 = Supreme Court judgment; 最決 = Supreme Court order. Preserve Japanese abbreviations when citing.
