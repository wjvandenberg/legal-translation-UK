# Portuguese — NDAs and Service Agreements Sub-Lexicon

Maps Portuguese confidentiality and services vocabulary to correct English equivalents.
Covers acordos de confidencialidade / não divulgação, and contratos de prestação de
serviços, SLAs, outsourcing, consultoria e serviços geridos.

See also `portuguese-corporate-ma-jv.md` and `portuguese-ip-it-technology.md`.

## Notes

- **No specific NDA legislation in Portugal**: NDAs are governed by general contract law
  (Código Civil) and the duty of good faith (Art. 227 CC — culpa in contrahendo). Trade
  secrets are protected under the Industrial Property Code (Arts. 313–315 CPI),
  implementing EU Directive 2016/943.
- **"Providência cautelar"**: Portuguese urgent interim relief (Arts. 362–376 CPC). For
  NDA breaches, "providência cautelar não especificada" may be sought. Translate as
  "injunctive relief" or "interim measures".
- **"Cláusula penal"** (Arts. 810–812 CC): Contractual penalties are enforceable but may
  be judicially reduced if manifestly excessive (Art. 812 CC). In English translation
  render as "liquidated damages" in commercial context and "service credits" in SLAs
  (avoid "penalty" as unenforceable under English law).
- **RGPD / GDPR**: Where the information includes personal data, the NDA and services
  contract must comply with the GDPR (RGPD). Flag any agreement that lacks a data-
  protection carve-out or a proper DPA when personal data is involved.
- **"Segredo de negócio"**: Three-part statutory test — secrecy, commercial value,
  reasonable steps — mirrors the EU Directive.
- **"Obrigação de meios" vs "obrigação de resultado"**: Civil-law distinction relevant
  to both the standard of care under an NDA and the nature of the provider's obligations
  under a services contract. Translate with context as appropriate.
- **"Prestação de serviços" (Arts. 1154–1156 CC)**: The Portuguese Civil Code framework
  for services contracts, supplemented by mandate (mandato) rules where the provider
  acts on behalf of the client.
- **"Resolução" vs "denúncia" vs "rescisão"**: "Resolução" = termination for breach /
  rescission; "denúncia" = unilateral termination (notice of non-renewal or termination
  for convenience depending on context); "rescisão" often used broadly for termination
  in labour law. Translate precisely.
