# French — Transport and Insurance Sub-Lexicon

Combines French-language transport and insurance vocabulary. Part 1 covers maritime, road, rail, air and multimodal transport: carriage contracts, bills of lading, charterparties, freight forwarding, carrier liability. Part 2 covers insurance contracts, life and non-life lines, reinsurance, brokers, claims handling and regulatory framework. Marine/transport insurance (cargo, hull, P&I, carrier liability) sits at the intersection.

## Notes

- **"Surestaries" (demurrage)**: Compensation payable by the charterer for exceeding
  laytime. Always "demurrage" in English, never "demurrage penalties".
- **"Avarie commune" (general average)**: A fundamental maritime law concept (York-Antwerp
  Rules). All parties share losses from voluntary sacrifice. Keep as "general average".
- **"Connaissement"**: Always "bill of lading" — the fundamental maritime transport document.
  A "lettre de transport maritime" (sea waybill) is NOT a bill of lading — it is not
  negotiable and does not represent title to goods.
- **French maritime law**: Primarily governed by the Code des transports (2010) and
  international conventions. Reference as "French Transport Code" on first mention.

## Notes

- **"Franchise"**: Context-dependent. In most insurance contexts, translate as "deductible"
  or "excess" (UK). A "franchise absolue" is a standard deductible. A "franchise relative"
  is a threshold — the insurer pays the full loss if it exceeds the franchise, but nothing
  if below. Keep the distinction clear.
- **"Assurance dommages-ouvrage"**: A specifically French mandatory insurance (Loi Spinetta,
  1978) that provides first-party cover for structural defects. No direct common law
  equivalent. Translate as "inherent defects insurance" (IDI) with a context note.
- **"Déchéance de garantie"**: Loss of the right to claim under the policy due to the
  insured's breach of a policy condition. Translate as "forfeiture of cover", not
  "cancellation" (which is prospective, not retrospective).
- **Lloyd's market terminology**: When the insurance document references the London market,
  use Lloyd's-specific terms: "slip" (not "proposal"), "placing" (not "subscription"),
  "leading underwriter" (not "lead insurer").
