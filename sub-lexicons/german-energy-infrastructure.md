## Notes

- **"Vertragsstrafe"**: Under German law (§§ 339-345 BGB), a Vertragsstrafe is a
  contractual penalty that can be reduced by the court if disproportionate (§ 343 BGB).
  For B2B contracts, this judicial reduction right can be excluded. Translate as
  "liquidated damages" (LDs), not "penalties".
- **"Abnahme" (§ 640 BGB)**: Formal acceptance of works. Under German construction law
  (VOB/B), acceptance is a critical milestone — it triggers the start of the warranty
  period, shifts the burden of proof for defects, and makes payment due. Translate as
  "acceptance" with context note.
- **VOB/B**: The German construction contract procedures (Vergabe- und Vertragsordnung
  für Bauleistungen, Teil B). Not a statute but a set of standard terms widely
  incorporated by reference. Translate as "German Construction Contract Procedures (VOB/B)".
- **HOAI**: The German fee schedule for architects and engineers. Keep abbreviation
  and translate on first mention.

- **EEG (Erneuerbare-Energien-Gesetz)**: The cornerstone of German renewable energy law.
  Provides the framework for feed-in tariffs and market premiums. Reference as
  "Renewable Energy Sources Act (EEG)" on first mention.
- **BNetzA (Bundesnetzagentur)**: The German federal energy regulator. Keep abbreviation
  and translate on first mention.
- **"BImSchG-Genehmigung"**: The permit required under the Federal Emissions Control Act
  (Bundes-Immissionsschutzgesetz) for wind and other energy installations. Translate as
  "permit under the Federal Emissions Control Act" or "BImSchG permit".
- **"Heimfall"**: The reversion of assets to the public authority at the end of a
  concession. Translate as "reversion" or "handback of assets".
