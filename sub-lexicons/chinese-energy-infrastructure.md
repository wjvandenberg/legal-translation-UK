## Notes

- **PRC Civil Code — Construction Contracts**: Book III, Part II, Chapter 18 of the PRC
  Civil Code governs construction contracts (建设工程合同). Key provisions address
  contractor qualifications, mandatory bidding, and payment of worker wages.
- **"竣工验收" (completion acceptance)**: Under PRC law, the employer must organise
  acceptance within a reasonable time after the contractor gives completion notice. If the
  employer fails to do so, the deemed completion date applies (Art. 799 Civil Code).
- **"质保金" (retention)**: Standard retention is typically 3-5% of the contract price,
  held during the defects liability period. Under the 2017 State Council guidance, the
  maximum retention is capped at 3%.
- **FIDIC in China**: FIDIC contracts are widely used for international and large-scale
  PRC projects, particularly those funded by international financial institutions. PRC
  courts may override FIDIC terms with mandatory PRC law provisions.
- **Mandatory bidding**: Certain construction projects must be procured through competitive
  bidding under the PRC Bidding Law (招标投标法). Non-compliance may render the contract void.
- **"监理" (supervision)**: PRC law requires independent site supervision (工程监理) for
  certain categories of projects. The supervisor is an independent third party, distinct
  from the FIDIC "Engineer". Translate as "site supervisor" or "supervising engineer".

- **PRC Renewable Energy Law (可再生能源法)**: The foundational legislation for renewable
  energy in China, first enacted in 2005 and revised in 2009. Provides the framework for
  feed-in tariffs, grid connection obligations, and renewable energy targets. Reference as
  "Renewable Energy Law" on first mention.
- **NEA (国家能源局)**: The National Energy Administration — the primary energy regulator
  under the NDRC. Reference as "National Energy Administration (NEA)" on first mention.
- **"双碳目标" (Dual Carbon Goals)**: China's commitment to peak carbon emissions by 2030
  and achieve carbon neutrality by 2060. A key policy driver for energy sector translation.
  Translate as "dual carbon goals" or "carbon peak and carbon neutrality targets".
- **Grid companies**: China's electricity grid is operated by two state-owned entities —
  State Grid Corporation (covering most of China) and China Southern Power Grid (covering
  five southern provinces). Both are counterparties to PPAs and grid connection agreements.
- **"竞价上网" / "平价上网"**: The transition from fixed feed-in tariffs to competitive
  bidding (竞价上网) and grid parity (平价上网) — where renewables compete without subsidies.
  Flag the applicable regime when translating PPA pricing provisions.
- **PPP regulations**: PRC PPP is primarily governed by NDRC and Ministry of Finance
  guidelines rather than a single statute. The regulatory framework has undergone
  significant tightening since 2023. Flag the applicable regulatory regime.
