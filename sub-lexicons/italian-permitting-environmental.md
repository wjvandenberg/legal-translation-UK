# Italian — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps Italian environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers environmental impact assessment, integrated permits, water,
waste, nature, chemicals, contaminated sites, climate, and construction/zoning.

## Notes

- **D.Lgs. 152/2006 (Codice dell'ambiente)**: The central environmental statute.
  Part II covers VIA/VAS/AIA, Part III water, Part IV waste and contaminated
  sites, Part V air, Part VI environmental damage. Heavily amended — verify
  current consolidated version at translation date. Note especially the 2021
  reforms implementing the Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) streamlining
  authorisation procedures.
- **PNRR (Piano Nazionale di Ripresa e Resilienza)**: Italy's NRRP has
  accelerated environmental permitting and created dedicated "fast-track"
  procedures for green investments. Commissione Tecnica PNRR-PNIEC reviews
  strategic projects. Flag in renewable-energy and hydrogen project permitting.
- **VIA vs VAS vs VInCA**: VIA is project-level EIA; VAS is plan/programme SEA;
  VInCA is Natura 2000 appropriate assessment. All three may apply to a large
  infrastructure project. Competent authority is MASE (state-level projects) or
  Region (regional projects).
- **AIA vs AUA**: AIA (IED installations) is the comprehensive integrated
  permit; AUA is a simplified single authorisation for SMEs consolidating up to
  seven separate permits (air emissions, small discharges, small-scale waste).
  Do not confuse in translation.
- **Siti di Interesse Nazionale (SIN)**: Heavily polluted industrial sites
  managed directly by MASE (not regions). Famous examples: Porto Marghera,
  Taranto-Ilva, Priolo, Gela, Brescia Caffaro. Specialised remediation regime
  with national oversight.
- **CSC / CSR**: Contamination threshold concentrations (CSC) trigger site
  characterisation; risk threshold concentrations (CSR) are site-specific
  levels derived from risk analysis that must be achieved by remediation. CSC
  values are listed in Part IV, Annex 5. Critical translation distinction.
- **RENTRi (Registro Elettronico Nazionale per la Tracciabilità dei Rifiuti)**:
  New electronic waste register replacing paper registers and MUD gradually
  from 2024–2026. Implementation phased by producer size and waste type.
- **Legge 68/2015 (ecoreati)**: Introduced Articles 452-bis et seq. of the
  Criminal Code criminalising serious environmental offences — "inquinamento
  ambientale" (pollution), "disastro ambientale" (disaster), "traffico
  organizzato di rifiuti" (organised waste trafficking). Significant increase
  in criminal penalties; also extends D.Lgs. 231/2001 (corporate liability) to
  these offences. Flag carefully in compliance translations.
- **Conferenza di servizi**: Procedural mechanism consolidating multiple
  authorisations into a single decision-making forum. Two main variants:
  istruttoria (simplified) and decisoria (with binding effect). Widely used in
  permit translations involving multiple authorities.
- **Autorizzazione paesaggistica**: Required for works in areas subject to
  landscape restrictions (vincolo paesaggistico) under D.Lgs. 42/2004.
  Simplified regime (autorizzazione paesaggistica semplificata) for minor works
  under D.P.R. 31/2017. Frequently encountered in real-estate and renewable-
  energy translations.
- **Aarhus Convention — Italian implementation**: Information access under
  D.Lgs. 195/2005; participation and justice integrated in Codice ambiente.
  Recognised environmental associations (ex art. 13 L. 349/1986) have standing
  before administrative courts. TAR Lazio and Consiglio di Stato are
  particularly active in environmental judicial review.
- **Cross-references**: See `italian-real-estate.md` for building permits and
  zoning; `italian-energy-infrastructure.md` for renewables permitting and EU
  ETS; `italian-corporate-ma-jv.md` for environmental reps and warranties;
  `italian-litigation-settlement.md` for administrative-court procedure.
