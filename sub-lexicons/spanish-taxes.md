# Spanish — Taxes Sub-Lexicon

Spanish tax-law terminology mapped to the neutral English equivalents in
`references/taxes.md`. Spanish tax law is governed primarily by the Ley General
Tributaria (LGT, Law 58/2003), the Corporate Income Tax Act (Ley 27/2014, LIS),
the Personal Income Tax Act (Ley 35/2006, LIRPF), the VAT Act (Ley 37/1992, LIVA),
and a dense body of regional tax law (Basque Country and Navarre have their own
tax regimes under the *concierto / convenio económico*). For corporate / M&A
context use alongside `spanish-corporate-ma-jv.md`.

## 15. Notes — Translator Guidance

- **IS vs "corporate tax"**. Impuesto sobre Sociedades (currently 25%; 15% for newly-
  created entities and certain startups) is Spain's corporate income tax. Translate
  as "corporate income tax (IS)" on first mention — not "corporation tax" (UK-only).
- **IRPF — regional component.** Personal income tax has a state component and an
  autonomous community component. The regional share varies materially (Madrid is
  lower; Catalonia higher). When translating individual-tax documents, flag the
  relevant region to the reader.
- **IRNR vs IS / IRPF.** Non-residents pay IRNR (Impuesto sobre la Renta de no
  Residentes, Law 5/2004). IRNR applies to both individuals and companies and is
  separate from IS / IRPF. Translate as "non-resident income tax (IRNR)" and always
  identify whether the taxpayer is resident or not.
- **Basque Country and Navarre have their own tax regimes** under the *concierto
  económico* (Basque) and *convenio económico* (Navarre). The foral tax laws mirror
  state law but can diverge on rates, deductions and procedures. Translate the
  institutional names as "Foral Tax Authority" and the instruments as "foral tax
  laws". Do not assume state rules apply.
- **ITP-AJD — three modalities.** ITP y AJD is an umbrella for three different
  taxes: (i) *Transmisiones Patrimoniales Onerosas* (TPO — on non-business
  transfers of assets); (ii) *Operaciones Societarias* (OS — on certain corporate
  transactions, mostly exempt today); (iii) *Actos Jurídicos Documentados* (AJD —
  stamp duty on notarial, commercial and administrative documents). Always specify
  which modality is in play.
- **Plusvalía municipal (IIVTNU).** The municipal tax on the increase in urban land
  value has been legislatively rebuilt following the 2021 Constitutional Court
  ruling (STC 182/2021). Always flag the 2021 / 2022 regime change when translating
  real-estate transaction documents referencing this tax.
- **Retención a cuenta vs retención liberatoria.** Critical distinction. *Retención
  a cuenta* (withholding on account) is creditable against the recipient's final
  tax. *Retención liberatoria* (final withholding) discharges the recipient's
  liability. The Spanish domestic system is predominantly "on account"; treaty or
  IRNR withholding can be final. Translate accordingly.
- **Prescripción tributaria (4 years).** The general tax prescription period under
  Art. 66 LGT is 4 years (for assessment, refund, collection, and penalty). Do not
  confuse with civil limitation (typically longer). Translate as "tax prescription
  (4-year period)" with a translator's note on first use.
- **Consulta vinculante.** Binding tax rulings issued by the Dirección General de
  Tributos (DGT) bind the administration (but not the taxpayer) in identical facts.
  They are persuasive authority in litigation. Translate as "binding tax ruling"
  and identify the DGT as the issuing body.
- **Operaciones vinculadas / precios de transferencia.** Article 18 LIS contains
  Spain's transfer pricing rules. Documentation thresholds and penalty-protection
  rules align broadly with the OECD standard, with Spanish specifics (country-by-
  country reporting threshold, master file / local file content). Translate
  functionally and always identify Art. 18 LIS on first mention.
- **Régimen especial de fusiones (Chapter VII LIS).** The Spanish tax-neutrality
  regime for mergers, demergers, contributions and share-for-share exchanges. Key
  requirements: (i) valid economic motive (*motivo económico válido*), (ii) proper
  accounting treatment, (iii) notice to tax authority. If the regime applies, the
  transaction is tax-deferred (carry-over of tax values). Translate as "tax-
  neutrality regime" or "special merger regime" with a translator's note.
- **ETVE (Entidades de Tenencia de Valores Extranjeros).** Spanish international
  holding regime allowing full exemption of qualifying foreign dividends and
  capital gains (participation exemption applies separately under Art. 21 LIS).
  Translate as "Spanish international holding regime (ETVE)".
- **SOCIMI.** Spanish REIT regime, roughly equivalent to US REITs and UK REITs,
  with pass-through economics through mandatory dividend distributions. Translate
  as "Spanish REIT regime (SOCIMI)".
- **Beckham regime (Art. 93 LIRPF).** Optional flat-rate IRPF regime for inbound
  workers moving to Spain: first year plus five following years at 24% flat rate
  (47% above €600k for employment income). 2023 reform extended eligibility to
  digital nomads and certain executives. Translate as "inbound expatriate regime"
  or "Beckham regime" with parenthetical explanation.
- **Impuesto Temporal de Solidaridad de las Grandes Fortunas.** Introduced in
  late 2022 as a temporary (now extended) top-up to the wealth tax for net wealth
  above €3m, intended to harmonise the wealth tax burden across autonomous
  communities (since Madrid and Andalusia bonify IP to 100%). Translate as
  "temporary solidarity tax on large fortunes".
- **Suministro Inmediato de Información (SII).** Since 2017, large taxpayers and
  VAT-group members must submit VAT invoice data in near real-time (4 working
  days) through the AEAT portal. Translate as "Immediate Supply of Information
  (SII)" — keep the Spanish acronym on first mention.
- **Delito fiscal (Art. 305 CP).** Criminal tax offence triggered where the
  defrauded tax quota exceeds €120,000 per tax and tax period (or €600,000 for
  aggravated forms). Voluntary regularisation before formal notification
  extinguishes criminal liability. Translate as "criminal tax offence" or "tax
  fraud offence".
- **Pillar Two implementation.** Spain implemented Pillar Two by Law 7/2024,
  effective for fiscal years starting on or after 31 December 2023. The
  *impuesto complementario* has three modalities matching the OECD GloBE rules
  (IIR, UTPR, QDMTT). Translate as "top-up tax" and use the OECD English
  abbreviations (IIR, UTPR, QDMTT).
- **Currency / number formats**. Spanish (as most Romance languages) uses comma as
  decimal separator and dot as thousands separator (1.234,56 €). Convert to UK
  English style unless the user specifies otherwise. Always preserve the currency
  symbol and exact figure.
