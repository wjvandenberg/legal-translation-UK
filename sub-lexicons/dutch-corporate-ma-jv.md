mergers and acquisitions, joint ventures, shareholders' agreements and related collateral,

## 23. Notes

- **Aandelenoverdrachtsakte**: Share transfers in a Dutch BV must be executed before a
  Dutch civil-law notary — a hard statutory requirement, not a mere formality. Translate
  as "deed of transfer of shares" and flag the notarial requirement. NV listed-share
  transfers go through Euroclear Nederland and do not require a notarial deed.
- **STAK / certificering**: A Dutch foundation (stichting administratiekantoor) holds
  legal title to shares and issues depositary receipts (certificaten) to economic owners.
  Used for employee participation, family wealth and control locks. Translate STAK as
  "trust office foundation" with gloss; certificaten as "depositary receipts".
- **Prioriteitsaandelen**: Distinct from preferente aandelen. Carry special governance
  rights (board nomination, veto). Translate as "priority shares" with gloss — there is
  no direct English equivalent.
- **Statutair bestuurder vs titulair directeur**: Critical distinction. Statutair
  bestuurder = formally appointed by general meeting, registered at KvK, bears full
  director's duties and BW 2:9 / 2:138/248 liability. Titulair directeur = employee with
  a "director" title but no corporate office. Never translate both as "director" without
  qualifying.
- **Commissaris**: Member of the supervisory board. Translate as "supervisory director"
  or "non-executive director" — never "commissioner" (too political/civilian in English).
- **Besluit**: Generic for "resolution" or "decision". In corporate-law context use
  "resolution"; in administrative/regulatory context "decision" may be appropriate.
- **Schriftelijk besluit / besluit buiten vergadering**: Written resolution adopted
  outside a formal meeting. Requires unanimity of all vote-entitled persons (BW 2:238
  for BV, BW 2:128 for NV). Always note the unanimity requirement.
- **Nietig vs vernietigbaar besluit**: Nietig = null and void ab initio (BW 2:14;
  contrary to mandatory law or articles). Vernietigbaar = voidable on challenge (BW 2:15;
  procedural defects). Different remedies and limitation periods. Do not translate both
  as "invalid".
- **Kwijting / decharge**: Discharge of directors and supervisory directors from
  liability for the past financial year. Granted by the general meeting (usually at the
  same time as adoption of annual accounts). Only covers matters disclosed or reasonably
  known. A foundational Dutch governance concept — translate as "discharge".
- **Tegenstrijdig belang**: Conflict-of-interest rule. A director with a direct or
  indirect personal interest conflicting with the company's interest cannot participate
  in deliberation or decision. The decision then passes to the supervisory board or
  general meeting. Since 2013 reform, contracts entered under conflict are not voidable
  — it is now an internal governance rule only.
- **Kennelijk onbehoorlijk bestuur**: Trigger under BW 2:138 / 2:248 for personal
  director liability for unpaid deficits in bankruptcy. "Manifestly improper management"
  is the standard translation; requires identifiable failings (no timely accounts, no
  proper records etc.), not mere business-judgment errors.
- **Persoonlijk ernstig verwijt**: "Personal serious reproach" — the BW 2:9 standard for
  director's personal liability vis-à-vis the company. Higher than ordinary negligence.
- **Enquêterecht / Ondernemingskamer**: Unique Dutch shareholder remedy. Minority
  shareholders meeting threshold requirements (typically 10% or EUR 225k of issued
  capital) can petition the Enterprise Chamber for an inquiry. If wanbeleid
  (mismanagement) is found, the Chamber can annul resolutions, suspend or dismiss
  directors, set aside share transfers, and appoint a trustee.
- **Geschillenregeling (BW 2:335-ff)**: Statutory dispute resolution — a shareholder who
  harms the company's interest can be compelled to transfer shares to fellow shareholders.
  No common-law equivalent. Translate as "statutory dispute resolution procedure" with
  gloss.
- **Vifo Act**: Dutch FDI screening regime (Wet veiligheidstoets investeringen, fusies en
  overnames) in force since 1 June 2023, with retroactive effect to 8 September 2020 for
  certain transactions. Reviewed by BTI (Bureau Toetsing Investeringen). Sectoral FDI
  regimes (energy, telecoms etc.) continue to apply in parallel.
- **Balance sheet and distribution test**: Post-2012 BV regime — distributions are
  approved by the management board (not the notary) based on a balance-sheet test (net
  equity above statutory reserves) AND a liquidity/solvency outlook test. Directors are
  personally liable if a distribution is made when they knew or should have known the
  company would be unable to continue paying its debts.
- **Juridische fusie / splitsing**: A statutory merger (BW 2:309) or division (BW 2:334a)
  effects universal succession — assets and liabilities transfer by operation of law.
  Distinct from an asset deal.
- **Volmacht under Dutch law (BW 3:60-79)**: Civil-law POA. Distinguish lastgeving
  (mandate, BW 7:414 — the underlying contractual relationship) from volmacht (the
  authority to bind the principal vis-à-vis third parties). Both can subsist
  independently. BW 3:68 prohibits self-dealing unless expressly authorised.
- **Levenstestament**: Dutch private-client instrument combining a continuing/lifetime
  POA with practical care and end-of-life directions. Distinct from a wilsbeschikking
  (testamentary disposition). Translate as "living will" or "lifetime POA" with gloss —
  there is no direct English equivalent.
- **Belgian Dutch documents**: Belgian deals use similar Dutch terminology but operate
  under the Belgian Code of Companies and Associations (WVV/CSA). Belgian BV
  (previously BVBA) is distinct from Dutch BV; no Enterprise Chamber equivalent. Always
  flag Belgian provenance for cross-check.
