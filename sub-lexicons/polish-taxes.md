and the Ordynacja podatkowa framework.

## Notes

- **Ordynacja podatkowa (Tax Ordinance)**: The Act of 29 August 1997 governs general tax
  law, tax procedure, and enforcement. It is the procedural counterpart to the material tax
  statutes. Cite as "Tax Ordinance" or "o.p." on first mention.
- **CIT rate structure**: Standard rate 19%. Reduced rate 9% for small taxpayers
  (revenues below EUR 2 million) and start-ups. Minimum income tax of 10% applies (from
  2024) to companies with low profitability. Flag when translating annual returns.
- **Estonian CIT (ryczałt od dochodów spółek)**: A lump-sum corporate tax regime effective
  since 2021, modelled on the Estonian system. Tax is deferred until profits are
  distributed. Only certain Polish taxpayers (primarily closely-held sp. z o.o. / S.A.)
  qualify. Flag when translating — the mechanics differ from ordinary CIT.
- **VAT rates**: Standard 23%, reduced 8% (certain services, construction for housing),
  reduced 5% (some food, books), 0% (exports, intra-community supplies). Rates may be
  adjusted temporarily — check current rates when translating.
- **KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur)**: Mandatory e-invoicing system. Mandatory roll-out
  dates have shifted multiple times. Verify current obligation dates when translating
  invoicing clauses.
- **Withholding tax "pay-and-refund"**: For payments to related parties exceeding PLN 2
  million per year, Polish payers must withhold WHT at statutory rates (19%/20%) and the
  payee must apply for refund. Treaty benefits or exemptions can be applied at source via
  WH-OSC statement or Head of KAS opinion. Explain in translator's note if the concept is
  unfamiliar to the target audience.
- **Transfer pricing thresholds**: Documentation thresholds are expressed as transaction
  values (e.g., PLN 10 million for goods, PLN 2 million for services). Related-party
  transactions with entities in "harmful tax jurisdictions" have lower thresholds
  (PLN 100,000) and more detailed requirements.
- **Art. 116 o.p. — board member liability**: Polish Tax Ordinance art. 116 imposes joint
  and several personal liability on management board members for tax arrears of the
  company if enforcement against the company is ineffective. Defence: timely bankruptcy
  filing. Key risk in Polish M&A and insolvency practice — flag consistently.
- **Solidarity levy (danina solidarnościowa)**: 4% additional PIT on individuals with
  annual income over PLN 1 million. Distinguish from ordinary PIT progressive scale.
- **KAS (Krajowa Administracja Skarbowa)**: Consolidated tax administration since 2017,
  comprising tax offices, customs offices, and tax audit offices. Reference as "National
  Revenue Administration" or "KAS" on first mention.
