# German — Permitting and Environmental Law Sub-Lexicon

Maps German environmental and permitting vocabulary to correct English
equivalents. Covers EIA, immission control (BImSchG), water, waste, nature,
chemicals, contaminated sites, climate, and construction/zoning.

## Notes

- **BImSchG as integrated permit**: The BImSchG permit operates as the
  integrated environmental permit for industrial installations. Installations
  listed in the 4. BImSchV require either a formal procedure (with public
  participation) or a simplified procedure depending on the column assignment.
  Permits cover air emissions, noise, waste, and water-discharge aspects are
  addressed in a concentrated procedure.
- **Planfeststellung**: Integrated multi-authorisation procedure for
  infrastructure projects (railways, motorways, waterways, airports, pipelines,
  high-voltage lines, nuclear). Results in a "Planfeststellungsbeschluss" with
  "Konzentrationswirkung" — absorbs all other required permits. Critical
  translation concept in infrastructure work.
- **Länder implementation**: Environmental law is federal in substance but
  administered by the Länder (federal states). Each Land has its own
  environmental ministry and agencies. Permits are typically issued by Land
  authorities. Verify the applicable Land for the project — Bavaria,
  North Rhine-Westphalia, Hamburg, etc. each have local administrative
  practice.
- **KrWG waste hierarchy and 5-step approach**: Prevention > re-use >
  recycling > other recovery > disposal. The 2020 amendment tightened end-of-
  waste criteria and clarified by-product status. Verpackungsgesetz (2019,
  extensively amended 2021 and 2023) implements the EU single-use plastics
  directive.
- **BBodSchG and Altlasten**: Federal Soil Protection Act plus BBodSchV
  implementing ordinance. Länder maintain Altlastenkataster (contaminated-site
  registers) with three categories: A (investigation needed), B (detailed
  investigation), C (remediation). Strict liability falls on "Verursacher"
  (polluter) and "Zustandsstörer" (current owner/occupier). Landmark BVerfG
  ruling (2000) capped Zustandsstörer liability at property value in certain
  cases.
- **USchadG (ELD transposition)**: Implements Directive 2004/35/EC. Strict
  liability for Annex III activities; fault-based liability for other
  operators causing biodiversity damage. Obligation to notify competent
  authority of imminent threat; remediation ordered by authority.
- **BEHG — national ETS**: National emissions trading system covering heating
  fuels and road transport fuels not covered by EU ETS. Fixed-price phase
  2021–2025; auction from 2026. Will be replaced or complemented by EU ETS 2
  (fuels for road transport and buildings) from 2027.
- **KSG — Federal Climate Protection Act**: Sets binding sectoral emissions
  budgets through 2030; net-zero by 2045 (earlier than EU-wide 2050). Famous
  BVerfG ruling of 24 March 2021 (1 BvR 2656/18) mandated stronger post-2030
  commitments — widely cited in climate-litigation translations.
- **Umwelt-Rechtsbehelfsgesetz (UmwRG)**: Implements the Aarhus Convention's
  access-to-justice pillar. Anerkannte Umweltvereinigungen (recognised
  environmental associations) have broad standing to challenge environmental
  decisions without showing personal injury. Post-2017 amendments significantly
  broadened scope following CJEU decisions.
- **Artenschutzrechtliche Prüfung (saP)**: Mandatory species-protection
  assessment for projects affecting protected species under § 44 BNatSchG.
  Particularly critical for wind-farm and infrastructure permits. § 45
  provides derogation regime — subject of extensive case law.
