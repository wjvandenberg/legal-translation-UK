## Notes

- **"Cautionnement"** (Art. 2288 Code civil): Personal guarantee where the guarantor
  undertakes to pay the creditor if the debtor defaults. Translate as "surety" or
  "guarantee" depending on context.
- **"Fiducie-sûreté"**: A trust-based security mechanism introduced in 2007. No direct
  English equivalent — translate as "trust by way of security" with a note if needed.
- **"Privilège de prêteur de deniers"**: A specific French lien on real property granted
  to the lender who financed the purchase. Translate as "lender's lien" with context note.
- **LMA standard**: English finance documents follow Loan Market Association conventions.
  Align terminology with LMA standards where possible.

- **ACPR**: The Autorité de contrôle prudentiel et de résolution is the French
  banking supervisor (under the Banque de France). Keep the abbreviation "ACPR"
  and translate on first mention.
- **"Établissement de crédit"**: The regulated entity category for banks under French
  and EU law. Always translate as "credit institution" in regulatory contexts.
- **"Déclaration de soupçon"**: The French SAR/STR, filed with Tracfin. Translate as
  "suspicious activity report" (SAR) in UK context or "suspicious transaction report"
  (STR) in EU/international context.
- **LMA alignment**: For syndicated lending terms, align with Loan Market Association
  (LMA) conventions — "facility agent" (not "administrative agent"), "majority lenders"
  (not "required lenders", which is US/LSTA).

## Notes

- **FBF vs ISDA**: The Fédération Bancaire Française (FBF) publishes its own master
  agreement for derivatives, which is used alongside or instead of the ISDA Master
  Agreement in French domestic transactions. Preserve the reference to the correct
  master agreement.
- **"Compensation avec déchéance du terme"**: The French concept of close-out netting,
  codified in the Code monétaire et financier. Translate as "close-out netting".
- **"Pension livrée"**: The French term for repo (repurchase agreement). Always translate
  as "repo" or "repurchase agreement" in market-facing documents.
- **AMF**: The Autorité des marchés financiers is the French securities regulator.
  Keep the abbreviation "AMF" and translate on first mention.
