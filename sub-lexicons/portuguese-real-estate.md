## Notes

- **"Escritura pública"**: Until 2008, most real estate transfers in Portugal required a
  notarial deed (escritura pública). Since DL 116/2008, a simpler procedure at the land
  registry (Documento Particular Autenticado — DPA) is an alternative. Both are valid.
  Translate as "public deed" or "notarial deed" for escritura; "authenticated private
  document" for DPA.
- **"Contrato-promessa"**: The preliminary agreement (promise to purchase/sell) is a
  binding contract under Portuguese law (Art. 410 CC). A deposit (sinal) of twice the
  amount may be forfeited or required to be returned doubled if either party defaults
  (Art. 442 CC). Translate as "preliminary agreement" with note.
- **"Propriedade horizontal"**: The Portuguese condominium regime (Arts. 1414-1438-A CC).
  Each unit (fração autónoma) is independently owned while common areas are jointly owned.
  Translate as "condominium" or "horizontal property regime".
- **"Direito de superfície"**: A right to build on or maintain a structure on another's
  land (Arts. 1524-1542 CC). Similar to a long leasehold in common law. Translate as
  "right of superficies" or "building right".
- **IMT and IMI**: IMT is the one-off transfer tax; IMI is the annual municipal property
  tax. Both are calculated on the "valor patrimonial tributário" (tax-assessed value).
  Keep Portuguese abbreviations and translate on first mention.
- **"Trespasse"**: The transfer of a commercial establishment including its lease — a
  specifically Portuguese concept (Art. 1112 CC). The tenant can transfer the lease
  together with the business without the landlord's consent. Translate as "business
  transfer" or "transfer of commercial lease" with explanation.
