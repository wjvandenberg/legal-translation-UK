# Polish — Energy and Infrastructure Sub-Lexicon

Maps Polish energy and infrastructure vocabulary to correct English equivalents.
Covers the electricity, gas, heat and renewable sectors, O&G, regulatory framework
(URE), network access, PPAs, capacity market and public-investment projects.

## Notes

- **Prawo energetyczne**: Act of 10 April 1997. Foundational energy statute governing
  generation, transmission, distribution and supply of electricity, gas, heat and liquid
  fuels. Extensively amended to transpose EU electricity and gas packages. Establishes
  the licensing regime (koncesja).
- **URE (Urząd Regulacji Energetyki)**: Independent regulator. Competencies include
  granting licences, approving tariffs, designating suppliers of last resort, overseeing
  unbundling, and imposing penalties. Independent appeal to the Court of Competition and
  Consumer Protection (SOKiK). Reference as "Energy Regulatory Office" on first mention.
- **Tariff approval**: Tariffs of regulated services (transmission, distribution,
  district heating) must be approved by the URE President. For retail electricity and
  gas supply to households, tariff approval applies — subject to phased liberalisation.
  Commercial customers typically operate under freely-negotiated prices.
- **Capacity market (rynek mocy)**: Established in 2018. Capacity auctions (main, T-5
  and T-1) award capacity agreements. Providers earn capacity payments for making
  capacity available; subject to availability obligations and penalties for
  underperformance. Significant element of Polish electricity market design.
- **RES support schemes**: Evolution — (1) green certificates (2005–2016) + (2) RES
  auctions (since 2016) + (3) FiT / FiP for micro and small installations. The net-billing
  regime for prosumers entered into force 1 April 2022, replacing earlier net-metering.
- **"Ustawa odległościowa" (Distance Act)**: Act of 20 May 2016 imposes a minimum
  distance (10x turbine height) between onshore wind turbines and residential buildings
  and protected areas. Significantly constrained Polish onshore wind development.
  Amendments in 2023 allowed 700 m distance under certain conditions. Watch for further
  legislative changes.
- **Offshore Wind Act**: Act of 17 December 2020. Provides dedicated support framework
  for Polish Baltic offshore wind: first phase via government-granted CfDs; subsequent
  phases via auctions. Permitting involves multiple decisions (PSZW, connection, EIA)
  and a multi-year development cycle.
- **Corporate PPAs**: Growing Polish market for corporate PPAs, both physical and
  virtual (synthetic/CfD-based). Structural considerations include balancing
  responsibility, regulatory consents for delivery across DSO, and treatment of
  guarantees of origin. Flag when translating commercial structures.
- **EU ETS — Polish specifics**: Polish power sector has been a major EU ETS participant.
  Polish installations historically received substantial free allocations that are being
  phased down. CBAM and Fit for 55 package are reshaping the framework — expect
  substantial new legislation.
- **"Decyzja ZRID" (road investment decision)**: Under the Road Investment Special Act,
  a single administrative decision combines land-use, construction and expropriation
  authorisations for national road projects. Similar specustawa regimes exist for
  railways, airports (CPK), flood protection, and strategic investments.
- **CPK (Central Communication Port)**: Flagship Polish transport investment — planned
  hub airport with rail connections. Dedicated CPK Act creates special administrative
  regime. Project scope and timing have been subject to political revision — verify
  current status when translating.
- **"Koncesja" vs "pozwolenie"**: In Polish administrative law, a "koncesja" is a
  specific type of licence granted for regulated activities (e.g., energy, mining,
  alcohol). Translate as "licence" or "concession" depending on context. Distinguish
  from "koncesja na roboty budowlane" (construction concession) under concessions law.
