# Spanish — NDAs and Service Agreements Sub-Lexicon

Maps Spanish confidentiality and services vocabulary to correct English equivalents.
Covers NDAs / confidentiality agreements and undertakings, and service agreements of an
administrative or professional nature (SLAs, outsourcing, consultancy, managed services,
MSAs/SOWs).

See also `spanish-corporate-ma-jv.md` for M&A-context NDAs and `spanish-ip-it-technology.md` for
IT-specific service schedules.

## Notes

- **"Medidas cautelares"**: Interim protective measures under Spanish civil procedure.
  Translate as "injunctive relief" or "interim measures" depending on remedy sought.
- **"Ejecución forzosa"**: Civil-law equivalent of "specific performance".
- **"Obligación de medios" vs "obligación de resultado"**: Civil-law distinction.
  Means-obligation = reasonable diligence without guaranteed outcome; result-obligation =
  strict obligation to achieve the outcome. Translate with context as appropriate.
- **"Arrendamiento de servicios" vs "contrato de obra"**: Under Spanish Civil Code
  (Art. 1544 and Art. 1588 et seq.), "arrendamiento de servicios" is a services contract
  (obligation of means); "contrato de obra" is a works contract (obligation of result).
  Both may appear in service-agreement contexts.
- **"Resolución" vs "rescisión" vs "desistimiento"**: "Resolución" = termination for
  breach; "rescisión" = rescission (narrower, typically for specific civil-code grounds);
  "desistimiento" = unilateral withdrawal (convenience).
- **"Penalización"** in SLA context: Prefer "service credits" over "penalties" per
  standard English SLA convention. In non-SLA commercial contexts, use "liquidated
  damages" rather than "penalty" (unenforceable under English law).
- **"Reversibilidad"**: Translate as "exit assistance" or "transition-out services";
  "plan de reversibilidad" → "exit plan" or "disengagement plan".
