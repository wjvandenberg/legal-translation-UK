## Notes

- **"Contrato de empreitada" (Arts. 1207-1230 CC)**: The Portuguese Civil Code's framework
  for construction/works contracts. The empreiteiro undertakes to produce a specific result
  (obra). This is an obligation of result, not of means.
- **"Receção provisória" and "receção definitiva"**: Portuguese construction law
  distinguishes between provisional acceptance (marking completion and start of the warranty
  period) and final acceptance (marking the end of the warranty period). Both are formal
  acts documented by a signed certificate (auto).
- **"Trabalhos a mais"**: Additional works ordered during execution. Under Portuguese
  public procurement law (CCP), trabalhos a mais are subject to strict limits (typically
  50% of the original contract value). Translate as "variations" or "additional works".
- **FIDIC**: FIDIC contracts are used in Portugal, particularly for larger infrastructure
  and international projects. Portuguese law supplements may modify FIDIC terms.
- **"Auto de consignação"**: The formal handover of the site to the contractor, triggering
  the start of the contractual period for completion. Translate as "site handover
  certificate" or "notice to proceed".
- **"Seguro decenal"**: A ten-year structural defects insurance policy — increasingly
  required for residential construction in Portugal. Translate as "decennial insurance"
  or "structural defects insurance".

- **ERSE (Entidade Reguladora dos Serviços Energéticos)**: Portugal's energy regulator.
  Reference as "Energy Services Regulatory Authority (ERSE)" on first mention.
- **DGEG (Direção-Geral de Energia e Geologia)**: The government body responsible for
  energy and mining policy and licensing. Reference as "Directorate-General for Energy
  and Geology (DGEG)" on first mention.
- **REN (Redes Energéticas Nacionais)**: Portugal's transmission system operator for
  electricity and natural gas. Keep the name and translate on first mention.
- **"Equilíbrio económico-financeiro"**: A key concept in Portuguese concession law —
  the concessionaire has a right to maintain the financial equilibrium of the concession.
  If government action disrupts this balance, compensation or tariff adjustment may be
  required. Translate as "financial equilibrium" with explanation.
- **UPAC / UPP**: Portugal's self-consumption and small-scale generation regimes under
  DL 162/2019. Keep the Portuguese abbreviations and translate on first mention.
- **"Avaliação de impacte ambiental"**: Note the Portuguese spelling "impacte" (not
  "impacto" as in Brazilian Portuguese). Translate as "environmental impact assessment".
