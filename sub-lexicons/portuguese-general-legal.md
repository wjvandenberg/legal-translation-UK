## Grammar Notes (Portuguese-Specific)

- **Definite article with legal terms**: Portuguese frequently uses the definite article
  before legal concepts (e.g., "a rescisão", "o contrato"). Drop the article in English
  unless the context requires it.
- **"Resolução" vs "rescisão" vs "denúncia"**: These three terms all relate to ending a
  contract but have distinct legal meanings in Portuguese law. "Resolução" is termination
  for cause (breach); "rescisão" is annulment for defect; "denúncia" is unilateral
  termination by notice. Map carefully based on context.
- **Brazilian vs European Portuguese**: This lexicon follows European Portuguese (Portugal).
  Brazilian Portuguese may use different terms (e.g., "distrato" for termination agreement,
  "inadimplemento" for breach). Flag when the source may be Brazilian.
- **"Pessoa coletiva"**: The Portuguese term for legal entity. Brazilian Portuguese uses
  "pessoa jurídica". Both translate as "legal person" or "legal entity".
- **"Escritura pública"**: A public deed executed before a notary — required for certain
  transactions (e.g., real estate transfers) under Portuguese law. Translate as "public
  deed" or "notarial deed".
