## Notes

- **"Rupture conventionnelle"**: A specifically French mechanism (Art. L. 1237-11 Code du
  travail) allowing employer and employee to agree to terminate the CDI by mutual consent,
  with mandatory approval by the labour inspectorate (DIRECCTE/DREETS). The employee
  receives a severance indemnity and is eligible for unemployment benefits. No direct
  common law equivalent. Translate as "mutual termination" or "negotiated termination"
  with a context note.
- **"Cadre"**: A legal category in French employment law with specific rules on working
  time, notice periods, and social security contributions. Translate as "executive" or
  "managerial staff" — not "framework" (which is the literal meaning).
- **"Forfait jours"**: A working time arrangement available only to cadres and certain
  autonomous employees, where working time is measured in days per year (max. 218) rather
  than hours. No English equivalent. Translate descriptively.
- **"Conseil de prud'hommes"**: The specialised French labour court. Translate as
  "employment tribunal" (UK) or "labour court". Not "industrial tribunal" (outdated UK term).
- **"Licenciement pour faute lourde"**: The most severe form of dismissal in French law —
  for intentional and particularly serious misconduct. The employee loses the right to
  notice period and severance pay. Translate as "dismissal for wilful misconduct" or
  "summary dismissal for gross misconduct".
