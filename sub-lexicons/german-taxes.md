# German — Taxes Sub-Lexicon

German tax-law terminology mapped to the neutral English equivalents in
`references/taxes.md`. German tax law is governed primarily by the
Abgabenordnung (AO — General Tax Code), the Einkommensteuergesetz (EStG — PIT),
the Körperschaftsteuergesetz (KStG — CIT), the Umsatzsteuergesetz (UStG — VAT),
the Gewerbesteuergesetz (GewStG — trade tax), and a body of special acts
(Umwandlungssteuergesetz, Außensteuergesetz, etc.). For corporate / M&A context
use alongside `german-corporate-ma-jv.md`.

## 15. Notes — Translator Guidance

- **KSt vs "corporate tax"**. Körperschaftsteuer is the federal corporate income
  tax (15%). Together with the solidarity surcharge (5.5% of KSt) and trade tax
  (Gewerbesteuer, varying by municipality but typically 14–17%), the effective
  total burden is approximately 30%. Translate KSt as "corporate income tax
  (KSt)" and always note that this is the federal component only — GewSt is
  separate.
- **Gewerbesteuer — no English equivalent.** A municipal tax on business income,
  computed on a separate base from KSt (with specific add-backs, e.g., 25% of
  interest, 20% of certain rent). Rate = federal base rate (3.5%) × municipal
  multiplier (*Hebesatz*, typically 400–500%, sometimes higher). Functionally a
  corporate-level tax, but with no single-word English equivalent. Translate as
  "trade tax (GewSt)" with a translator's note on first use; never as "business
  tax" or "municipal corporate tax" alone.
- **Solidaritätszuschlag.** A 5.5% surcharge on KSt (and, for high-income
  individuals, on ESt). Retained for corporates even after 2021 reform removed
  Soli for most individuals. Translate as "solidarity surcharge (Soli)" and flag
  that it still applies to KSt.
- **Kirchensteuer.** Church tax, levied as 8% (Bavaria, Baden-Württemberg) or 9%
  of ESt for members of recognised religious communities. Collected by the tax
  office and passed to the church. Translate as "church tax" — genuine tax, not a
  voluntary contribution.
- **Maßgeblichkeitsprinzip.** The authoritative principle: the commercial balance
  sheet (HGB) is authoritative for the tax balance sheet, subject to tax-specific
  overrides (e.g., restrictions on provisions, hidden reserves, off-book
  depreciation). Translate as "authoritative-principle rule" and explain that it
  is the bridge between accounting profit and taxable profit.
- **Organschaft.** German fiscal unity / tax group. Requires (i) financial
  integration (>50% voting rights by the controlling entity), (ii) a profit-and-
  loss transfer agreement (Gewinnabführungsvertrag, 5-year minimum) registered in
  the commercial register, and (iii) actual implementation. Translate as "fiscal
  unity" or "tax group" and preserve "Organschaft" in parentheses on first
  mention — no clean English one-word equivalent.
- **§ 8b KStG.** The participation exemption for dividends and capital gains on
  shareholdings in corporations. 95% of the amount is exempt (with a 5%
  disallowance for assumed expenses). For dividends, a 10% minimum participation
  is required (no holding period for KSt; 1-year holding for trade tax). Always
  cite § 8b KStG.
- **Zinsschranke.** Interest limitation rule (Art. 4h EStG / Art. 8a KStG): net
  interest expense deductible only up to 30% of tax EBITDA. Exceptions:
  €3m safe-harbour threshold, standalone exception, equity-escape exception.
  Translate as "interest limitation rule" or "German interest barrier".
- **vGA (verdeckte Gewinnausschüttung).** Constructive / deemed dividend: any
  asset advantage given by a corporation to a shareholder that a prudent manager
  would not have granted to a third party. Triggers income at shareholder level
  and non-deductibility at company level. No capital gains exemption. Translate
  as "constructive (deemed) dividend" or "hidden profit distribution" — and flag
  as a key audit risk.
- **Wegzugsbesteuerung (§ 6 AStG).** German individual exit tax on unrealised
  gains in shares ≥1% (corporations) when an individual ceases to be resident.
  Reformed in 2022 (removal of automatic deferral for EU/EEA moves; instalment
  payments available instead). Translate as "German exit tax" and cite § 6 AStG.
- **Hinzurechnungsbesteuerung (AStG §§ 7–14).** German CFC rules. Passive income
  of a low-taxed (<15%) CFC is attributed to German residents proportionally.
  Reformed in 2021 (ATAD implementation). Translate as "German CFC rules" or
  "add-back taxation" — keep the AStG citation on first mention.
- **Mantelkauf / § 8c KStG.** Loss forfeiture on change of ownership: acquisition
  of >50% of shares within 5 years eliminates loss carry-forwards. Art. 8d KStG
  offers an escape (continuation-linked carry-forward). Restructuring clause in
  Art. 8c(1a) KStG preserves losses in genuine turnaround cases. Flag whenever
  translating M&A documents involving target with loss carry-forwards.
- **Grunderwerbsteuer (GrESt) auf Share Deals.** Since the 2021 reform, a share
  deal transferring ≥90% of shares in a real-estate-holding company within 10
  years triggers RETT. Rates vary by Land (typically 3.5%–6.5%). Translate as
  "share-deal real estate transfer tax" and always specify the applicable rate.
- **Abgeltungsteuer.** Final flat-rate withholding on investment income: 25% +
  5.5% Soli + church tax (if applicable), effectively ~26.4–28%. Applies to
  interest, dividends, and private capital gains. "Discharging" in the sense
  that no income tax return filing is required if properly withheld. Translate
  as "final flat-rate withholding tax" or "Abgeltungsteuer" with explanation.
- **Steuerhinterziehung (§ 370 AO) + Selbstanzeige (§ 371 AO).** Tax evasion is
  a criminal offence. Voluntary disclosure (*Selbstanzeige*) before discovery can
  extinguish criminal liability, subject to strict completeness and timeliness
  requirements (tightened in 2011 and 2015). Translate as "tax evasion" and
  "voluntary disclosure with exculpating effect" — do not conflate with mere
  amendment.
- **ELSTER.** Mandatory electronic tax filing portal. When a source cites an
  ELSTER reference, preserve the tag and translate surrounding narrative.
- **Pillar Two (MinBestG).** Germany implemented Pillar Two via the
  *Mindestbesteuerungsgesetz* (MinBestG), effective for financial years beginning
  on or after 31 December 2023. Translate the statutory name as "German Pillar
  Two Act (MinBestG)" and use OECD English acronyms (IIR, UTPR, QDMTT).
- **BMF-Schreiben.** Administrative guidance issued by the Federal Ministry of
  Finance — binding on the tax administration but not on taxpayers or courts.
  Persuasive. Translate as "BMF circular" and preserve the date of the circular
  on first mention.
- **Currency / number formats**. German uses dot as thousands separator and
  comma as decimal separator (1.234,56 €). Convert to UK English style unless
  the user specifies otherwise. Always preserve the euro sign and exact figure.
