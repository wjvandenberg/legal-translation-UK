# German — Transport and Insurance Sub-Lexicon

Combines German-language transport and insurance vocabulary. Part 1 covers maritime, road, rail, air and multimodal transport: carriage contracts, bills of lading, charterparties, freight forwarding, carrier liability. Part 2 covers insurance contracts, life and non-life lines, reinsurance, brokers, claims handling and regulatory framework. Marine/transport insurance (cargo, hull, P&I, carrier liability) sits at the intersection.

## Notes

- **HGB (Handelsgesetzbuch), Book 5**: German maritime commercial law was substantially
  reformed in 2013. The HGB now contains modernised provisions on carriage of goods by sea,
  general average, and maritime liens. Reference as "German Commercial Code (HGB)" on
  first mention.
- **CMR (Übereinkommen über den Beförderungsvertrag im internationalen Straßengüterverkehr)**:
  The Convention on the Contract for the International Carriage of Goods by Road. Germany
  is a contracting state. Reference as "CMR Convention" on first mention.
- **"Verklarung"**: A formal statement by the ship's master before a court or notary
  after an incident at sea. A specifically German-law procedure (formerly § 522 HGB).
  Translate as "sea protest" or "maritime protest".
- **"Große Havarie"**: General average — the principle that all parties to a sea voyage
  proportionally share losses resulting from a voluntary sacrifice of cargo or expenditure
  to preserve the vessel and cargo. Follow York-Antwerp Rules terminology.
- **"Spediteur" vs "Frachtführer"**: A Spediteur (freight forwarder) arranges transport;
  a Frachtführer (carrier) actually performs it. The legal regimes differ significantly
  (§§ 453-466 HGB for Spediteur, §§ 407-452d HGB for Frachtführer).

## Notes

- **"Selbstbeteiligung" / "Selbstbehalt"**: Translate as "deductible" or "excess" (UK).
- **Versicherungsvertragsgesetz (VVG)**: The German Insurance Contract Act. Reference as
  "German Insurance Contract Act (VVG)" on first mention.
- **BaFin (Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht)**: The German federal
  financial supervisory authority overseeing insurance. Keep abbreviation and translate.
- **"Vermögensschadenhaftpflicht"**: Liability insurance covering pure financial loss
  (as opposed to personal injury or property damage). Translate based on context — often
  "professional indemnity" or "D&O" depending on who is insured.
