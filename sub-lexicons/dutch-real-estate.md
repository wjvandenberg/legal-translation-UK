## Notes for Translators

- **Erfpacht / opstalrecht**: Key Dutch rights in rem. Erfpacht = long-lease of land (the holder may use and enjoy, pays canon; owner retains bloot eigendom / bare ownership). Opstalrecht = right to own buildings/works on another's land (crucial for wind turbines, solar installations, infrastructure). Never translate erfpacht as "leasehold" alone — it is closer to English "long lease" or civilian "emphyteusis". Opstalrecht is often translated as "right of superficies" (civilian) or "building right"; preserve Dutch term on first use with gloss.
- **Art. 7:290 vs 7:230a BW**: Two Dutch commercial lease regimes. 7:290 protects tenants of retail/hospitality space (shops, restaurants, hotels, take-aways) — mandatory 5+5 year minimum, rent review procedure, tenant substitution right. 7:230a covers other commercial space (offices, warehouses) — less protected, but has "ontruimingsbescherming" (vacation protection) after notice. Absolutely distinct — never conflate. Flag the regime in translation.
- **ROZ-model**: Raad voor Onroerende Zaken standard lease models (2015 for retail, 2015 for office). Heavily used market standard. When translating a ROZ-based lease, flag reference to the model on first use ("ROZ model lease 2015 (office)"/("retail)") so reader understands the framework.
- **Koopovereenkomst / voorlopige koopovereenkomst**: "Voorlopige" is misleading — it is a fully binding purchase agreement in Dutch practice (subject to standard conditions). Translate as "purchase agreement" and never as "letter of intent". Consumer buyers of residential property have 3-day statutory reflection period (BW 7:2).
- **Notariële akte**: Dutch property transfers, mortgages, and apartment rights require notarial deed executed before civil-law notary (BW 3:89) and registration with Kadaster. This is mandatory, not optional. Translate "notaris" as "civil-law notary" or "notary" (not "notary public" — different role).
- **Kadaster**: Dutch land registry. Combines cadastral mapping with ownership/mortgage register. Public. Translate as "Kadaster" (retained Dutch) or "Land Registry". Always capitalise when referring to the institution.
- **Kettingbeding**: Contractual obligation imposed on successors via mandatory clause in onward transfer. Combined with penalty. Essentially a contractual substitute for qualitative obligation where statutory regime cannot reach. Translate as "chain clause" or "chain obligation"; always explain on first use.
- **Kwalitatieve verplichting (BW 6:252)**: Obligation that runs with the land automatically (registered). Different from kettingbeding. Translate as "qualitative obligation" or "obligation running with the land".
- **Omgevingswet (in force 1 Jan 2024)**: Major consolidation of 26 land-use/environment statutes. Introduced omgevingsvergunning (integrated environmental permit) and omgevingsplan (replaces bestemmingsplan). Transition period ongoing. Translate as "Environment and Planning Act" with reference to effective date. Old terms (bestemmingsplan, Wabo) may still appear in older documents.
- **Wkb / Wet kwaliteitsborging voor het bouwen (2024)**: Major construction liability reform. Contractors' post-delivery liability extended; independent quality assurer (kwaliteitsborger) required for certain categories. Translate as "Quality Assurance for Construction Act"; flag effective date.
- **Aanneming van werk (BW 7:750)**: Dutch construction contract regime. Different from opdracht (services). Result-based, with specific rules on waarschuwingsplicht (duty to warn about errors in principal's design), oplevering (delivery) and post-delivery liability. See services sub-lexicon for opdracht/aanneming distinction.
- **UAV 2012 / UAV-GC 2005**: UAV 2012 = traditional specification-based contracts (owner provides design). UAV-GC 2005 = integrated contracts (contractor designs and builds). Standard Dutch construction general conditions. Many construction contracts incorporate by reference. Flag in translation.
- **Waarschuwingsplicht (BW 7:754)**: Contractor must warn principal about errors in design, materials specified, or conditions. Critical — breach leads to shared liability despite specification. Translate as "duty to warn" and flag in translator's note if it affects risk allocation.
- **Overdrachtsbelasting**: Dutch real estate transfer tax — 2% for residential owner-occupier, 10.4% general rate (2025), with the rate for residential investment property planned to drop to 8% from 1 January 2026. Rates change frequently. Startersvrijstelling for first-time buyers 18-35 under price cap. Flag applicable rates based on document date.
- **BTW / VAT on property**: New-build and undeveloped land subject to VAT rather than overdrachtsbelasting (or both in some cases). Rule: building/land in first 2 years from first use is VAT-taxable; older is transfer-tax-only. Complex — flag for VAT specialist.
- **Huurbescherming for residential**: Very strong statutory protection for residential tenants — limited termination grounds, Rent Tribunal (Huurcommissie) jurisdiction for rent disputes, mandatory points-based rent system for regulated sector. Translate "huurbescherming" as "tenant protection" or "lease protection"; do not flatten.
- **Wet betaalbare huur (1 July 2024)**: Extended rent regulation to mid-market housing. Major policy shift. Investors' mid-market rentals now subject to rent caps. Flag in any lease/investment translation.
- **Mandeligheid (BW 5:60-69)**: Dutch-specific institution of common ownership of boundary features (party walls, hedges, shared paths). No exact English equivalent; translate as "common ownership" or "mandelig ownership" with gloss.
- **Ondernemingsrechtelijke / fiscale ABC-structuren**: ABC transactions (A-B-C back-to-back transfers) common in Dutch property practice, especially project development. Note CJEU and HR case law limits. Translate as "ABC transfer".
- **Belgian real estate**: Belgian property law similar framework (Napoleonic tradition) but different statute. Belgian civil-law notary (notaris/notaire) also required. Registration at Algemene Administratie van de Patrimoniumdocumentatie (Hypotheekbewaring). Belgian "erfpacht" is regulated differently (law of 25 April 2014). Flag Belgian documents.
- **WOZ-waarde**: Value determined annually by municipality under the Wet waardering onroerende zaken, used for municipal property tax, income tax on own-home, water board tax. Different from market value. Translate as "WOZ value" (retained acronym) with gloss.
- **Woningcorporaties**: Dutch housing associations — not purely private or public. Regulated under Woningwet 2015, must be "toegelaten" by Minister. Operate social housing (DAEB) under strict rules. Translate as "housing associations" or "authorised institutions"; flag regulatory context.
