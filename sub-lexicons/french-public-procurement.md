## Notes

- **"Marché public"**: Covers both works contracts and service contracts under French
  procurement law. Translate based on context: "public works contract" or "public
  service contract" or generically "public contract".
- **"Pouvoir adjudicateur" vs "entité adjudicatrice"**: The first applies to public
  authorities under the Public Contracts Directive; the second to utilities under the
  Utilities Directive. Use the correct English term based on the directive.
- **"Résiliation pour motif d'intérêt général"**: A specifically French prerogative
  allowing the public authority to terminate a contract in the public interest. Similar
  to "termination for convenience" but with different compensation rules. Translate as
  "termination for public interest" or "termination for convenience" depending on context.
- **Code de la commande publique**: The French Public Procurement Code (2019). Reference
  as "French Public Procurement Code" on first mention.
