# Finnish — Corporate, M&A, JV and Powers of Attorney Sub-Lexicon

Unified Finnish reference covering corporate resolutions and governance under the
Companies Act (osakeyhtiölaki, 624/2006), mergers and acquisitions, joint ventures,
shareholders' agreements and related collateral, and powers of attorney / proxies. Use
alongside `references/corporate-ma-jv.md`. For tax-related terminology (tax
indemnities, tax warranties, tax authorities), see `finnish-taxes.md` and
`references/taxes.md`.

## 19. Notes

- **Companies Act (osakeyhtiölaki, 624/2006)**: Finland's principal corporate law.
  Governs all Finnish limited liability companies — incorporation, governance, capital
  structure, mergers, demergers, share issues, and dissolution. Reference as "Companies
  Act" on first mention.
- **Sulautuminen (merger)**: Distinguish absorption merger (absorptiosulautuminen — one
  entity survives) from consolidation merger (kombinaatiosulautuminen — new entity
  formed). Both follow the Companies Act procedure with creditor-protection mechanisms.
  Translate with the type specified.
- **Jakautuminen (demerger)**: Full demerger (kokonaisjakautuminen — the demerging
  company ceases to exist) versus partial demerger (osittaisjakautuminen — the demerging
  company survives). Partial demerger is the more common restructuring tool. Translate
  with type specified.
- **FCCA merger control**: Transactions meeting the threshold (combined turnover
  > EUR 350M and at least two parties' turnover > EUR 20M each in Finland) require
  notification. The FCCA has 23 working days for Phase I review. Flag threshold and
  timing on first reference. Transactions meeting the EU Merger Regulation thresholds
  are reviewed by the European Commission rather than the FCCA.
- **Foreign investment screening**: Finland's Act on the Screening of Foreign Corporate
  Acquisitions (172/2012, amended 2020) requires Ministry of Economic Affairs (TEM)
  confirmation for foreign acquisitions in defence and certain critical sectors.
- **Lunastuslauseke (redemption clause)**: A clause in the articles giving existing
  shareholders the right to redeem shares from a new acquirer. Common in JV companies.
  Translate as "redemption clause" and explain the mechanism.
- **Suostumuslauseke (consent clause)**: A clause requiring board or shareholder consent
  for share transfers — an alternative to the redemption clause.
- **Toimitusjohtaja (managing director)**: Under the Companies Act, the managing
  director is appointed by the board and manages day-to-day operations. Specific
  statutory duties and liability provisions apply. Not all Oy companies are required to
  have one. Translate as "managing director" or "CEO".
- **Board structure**: Finnish law uses a single-tier board (hallitus) by default. The
  managing director is appointed by the board. A supervisory board (hallintoneuvosto)
  is optional and uncommon outside certain financial institutions. Flag the governance
  structure where relevant.
- **Vastuuvapaus (discharge from liability)**: At each AGM, shareholders vote on
  discharging the board and managing director from liability for the preceding financial
  year. A Nordic practice with no direct common-law equivalent. Translate as "discharge
  from liability" and explain on first use.
- **Nimenkirjoitusoikeus**: The power to sign on behalf of the company. Under the
  Companies Act the board represents the company; specific persons can be granted signing
  authority, registered with the Trade Register (PRH). Translate as "power of signature"
  or "authority to sign".
- **Määräenemmistöpäätös (2/3)**: The Companies Act requires a 2/3 supermajority for
  certain fundamental decisions: amendments to articles, directed share issues, mergers,
  demergers, and dissolution. Both 2/3 of votes cast AND 2/3 of shares represented at
  the meeting are required. Flag both thresholds.
- **Special audit (erityinen tarkastus)**: Minority shareholders holding at least 10%
  may request a special audit. A meaningful minority-protection tool — flag in
  governance translations.
- **Pääomalaina (subordinated capital loan)**: Companies Act Ch. 12 instrument. Treated
  as equity for distributability and insolvency-priority purposes despite being a loan.
  Translate as "subordinated capital loan" with brief explanation.
- **Edunvalvontavaltuutus (continuing power of attorney)**: Governed by the Act on
  Continuing Power of Attorney (648/2007). A person may, while still capable, designate
  an attorney-in-fact to manage their affairs if they later lose capacity. The Digital
  and Population Data Services Agency (DVV) confirms the POA when triggered. Translate
  as "continuing power of attorney" — distinct from a regular valtakirja.
- **Valtakirja vs valtuutus**: Valtakirja is the document; valtuutus is the underlying
  authorisation/mandate. Both can be translated as "power of attorney" or
  "authorisation" depending on context.
- **Kaupanvahvistaja**: A public attestor required for the validity of real estate
  conveyances under the Code of Real Estate (maakaari). Distinct from a julkinen notaari
  (general notary public). Flag where real estate is involved.
