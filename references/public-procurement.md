# Public Procurement Lexicon

Standard English terminology for public procurement: tenders, concessions, PPP/PFI, state aid, EU procurement directives, public contracts, and related instruments.

## Core Procurement Terms

| Correct English term | Usage / meaning | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| public contract / public procurement | General term for all contracts where the contracting authority is the buyer. | procurement (too vague) |
| works contract | Contract for the supply of labour and materials to execute construction works. | construction contract (less precise) |
| service contract | Contract for the provision of services. | services agreement (less precise) |
| supply contract | Contract for the provision of goods. | goods contract (less precise) |
| concession | Agreement transferring operational risk to the concessionaire; distinct from a purchase contract. | award agreement (too vague) |
| works concession | Concession for construction and/or operation of works infrastructure. | — |
| service concession | Concession for the supply and operation of services. | — |
| public-private partnership (PPP) | Structured arrangement between public authority and private entity to deliver services. UK: PFI (Private Finance Initiative). | partnership (less precise) |
| project finance / PFI | Financing structure for large projects, especially PPP. | — |
| contracting authority | Public entity responsible for commissioning public contracts. | purchasing authority (less precise) |
| contracting entity | Utilities or other entities subject to the Utilities Directive. | contracting body (less precise) |
| economic operator | Any natural or legal person offering goods or services in a tender. | market participant (less precise) |
| tenderer / bidder | Party submitting an offer in response to a tender. | offeror (less precise in procurement context) |
| successful tenderer / contractor | Party awarded the contract. | awardee (ambiguous outside procurement) |
| subcontractor | Party contracted by the main contractor to supply part of the work/services. | subcontracted party (less precise) |
| temporary grouping / joint venture / consortium | Association of economic operators to participate in a tender. | partnership (less precise) |
| reliance on third-party resources | Reliance by an operator on another entity's technical, financial, or professional capacity (EU procurement concept). | outsourcing capacity (less precise) |
| permanent consortium | Long-term grouping of operators; distinct from a temporary grouping. | persistent partnership (ambiguous) |
| social value / social considerations | Requirements for social outcomes (employment, training, environmental sustainability) as part of award criteria or contract conditions | Social impact (less precise) |
| reserved contract | Contract reserved for sheltered workshops, social enterprises, or operators employing disadvantaged persons (EU Directive 2014/24/EU, Art. 20) | Protected contract (less standard) |

## Notes

- **EU vs national procurement**: The three directives (2014/24, 2014/25, 2014/23/EU) set the floor for procurement rules across the EU. National codes often provide stricter requirements or additional procedures. Always identify which framework governs the transaction.

- **Contract vs concession**: A public contract transfers goods/services to the authority; a concession transfers operational and/or financial risk to the concessionaire. This distinction is fundamental across all EU procurement frameworks.

- **MEAT and award criteria**: The shift from "lowest price" to "most economically advantageous tender" reflects modern procurement favouring quality, sustainability, and innovation. Always clarify which criterion applies.

- **Standstill period (Alcatel period)**: The mandatory 10-day waiting period after award notice is a critical procedural safeguard. Failure to observe it can invalidate the award.

- **Framework agreements and dynamic purchasing systems**: These are modern EU procurement tools enabling flexibility and value for money, especially for repetitive purchases of standard goods and services.

- **ESPD**: Replaces multiple national forms; substantially standardizes eligibility evidence across the EU, though national interpretation may vary.

- **State aid vs public procurement**: Public procurement rules govern *how* the authority buys; state aid rules govern *whether* the support is market-compliant. Both may apply.
