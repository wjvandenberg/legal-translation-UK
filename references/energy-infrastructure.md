# EPC / Energy / Infrastructure Lexicon

English terminology for (a) engineering, procurement and construction contracts (EPC),
turnkey contracts, construction agreements, balance of plant (BOP) contracts, operations
and maintenance agreements (O&M); and (b) energy and infrastructure agreements — power
purchase agreements (PPAs), grid connection, offtake agreements, concession agreements,
regulatory and tariff matters, renewables, and related instruments.

This consolidated lexicon unifies the construction and commissioning terminology with
the operational, regulatory and commercial energy / infrastructure terminology. Many
terms cross the interface between the two contract families (performance guarantees,
availability guarantee, taking over vs. commercial operation date, change in law,
liquidated damages, concession step-in) and should be treated as one terminology system.

| Correct English term | Usage / meaning | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| power purchase agreement (PPA) | Fundamental energy sales contract; specify type (corporate, merchant, physical, virtual/synthetic) | energy purchase contract; energy supply contract (less precise) |

### Design Stage Hierarchy (Critical)

| Stage | Correct English | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 — conceptual | preliminary design | "preliminary project" |
| Stage 2 — intermediate | detailed design | "definitive project" (calque), "preliminary design" (WRONG STAGE) |
| Stage 3 — construction-ready | construction design | "executive project" (calque), "detailed design" (reserve for Stage 2) |

**WARNING**: Confusing Stage 2 (detailed design) with Stage 1 (preliminary design) is a
material error. The hierarchy is: Preliminary Design → Detailed Design → Construction Design.

## Notes

- **FIDIC alignment**: Many international EPC contracts follow FIDIC standard forms. Use
  FIDIC terminology where the source follows that structure: "Engineer" (not "Director of
  Works"), "Taking-Over Certificate" (not "Provisional Acceptance Certificate"), "Defects
  Notification Period" (FIDIC 2017 editions).

- **"Employer" vs "Owner"**: FIDIC uses "Employer"; many US contracts use "Owner". Default
  to "Employer" for international/European contracts.

- **Liquidated damages**: Always say "liquidated damages" (not "penalties") to preserve
  enforceability under English law.

- **BOT/BOOT/BOO**: For concession-based construction (Build-Operate-Transfer etc.),
  additional concession-specific terminology applies.

- **"Construction design" vs "as-built drawings"**: These are completely different things.
  Construction design documents are produced BEFORE construction; as-built drawings are
  produced AFTER construction showing what was actually built. Confusing them is a material error.

- **O&M and EPC interface**: The handover from EPC to O&M is a critical project milestone.
  Ensure terminology is consistent between the EPC contract and the O&M agreement,
  particularly for performance guarantees, defects liability, and warranty provisions.

- **Capacity vs. Energy**: Never confuse the two. Capacity = maximum output (measured in MW, GW). Energy = actual output over time (measured in MWh, GWh). A 100 MW wind farm produces ~350 GWh/year (at 40% capacity factor), not "100 MWh".

- **PPA Types**: Modern PPAs vary widely. Always specify: (1) Corporate vs. merchant offtake, (2) Physical vs. virtual/financial, (3) Fixed vs. variable pricing, (4) Tenor (typically 10–25 years).

- **Grid Curtailment**: Distinguish between (a) planned/scheduled curtailment (maintenance), (b) forced curtailment (grid constraints with compensation), and (c) spilling (water dams; no compensation).

- **Renewable Energy Certificates**: GO (Guarantee of Origin) is EU-specific. REC (Renewable Energy Certificate) is international standard, primarily used in US, Australia, India. In EU contracts, typically refer to GOs unless dealing with cross-border/international buyers.

- **Support Mechanisms**: Modern mechanisms include FIT (declining), feed-in premium (with market price floor), CfD (UK model), tender-based (auction), net metering (distributed generation), and simple sale (commodity rate). Identify which applies.

- **National Regulatory Bodies**: When translating contracts, keep national regulatory acronyms (e.g., GSE, GME, Terna, ARERA in Italy; Ofgem, National Grid ESO in UK; FERC, NERC in US) and explain on first use in English context. Sub-lexicon provides source-language mappings.
